Rainy Easter

Zureikats gather in Umm Al-Amad celebrating Easter 2003

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Zureikats in Jordan celebrated Easter on Sunday, April 27, 2003.  On this occasion, the family gathered at Marwan's farm in Umm Al-Amad.  Despite the rainy weather, the Zureikat children were still able to egg-hunt, just like every year.  Guests in this year's Easter lunch included Mr. Wael Zureikat, his wife and son, and Mrs. Nabila Lababidi.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish you all a very happy Easter and all the best.

 

Amal and Ziad Celebrate Easter in Aqaba

 

Amal and Ziad left to Aqaba on Saturday, April 26, to spend their Easter vacation there.  They are expected to return to Amman on Tuesday, April 29, 2003.

 

'Easter Dinner' at Abu Harran's

 

Zureikats gathered at Abu Harran's on Saturday, April 26, 2003, for an Easter dinner.  The dinner, which included kafta bi taheeniyeh and freekeh and chicken, was prepared by Abla Z. and Hala.

Jordan Mourns Talal Al-Hassan

Talal Sat'an Al-Hassan, a very good friend of the Zureikat family, passed away on Wednesday, April 23, at the age of 64.  Al-Hassan served as Minister of Culture and Youth, Minister of State for Foreign Affairs, and as an ambassador for Jordan in a number of countries.  Several Zureikats attended Al-Hassan's funeral in Irbid on Thursday, April 24, 2003.  Our deepest and most sincere condolences go out to Al-Hassan's family and friends.  May God rest his soul.

Talal Returns to England

Talal and Marwan, the night before Talal's departure.

After spending two weeks in Amman, Talal returned to England on Saturday, April 26, 2003.  Talal is currently in his second year at the St. George's Hospital Medical School.

Amal Undergoes Minor Surgery

"Colonoscopy was performed, plus hemorrhoid ligature"

Amal was admitted to the Amman Surgical Hospital last week for a minor surgery due to bleeding from the colon.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Amal said, "Colonoscopy was performed, plus hemorrhoid
ligature.  If bleeding does not stop in the next few days, I might need a major surgery."

Kareem Gets his Driver's License

After passing his theoretical and practical tests, Kareem got his driver's license last week.  This comes almost one month after Kareem turned eighteen on March 25, 2003.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Kareem on getting his driver's license, wishing him all the best.

Zureikats Celebrate 2 Birthdays

 

Zureikats celebrated Abla Z.'s birthday on Wednesday, April 23, and Mona's birthday on Friday, April 25, 2003.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish both Abla and Mona very happy birthdays and all the best.

 

Mona: "A Birthday to Remember"

In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Mona said, "It was my birthday on Friday.  The Hennings celebrated the occasion with flowers and coffee and birthday cake.  In the evening, Helene arranged for a nice evening at the Royal Dramatic Theater after which the family Henning enjoyed a superb dinner in one of Stockholm's nice restaurants.  It was a birthday to remember!"  On the other hand, in her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Alia sent her family's wishes to Mona on her birthday.  "Harran and family would like to wish Mona a very happy birthday," she said, "hoping that she had a great day."

4 Years and Counting...

The Zureikat Weekly celebrated its fourth anniversary on Wednesday, April 23, 2003.  Many would agree that the ZW's fourth year wasn't 'just another year'.  Most of this year's issues were released from the US.  Another major change that the newsletter underwent, as a result, was the appointment of Correspondents and Photographers.  The Weekly, since then, has become the result of a remarkable group effort.  On this occasion, the Editor in Chief of The Zureikat Weekly would like to thank the Correspondents, Photographers, and readers of the ZW for what they have been doing to keep our newsletter lively and interesting.
