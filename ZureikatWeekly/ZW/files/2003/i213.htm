June 22, 2003

Almost two weeks after their wedding

Amer and Leanne Move to the US

 

Amer and Leanne are now living in their new apartment in Chicago, Illinois, where Amer will be doing his residency at the University of Chicago Hospital.  Amer left Amman on Tuesday, June 17, while Leanne left on  Thursday, June 19. What should be mentioned is that Amer and Leanne had earlier returned to Amman from their honeymoon in Paris on Monday, June 16, 2003.

Abla in DC to Oversee Wedding Preparations

In a surprising turn of events, Abla N. left to Washington DC on Tuesday, June 17, to oversee and help out in the preparations for Dana and Ammar's August 2 wedding.  Abla is expected to return home on Monday, June 30, 2003.

Sharaihas Return after Attending Graduation

   

After attending Reem's graduation from St. George's, Amal, Ziad, and Samer returned to Amman last week.  Amal and Samer returned on Thursday, June 19, 2003, while Ziad returned a few days earlier.

 

Ayman Returns to Boston

Ayman, who also attended Reem's graduation in London, returned to Boston on Monday, June 16, 2003, to continue his summer job at Tufts.  Ayman is expected to return to Amman next month.

Congratulations from Dana and Ammar

We would like to congratulate Amer and Leanne on their wedding.  We wish them the best of luck and all the happiness, health, and wealth in the world.  The pictures were amazing.  Leanne looked like a princess, and Amer like a prince.  We regret not being able to make it but look forward to a family reunion in DC very soon.

We would also like to congratulate Dr. Reem Sharaiha, whom we have known to always excel in whatever she does.  Reem, we miss you and are glad you will make it to our wedding.  CONGRATULATIONS!

Marwan Recovers from Minor Injury

Marwan is almost fully recovered from a minor injury he obtained in his right hand last Monday (June 16) when he fell down the stairs at the construction site of the new JCE building.  After the fall, Marwan was immediately taken to the Jordan Hospital, where he was x-rayed.  No fractures were found.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Marwan a very speedy recovery and all the best.

Zureikats Go to the Polls

Abdullah Ghanem Zureikat wins for the second time in a row

6 women, none from Amman, were chosen as part of the new 'quota' system

Jordanians went to the polls on Tuesday, June 17, 2003, to elect their representatives in the 14th Lower House of Parliament.  The new parliament includes 110 seats, including 6 extra seats which were reserved for women as part of the new 'quota' system.  What should be mentioned is that Abdullah Ghanem Zureikat, who ran in the Karak governorate in this year's elections, won for the second time in a row.  Abdullah Salem Zureikat, however, who ran for the first time this year, lost the election.
