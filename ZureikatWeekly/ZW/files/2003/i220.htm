220th Issue
August 10, 2003

Hala Moves to Maryland for 1-Year Program at UMD

Naief and the children will be leaving on the 23rd

A few weeks after being awarded the Hubert Humphrey fellowship to attend the class of 2003-2004 at the University of Maryland at College Park, Hala left to the US on Saturday, August 9, to start the orientation program.  Naief, Dalia, Deena, and Salem are expected to leave Amman on August 23, 2003.  Naief, who was having trouble moving his Distinguished Scholarship Award which he received from the Arab Fund for Social and Economic Development (based in Kuwait), succeeded in moving the scholarship to Washington, DC (near Maryland).  Naief will be spending the next academic year teaching and doing research at the George Washington University in DC.  What should be mentioned is that Hala and the family had earlier spent the 1998-1999 academic year in Birmingham, Alabama.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Hala and the family all the best in their year in the US.

Bashar Starts his New Job at Standard Chartered

Bashar started his new job as Relationship Manager at Standard Chartered Bank on Wednesday, August 6, 2003, six days after he had left his job at Citibank.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Bashar on his new job and wish him all the best.

Reem Returns to London

Reem returned to London on Tuesday, July 29.  Dr. Sharaiha registered at the hospital she will be working at on Sunday, August 3, 2003, and started work the next morning.

After attending Dana and Ammar's wedding in Washington

Zureikats Return from the US

   

 

Marwan, Abla, Hazem, Reema, and Alia returned to Amman after attending Dana and Ammar's wedding in Washington, DC.  Marwan, Abla, and Hazem returned home on Wednesday, August 6.  They had left Washington on Monday, August 4, and spent one night in New York.  Reema and Alia arrived in Amman on Sunday, August 10, after spending a few days with Amer and Leanne in Chicago.  Tarek, who left Washington on August 4 for London to visit some friends, is expected to be back in Amman on Monday, August 11, 2003.  Mona and Dag, as mentioned in the previous issue, returned to Sweden shortly after the August 3 brunch at Sequoia.

 

The Editor in Chief would like to take this opportunity to thank Abla N. (the ZW Photographer), Leanne, and Miss Bana Bashour for their efforts in taking many of the pictures in the 219th issue.

Amal and Ziad in South Africa

 

Amal and Ziad left to South Africa on Monday, August 4, 2003, to attend the African-Middle Eastern Conference on Gastroenterology, which is held under the patronage of former South African President Nelson Mandela.

One day after returning from his honeymoon

Ammar Leaves to Vietnam

Dana and Ammar at the August 3 brunch

Dana and Ammar left Washington on Monday, August 4, two days after their wedding, to spend their honeymoon at a nearby resort.  They returned to DC on Wednesday, August 6.  One day later, on Thursday, August 7, 2003, Ammar left to Vietnam for a two-week visit which is part of the Georgetown University MBA program he is currently enrolled in.

Zureikats Celebrate 2 Birthdays

 

Zureikats celebrated Samer's birthday on Wednesday, August 6, and Hassan's birthday on Saturday, August 9, 2003.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish both Hassan and Samer very happy birthdays and all the best.

  • Samer started his new summer job at the Queen Alia International Airport in Amman.  Samer is responsible for doing certain pre-flight checks in the cockpits of departing airplanes.

  • Ayman and Talal left to Wadi Rum on Thursday, July 31, and returned to Amman on Saturday, August 2, 2003.
