A Memorable End to 2003

Zureikats celebrate Christmas

 

 

By Reem Sharaiha - Amman - Edited by the Editor in Chief:

Christmas Eve, as always, did not disappoint any of us. It was a night of many firsts and, to some, the first of many. We all gathered at the Harran household. Abla N., as usual, started the evening with her rendition of Christmas carols. Soon, most of the grandchildren gathered round and started singing. The favorite song by far was 'The 12 Days of Christmas', where Bana divided the Zureikats into 10 different sectors, where they each had to sing a verse. Tarek got to sing “five gold rings”, much to his delight, but he was definitely overshadowed by the amazing talent of Reem and Abla Z.’s “partridge in a pear tree”.

Dinner was served, and, as usual, Reema did her best to amaze us with the array of food. The menu this year consisted of two salads, one with bacon bits (a new addition), cold cuts, turkey and stuffing, lamb, salmon, shrimps, the well known chicken pie,  vegetables and noodles. Along with dinner came a series of speeches and toasts which, by now, the Zureikats are well accustomed to. The first bit of news came from Harran about Amer and Leanne. Harran spoke of Amer's recent successes, not to mention that Leanne, with her new job, will be the bread winner in the family, without doing six years of medicine!! He also announced, much to everyone’s delight, that both will be coming at the end of the month. Followed that speech were a series of toasts directed at several  members of the family, including those who are currently abroad. This ended with Amal raising a toast to Dian and Umm Phillip, which ended up with lots of hugs.

The evening continued with some music and dancing, where Sabine displayed some of her amazing talent, which left many speechless.

This year's celebrations reminded us all of what Christmas, and the Christmas spirit, is all about.

In a surprising turn of events

Amer and Leanne Arrive in Amman on Monday

This will be Amer and Leanne's first visit to Jordan after moving to Chicago last June

In a Christmas Eve announcement, Harran said that Amer and Leanne will be arriving in Amman on Monday, December 29.  On this occasion, a dinner will be held at Harran and Reema's on Tuesday, December 30, 2003.  Harran's announcement came after many speculations on the date of Amer and Leanne's visit.  The initial date had been set to February, but Leanne ruled out a visit during that month in an e-mail she sent to the Editor in Chief two weeks ago.

Abu Harran Recovering from Minor Injury

Abu Harran is currently recovering from a minor injury he sustained after falling down in his bedroom on Saturday, December 20, 2003.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Abu Harran a speedy recovery and all the best.

Dana and Ammar Celebrate Christmas in Cincinnati

Dana and Ammar left to Cincinnati, Ohio, on Wednesday, December 24, where they celebrated Christmas with the Daoud family.  They will be returning to DC on Sunday, December 28, 2003.

Photo courtesy of the Jordan News Agency (Petra)

Amal Voted as Chairperson for 'Operation Smile' in Jordan

Amal was voted as the chairperson for Operation Smile in Jordan a few months ago.  Operation Smile volunteers, according to the organization's website, "repair childhood facial deformities while building public and private partnerships that advocate for sustainable healthcare systems for children and families".  Operation Smile held a gala dinner under the patronage of Her Majesty Queen Rania on Sunday, December 21, 2003.  Amal, along with a number of Zureikats, attended the dinner.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this belated opportunity to congratulate Amal on her post, wishing her all the best.
