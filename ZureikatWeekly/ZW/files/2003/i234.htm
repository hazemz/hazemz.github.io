Zureikats Respond to Marwan's Letter

Zureikats responded to Marwan's recent letter to the Editor in Chief in which he said that now is the right time to consider discontinuing The Zureikat Weekly.  Following are the responses received so far (sorted alphabetically by first name):

Alia

Hi Hazem,

...I feel it would be a great shame to discontinue ["our weekly"].  As Dag has said, it is a great source of comfort for those abroad.  It is also our legacy and a constant reminder of the bonds that unite us.  Having said that, sending their weekly news to you seems to be somewhat difficult to some Zureikats, particularly as we all are busy with work, studies, etc.  Therefore, I recommend that the weekly becomes a bi-weekly.  Perhaps you could send out a questionnaire, recommending future changes, for everyone to fill out.

Dear Zureikats,

I urge you all not to kill such a beautiful and resourceful innovation that, with a little more cooperation from your part coupled with Hazem's creativity, could continue to be our source of pride and inspiration for many more years to come.  Many thanks to you all...

Alia

Amal

Dear Hazem,

I have read Marwan's letter.  I don't agree with him at all.  This ZW was an inspiration to all members, especially to the ones who live outside Amman.  It is a great commitment and effort which has taken lots of your time to make someone happy.  Is not that enough reason to continue?

We love you.

Amal

Dag

Hi Hazem,

I read Uncle Marwan's letter, and I have to say it would be sad if you stop publishing The Zureikat Weekly.  It is for us Hennings/Zureikats living abroad a very good way to stay close to the family in Jordan.  Even the slightest news like Marwan's tooth operation is interesting.  Keep up the good work you do.  Maybe instead of a weekly newsletter, do a monthly or every second week instead, then you have more time to collect more news material and pictures.  Just a suggestion.  I like what you are doing, and I support you...

Take care my brother.

Regards,
Dag

Dana

 "Our Family Diary"
a half decade of recorded family history and still growing...

My father's words were very harsh...too harsh in fact.  My heart was racing as I read his letter.  Although, I tend to agree with him most of the time, I'm afraid I don't regarding ceasing the publication of our dear "ZW".  My father may have a different perspective seeing it from Jordan, but you have to take the opinion of the Zureikats living abroad into consideration.  We do constitute almost fifty percent today, i.e. huge lobby you're dealing with here!  We look forward to the weekly issue and find it extremely enjoyable to read no matter how little or how much the content of each issue is.  We have to realize that the ZW cannot be rich in its every issue since we don't always have breaking news on a weekly basis, but the consistency itself is a significant element here.  I bet you we all grin and smile when we find it in our inbox and open it with great anticipation.  Isn't that an act of greatness in itself?

To give an example, I personally cried so much for not being able to attend Amer's wedding but felt like I was among the family as soon as Hazem sent the ZW out.

Ammar and I have discussed what my father had written in the ZW's previous issue and believe that maybe now is a good time to build on what has already been achieved.  We should contribute differently and add value to the publication.  For example, we can have a column on political views by Tarek Zureikat, or a section relating to news from Sweden by Mona Henning, etc.

What do you think?

Dana

Hala

Dearest Hazem,

...I have been thinking all this week about ["Marwan's letter to you"], and I felt really sorry if you would listen to him and stop the magazine.  The only thing I agree with him is you might think of making it bi-weekly or monthly.  This is up to you.  Also, it is up to you to stop it if you feel tired, and maybe in this case somebody else (which I don't like, because I don't think any of us has the qualities of consistency like you) can replace you.

Marwan mentioned the culture and values of our family which my mother fought for all her life.  I think we should work on to keep these values for the grand grand children.  Even if the family grows, and the priorities for each one of us are different, but still we have to keep this unity through the ZWeekly.  We don't meet all of us weekly or monthly, as do some other families I know in Amman.  The ZWeekly managed to keep all of us informed about each other's activities, and that, by itself, is great.

It was great to see all these beautiful pictures of Dana and Ammar and Amer and Leanne when they got married.  We will be seeing more of these pictures soon for other members.  What you are doing Hazem is a great archive work.  Maybe I should mention here that a great French writer, Robert Solé, wrote a trilogy, three very nice stories about Christian families who left Syria to live in Egypt at the end of the 19th. century, and this was based on the memories of a Syrian journalist at that time, describing the day to day life of his family.  The work you are doing is a great one, and we should keep it.

Again, it is up to you Hazem, but this is really what I think.

Love,
Auntie Hala

Leanne

Hi everyone,

Hope all is well.  Well I must say that Ammo Marwan's e-mail came as a shock when I opened the Weekly last night, as I do every Sunday, looking forward to reading the week's news.  As I read Ammo's letter, panic was being swept across the US as I broke the news to a very distressed Auntie Hala, who had not yet read the issue, followed by an urgent call from Dana!  I think I speak on behalf of all Zureikats abroad when I say that the Weekly is a very important part of our lives.  The pictures take us back home, and the news puts us at ease, as we are reassured that everyone is doing well.  It is unfortunate that we are not all always able to contribute, but life sometimes gets so crazy that one doesn't have time to do the simplest things, but we all have time to read the Weekly, and when we do - as I said in one of my past emails - we mentally travel back to Amman and feel like we are among the family again.

I don't know any other family in Jordan that has such a special way of keeping in touch with each other.  So Ammo Marwan, please please please change your mind.  The Weekly is so important to us!

Love to you all,
Leanne

Mona

I will be sad if The Zureikat Weekly stopped.  It is my only window to hear the news of the members of the family who I do not hear from very often.  But it is like all initiatives in the Arab World [that] die due to the lack of the team spirit.  Success is a strange word for them.  Again, I am really sad to see The Zureikat Weekly vanish away.

Love,
Mona

Reema

(In a phone call with the Editor in Chief):
The Zureikat Weekly is a pioneer foundation.  It's a model to other friends and families.  Stopping this pioneer foundation will sadden and shock me because, whether Hazem likes it or not, we've been depending on it a lot.  Sometimes, one tends to take things for granted, things that he needs most and loves most, but aren't we all human?  We make mistakes, and we should learn that we shouldn't take it for granted for longer than it takes, otherwise, we'll lose a beautiful way of getting in touch and the base for a family  union.  The Zureikat Weekly must never, never ever stop.  It is a necessity.

My warmest regards to the Chief.

Tarek

Dear fellow Zureikats,

I am writing with regards to Abu Tarek's e-mail.  It seemed to have caused quite a stir.  I do not agree with everything he said, but I will not be overly critical either.  After all, he does pay my salary.  However, I would
like to take this opportunity to share a concern I have had for a long time.

I am the last person to be talking about the future of The Zureikat Weekly, because, although I am a regular reader, I have rarely participated any information which is something I am sure Hazem does not appreciate even though he, being the person that he is, would never tell me that.  I look back at my time in Scotland, and I really could not imagine how it would have been like without The Zureikat Weekly.  Reading all the news of the family, seeing the pictures of everyone, reading about myself in occasions as well.  It has a special place in my heart, as I am sure it's the same with most other members of the ZW.

But we must all accept a fact that I believe we tend to forget in occasions: Hazem is 19.  Hazem is also a university student.  Hazem has school work, societies, soon enough a girl friend (if he doesn't have one by now).  Should we really be expecting him to produce an issue EVERY week.  Some might say it doesn't take a lot of time, but I can just imagine a Saturday night when all of us are enjoying ourselves, Hazem will be out with friends, and in the back of his mind there will always be the subject of this week's ZW issue.  In many cases, he will have to call home and get information when there is none (no thanks to me).  Some will argue that Hazem enjoys it.  I think we will be kidding ourselves if we think that, after 250 issues, Hazem is excited every weekend about producing the next issue.

Nevertheless, the discipline and efficiency Hazem has shown in producing a quality issue every week is beyond me.  I am sure my boss will concur that.  Personally, and I am saying this as an older brother, I would be just as pleased if instead of a ZW issue, I get a phone call from Hazem saying: "Sorry I forgot to produce this week's issue, as I was partying till the early hours of the morning with a Russian girl called Nicole".  Obviously, this is up to Hazem, but knowing him, he has never said no to anybody when his help was requested.  And being the selfless person he is, I am sure he will not say no if we all asked him to keep producing the ZW.  Perhaps it is time for the new generation to take charge of that.  There is so much energy and talent in the [younger Zureikat] generation to produce something similar.

We have a great family and far too many good memories together that I doubt the ending of the ZW will change things dramatically.  I realize that this email will upset a few people.  I would love the ZW to continue.  I am simply writing as a concerned brother, as I am sure Mr. Marwan was writing as a concerned father.

Yours truly,
The most good looking Zureikat,
Tarek Zureikat

Harran, Reema, Alia Return Home

Harran, Reema, Amer, and Leanne in Chicago last week

After visiting Chicago and Orlando, Harran and Reema returned to Amman on Thursday, November 13, 2003.  Alia, who spent two weeks doing physiotherapy in the UK, also returned home later that day.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Alia said, "During the week, we (Deena Kawar and I) were in Hemel Hempstead. v My physio was great but pretty intensive (1.5 hours daily; 2.5 hours the last day).  It seems I have to work harder.  I think I did pretty well though, all things considered.  Deena filmed the sessions during the second week so mom would be able to carry out the program with me here.   We also had loads of fun in between the physio.  I spent the weekends with friends from university.  It was great!!  I also saw a few Jordanian friends who were there.  Didn't manage to see the other Zureikats there, but spoke to them on the phone, as they were all quite busy."  What should be mentioned is that Alia was supposed to return home on Friday but was advised to return a day earlier because of a possibility of a strike at Heathrow Airport.

Hala Visits the White House

"Part of my training was to visit the White House...to see how briefings are done"

Hala, who is currently doing a one-year Hubert Humphrey program at the University of Maryland, visited the White House on Friday, November 14, 2003.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Hala said, "Part of my training was to visit the White House on Friday to see how briefings are done by the spokesman.  It was nice to see though it was very cold to go outside because of the cold weather we had Thursday and Friday."
