225th Issue
September 14, 2003

For the first time after their wedding

Dana and Ammar in Jordan...Next Week

Dana, who hasn't been in Jordan for almost 11 months, will be arriving on Thursday

Reception to be held on the 26th

For the first time after they got married on August 2, Dana and Ammar will be visiting Jordan.  Dana, who hasn't been in Amman since last October, is expected to arrive on Thursday, September 18.  Ammar, who last visited Jordan in May, will be arriving on Monday, September 22.  A reception celebrating Dana and Ammar's wedding is expected to be held at Marwan and Abla's on Friday, September 26, 2003.

Nick, the Hennings' Pet Dog, Dies in Sweden

Nick, who has been the Hennings' pet dog for the past thirteen years or so, died in Sweden on Monday, September 8, 2003.  Mona, who was in Amman just one day before Nick's death, had earlier told the Editor in Chief that Nick had been sick for some time.

Mona Returns to Sweden

After spending about two weeks in Amman, Mona returned to Sweden on Sunday, September 7, 2003.

Read Marwan's letter in the 'Letters to the Editor' section.

Dian Leaves to Syria to See Brother and New Baby

Dian left to Syria on Wednesday, September 10, to see her brother and his new baby.  She is expected to return home on Wednesday, September 17, 2003.

Amal Returns Home after Visiting Oman

Amal returned home after making a business trip to Oman.  She left Amman on Monday, September 8, and returned on Thursday, September 11, 2003.

Hala Spends Weekend at the Appalachian Mountains

"Please convey our best regards to everyone in Amman.  We miss them all."

Dalia, Deena, and Salem started school last week

Hala, who is living in the US this year, spent the weekend at a camp on the Appalachian Mountains in Maryland along with several Hubert Humphrey fellows.  The camp included two lectures on leadership.  "There was a dancing party at night.  We also went hiking," she said in a phone call with the Editor in Chief.  Hala left to the camp on Friday, September 12, and returned to Rockville on Sunday, September 14.  As for Dalia, Deena, and Salem: Deena started school on Thursday, September 11, while Dalia and Salem started on Friday, September 12, 2003.  "They are helping a lot at home," said Hala in the phone call, "Salem is helping with the laundry...Dalia is responsible for everything at home when Mommy is not there.  She has her own key...She comes back from school at 2:30 and waits for Deena and Salem to get back...I am very proud of her."  In the phone call, Hala also said that the children had to take an English test before being going to their schools.  "Thanks to Amman National School," she said, "their grades were beautiful."  Hala also sent her regards to the family.  "Please convey our best regards to everyone in Amman. We miss them all."

Marwan Zureikat - Amman, Jordan:

Dear Editor in Chief,

Thanks for the latest issue.  It is amazing how things develop over time.  I refer, of course, to the article in the Weekly concerning the number of Zureikats in the United States.  I think we are at a time now when there are more Zureikats outside Jordan than in Jordan.  This is not the only amazing thing happening.  The news of Leanne turning into a good cook is also “amazing”.  We trust Leanne's words but need to have others certify to the fact that she is turning into a good cook, not the least of which is Amer himself.  I miss them both.

Marwan Zureikat

Abu Harran with Wael Zureikat and his son last Easter.
