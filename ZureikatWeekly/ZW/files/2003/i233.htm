Marwan Suggests Discontinuing ZW

In a debate that has been gradually taking place for the past year or so regarding the future of The Zureikat Weekly, Marwan sent an open letter to the Editor in Chief suggesting that now is the right time "to consider stopping the issue of the Weekly".  The Editor in Chief would like to take this opportunity to ask all Zureikats to send their opinions on this issue.  A decision on such an issue is not an easy one to make and should be carefully considered.  Following is the full text of Marwan's e-mail:

Dear Hazem,

I have hesitated a lot before I decided to write this letter to you.  I have been regularly reading The Zureikat Weekly issues and never stopped admiring the effort and the dedication that is needed to keep this web magazine running weekly, non-stop, for over 4.5 years.  However, I consider it the right time now to consider stopping the issue of the Weekly and/or consider other options to maintain flow of the family news and information.

It is no secret that the Weekly was intended to maintain and keep our family culture and values.  It is not a coincidence that the Weekly was thought of and initiated by you after the passing away of your grandmother.  It was one expression of your and our desire to keep her memory and what she stood for alive and living with us.  But with the passing of time, things change.  The family grows, and people age.  The bonds that used to tie the family become overshadowed by other priorities and interests.  It is a fact now that some of us have no relationship with the Weekly at all.  Others do not read it, and many do not contribute to it.  To keep forcing the Weekly would, in my mind, be like working against the tide and may result in negative consequences.  It is great to maintain and keep our family values and culture, but this is something that we should not force and must leave for each of the smaller Zureikat family units to deal with in the manner they deem convenient to them.

The Zureikat Weekly has done a great job.  Its significance and value are huge.  In the future, we will all look back at it and be proud of it.  It will serve as an example for those who will follow us.  For me, it has shown, more than anything else, how much of a difference a single individual can make given the dedication and commitment that you have shown.  I am certainly a proud father and a proud Zureikat.

I leave it to you to consider this issue and make whatever decision you deem appropriate.

Marwan Zureikat

Amal and Ziad Return from Madrid

Amal sat next to former French President Giscard d’Estaing on her way to Amman

 

After a five-day visit to Madrid and after missing their flight from Paris to Amman causing a one-day delay, Amal and Ziad returned home on Thursday, November 6, 2003.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Amal said, "The trip to Madrid was fantastic.  It brought the memory of our honeymoon.  The weather was fine, except the first day, it was cold.  We visited the Prado Museum and did half day tours.  We missed the plane from Paris to Amman, so we had to fly to Frankfurt, and guess who was on board next to me?  Giscard d’Estaing, the former President of France, so I sat with him for 20 minutes talking about Iraq, Jordan, and Palestine.  It made our delay worth it."
