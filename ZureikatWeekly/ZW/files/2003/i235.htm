Ziad Appointed in the Senate

A royal decree was issued on Monday appointing the 54-member 19th Upper House of Parliament

Members of the new Senate after taking the oath of office.  Ziad is the third from the right.

Ziad was appointed as a member of the 19th Senate (Upper House of Parliament) on Monday, November 17,  after a royal decree was issued by His Majesty King Abdullah II appointing the new, 54-member senate, presided by Zeid Rifai.  Members of the new Senate took the oath of office on Thursday, November 20, 2003.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Ziad on this prestigious post, wishing him all the best.

Marwan: I Suggest We Close the Debate on This Issue and Go On

"All of the responses...reinforced the fact that we are a special family”

After two weeks of debating over the future of The Zureikat Weekly, Marwan has responded to the e-mails sent by a number of Zureikats, most of whom criticized his calls for discontinuing The Zureikat Weekly.  In his e-mail, Marwan suggested that the debate be closed and said that family members should find ways of "enriching the Weekly through more participation and support".  Following is the full text of Marwan's e-mail:

Dear Hazem,

It was both interesting and pleasing to me to read the responses of the ZW family members who commented on my letter.  Despite some support for the letter, I admit that the majority was not happy with the idea of discontinuing the Weekly, and some used harsh and angry words in their responses.  All of the responses, however, reinforced the fact that we are a “special family”.  I see no harm of reminding ourselves every now and then of this fact. We will all need to also remind ourselves that for this special tie to continue, we all need to find ways of enriching the Weekly through more participation and support to the Editor and the editorial staff.

I suggest we close the debate on this issue and go on.  Thank you for making all this happen.

Regards,
Marwan Zureikat

In light of the recent debate, the Editor in Chief has decided to postpone any decision on the future of The Zureikat Weekly until next year, when the newsletter celebrated its fifth anniversary.
