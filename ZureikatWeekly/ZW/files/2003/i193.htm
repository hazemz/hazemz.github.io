February 2, 2003

Physiotherapy Exercises Continue as Abu Harran Makes Speedy Recovery

Abu Harran is still doing his physiotherapy exercises.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Amal said, "The physiotherapist visited Jiddo; he was surprised by his speedy recovery.  He gave him few more exercises for his hand.  His walking and talking are getting better."

Mona Visits Amman

Mona left to Abu Dhabi on Friday...returns to Amman Tuesday

Mona arrived in Amman on Wednesday, January 29.  She then left to Abu Dhabi, UAE on Friday, January 31, to attend a conference and deliver a speech there.  Mona is expected to return back to Amman on Tuesday, February 4, 2003, to spend five more days there.

 

Zureikats Have Lunch at Abu Harran's in Mona's Honor

Celebrating Mona's visit, a number of family members had lunch (mashawi) at Abu Harran's on Friday, January 31, 2003.

Harran Undergoes Colonoscopy, Resumes Work

Harran underwent a successful colonoscopy on Thursday, January 30.  He resumed his work on Saturday, February 1, 2003.

After making her longest visit to the US

Abla Returns from DC

The family gathered at Marwan's after Abla's return

After making a ten-day trip to Washington DC, the longest she has ever made to the US, Abla N. returned home on Friday, January 31.  On this occasion, the family gathered at Marwan's on Saturday, February 1, 2003.

Abla Returns from Cairo after School Visit

Abla Z. returned home on Friday, January 31, after visiting Cairo along with some teachers and 12th grade students from Amman National School.  Abla had left Amman on Monday, January 27, 2003.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Bana said, "She had a wonderful time visiting the museums, the pyramids, and all the other touristic sites.  She also bumped into George Qirdahi (the host of the Arabic version of Who Wants to be a Millionaire) and took a picture with him.

Second Semester Begins

After almost a three-week mid-year break, the second semester for the 2002-2003 school year began on Sunday, February 2, 2003.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Zureikat principals, teachers, and students all the best in this semester.  What should be mentioned is that Kareem already started the second semester early last month.

Hassan Visits Syria and Lebanon, Returns Home

Hassan returned home on Thursday, January 30, 2003, after making a three-day business trip to Syria and Lebanon.

Reem Leaves to LA...Returns to the UK in a Week

Reem, who has been working in the US for the past few weeks, is currently visiting Los Angeles, California, where she will be spending one week before returning to the UK.

Marwan Signs Treaty for Reducing Unaccounted Water in Karak

Marwan (far left) along with the Minister of Water and Irrigation (second from right) and other officials signing the treaty (Source: http://www.petra.gov.jo/)

Marwan, the Chairman of the Board of the Jordanian Consulting Engineer Company (JCE), signed a treaty on Tuesday, January 28, 2003, for a technical and feasibility study project for reducing the unaccounted for water in the Governorate of Karak.  The above picture, along with details about this treaty, appeared in Jordanian newspapers on Wednesday.

Tarek Congratulates Kareem on Acceptances

 

In an e-mail he sent to the Editor in Chief last week, Tarek congratulated Kareem on the offers he got from a number of universities in the UK.  In his e-mail, Tarek said, "Please send my congratulations to Kareem for getting offers from Bristol and Kent to study law.  If he goes to Bristol, I will personally be doing a lot of visiting.  Bristol is where my company was located...amazing city."  The Zureikat Weekly would also like to take this opportunity to congratulate Kareem on his acceptances and wish him all the best in the future.

Zureikats Celebrate Reema's Birthday

Zureikats celebrated Reema's birthday on Tuesday, January 28, 2003.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Reema a very happy birthday and all the best.
