Dana and Ammar Set August 2 as their Wedding Day

Wedding to be held in Washington DC

Dana and Ammar, who got engaged on February 20, have set Saturday, August 2, 2003, as their wedding day.  The wedding, which will be the second Zureikat wedding for this year, will be held in Washington, DC.

Harran Suffers Acute Disk Prolapse

Alia: He is doing much better now...no worries

Harran suffered some back pains on Monday, April 7, 2003, which later turned out to be a result of an acute disk prolapse at levels 4 and 5.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Alia said, "He had been told to rest for a few days, starting last Monday evening.  Defying the pain, Harran went to work on Tuesday morning, but due to severe pains was brought to the ER of the hospital for an MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging).  Defying doctors' orders, because he was "much better" by then, Harran managed to 'go out' Friday night and went to work today (Saturday).  He came back for his usual lunch hour, but could not return to work because the pains flared up again.  All in all, Harran is doing much better now.  No worries -- provided he doesn't go to Anjara tomorrow! So, let's all keep our fingers crossed!"  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Harran a speedy recovery and all the best.

Dag Promoted to International Marketing and Sales Director

Mona updates us on the latest with the Hennings

Dag has been promoted to International Marketing and Sales Director at the company he works for in Sweden.  What should be mentioned is that Dag was appointed last August as the Head of Marketing in Europe.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Dag on his promotion and wish him all the best.  In other Henning news, Mona sent us the following update from Sweden: "Helene is still at Accenture sorting out the problems and finding solutions.  Sylvia in the pediatrics at Vienna University Clinic treating both the new born and old born.  I, myself, planning and working in Dar Al-Mona.  Gero is working as usual as an agent to Tuv Reinland and helping in the economy of Dar Al-Mona.  The snow is melting today and the beautiful snow drops flowers are coming from underneath. It is the spring."

Amal Spends a Day at the Ruweished Camp

Amal, who was appointed as a trustee for the National Center for Human Rights in February, spent Wednesday, April 9, 2003, at the Ruweished refugee camp on the Jordanian-Iraqi border.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Amal said, "On Wednesday, I went to Ruweished on the Iraqi border with the Red Crescent to check the hospital for the refugees from Iraq with other members from the National Council for the whole day."
