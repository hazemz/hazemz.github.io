226th Issue
September 21, 2003

Dana Returns Home

 

 

Right: Abla celebrating Dana's return with traditional songs.

 

Dana returned home on Thursday, September 18, 2003, on her first visit to Jordan after moving to Washington, DC last October.  Celebrating Dana's return, family and friends gathered at Marwan and Abla's on Thursday night.

 

Ammar Arrives Monday

Ammar left Washington on Sunday, September 21, and is expected to arrive in Amman on Monday, September 22, 2003.

An unusual number of Zureikat trips abroad last week:

Harran and Reema Back from Lebanon

 

Harran and Reema returned from Beirut after making a four-day trip to the Lebanese capital.  They left Amman on Wednesday, September 17, and returned on Sunday, September 21, 2003.

Mona and Gero Return from Russia

Mona: I recommend St. Petersburg for all Zureikats who love traveling

Mona and Gero returned to Sweden on Sunday, September 21, after making a three-day visit to St. Petersburg, Russia.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief which she sent upon her return, Mona said, "St. Petersburg I recommend for all the Zureikats who love traveling.  The city is celebrating its 300 years anniversary, so things were marvelous."  Mona and Gero had left to Russia on Thursday, September 18, 2003.

Marwan Returns from Kuwait

Marwan left to Kuwait on Monday, September 15, on a two-day business trip.  He returned home on Wednesday, September 17, 2003.

Amal Leaves to San Francisco

Amal left to San Francisco, California on Thursday, September 18, 2003, on a business trip.

Dian Returns Home after 8-Day Visit to Syria

Dian returned home on Thursday, September 18, after an eight-day visit to Aleppo, Syria.  Dian had left Amman on Wednesday, September 10, 2003.

Bana Gets 710/800 in the GMAT

Bana got 710 out of 800 (with a percentile score of 95) in her General Management Admissions Test (GMAT).  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Bana on her score and wish her all the best.

Kareem Leaves to Bristol on Thursday

Kareem will be leaving to the UK (accompanied by Reema) on Thursday, September 25, 2003, to start his first year as a law student at the University of Bristol.

  • Zureikats on the East Coast Survive 'Isabel'

Hala and the family and Ammar, who are currently in DC and Maryland (on the US East Coast), survived hurricane Isabel, which was later downgraded to a tropical storm.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Hala said that she found Isabel just like any "munkhafad jawwi" in Jordan.  Thursday and Friday were a holiday, and "I was very happy to relax", she added.  Hala told the Editor in Chief that she and Deena took a walk on Friday evening and saw some of the damages that Isabel left in their neighborhood.
