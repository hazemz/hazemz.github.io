Hala E-mails the ZW from Maryland

"Everything is fine now...I am now settled in our new townhouse"

Twenty days after leaving Amman to Maryland, Hala sent the following e-mail to the Editor in Chief on Friday, August 29, 2003:

Dearest Hazem,

Sorry for the delay in writing to you, but I was very busy settling down, finding housing, furniture, and it is not easy when you don't have a car, and I could not rent one because I did not bring with me the international driving license.

Anyway, thank the Lord everything is fine now, and my telephone has been hooked since last night.  I am now settled in our new townhouse which I like very much.  I came here on August 20 and started to buy few things for the kitchen and to prepare for the arrival of the Karadsheh.

I live in a very nice area (Rockville, Maryland).  The apartment. complex is very nice, lots of green area around so that the children will enjoy playing.  We have also swimming pools, tennis courts, and a very nice park, walking distance.  I have also two shopping centers around, so everything is easy to get.  I have also met very nice neighbors who have children the same age as mine and go to the same schools.  I visited already Salem's school...beautiful, and he will have wonderful teachers.

I will be having some courses in Media Management at the School of Journalism every Monday and Tuesday afternoon and seminars on Friday.  The other days, I will be working on my professional development either in TV or in radio stations.  The school and the International Center for Journalists will help me in that.  So all is pretty nice."..."

Please convey my best regards to all."..."

Aunti Hala

What should be mentioned is that Naief and the children are expected to leave Amman on Wednesday, September 3, 2003.
