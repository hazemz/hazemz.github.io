224th Issue
September 7, 2003

Naief, Dalia, Deena, Salem Leave to the US

     

After several delays, Naief, Dalia, Deena, and Salem left to the US to spent this academic year there.  Naief and the children left Amman on Wednesday, September 3, 2003, and arrived in Maryland the next day.

Hazem Returns to Minnesota

Hazem returned to the US on Monday, September 1, 2003, to start his second year at Macalester College in St. Paul, Minnesota.

New Academic Year Begins

Sabine: 10th grade; Dalia: 9th; Nadine and Yacoub: 8th; Deena: 7th; Yacoub: 6th; Salem: 4th

The 2003-2004 academic year has started around the world.  Zureikat school students in Jordan started classes on Monday, September 1, 2003.  Sabine (Modern Schools) is in the 10th grade this year; Nadine (Modern Schools) and Yacoub S. (Amman National School) are both in 8th; and Yacoub Z. (Modern Schools) is in the 6th grade.  Abla N., Director of the Jubilee School, and Abla Z., Coordinator of the English Department at Amman National School, both started work a few weeks before September 1.

 

More Zureikats in the US than Ever

This academic year, there are more Zureikats in the US than ever before: Hala, Naief, Dalia, Deena, and Salem in Maryland, Dana and Ammar in DC, Amer and Leanne in Illinois, Samer in Louisiana, Ayman in Massachusetts, and Hazem in Minnesota.  Samer, Ayman, and Hazem already started their classes, while Dalia, Deena, and Salem, who are attending three different schools, will start school on Monday, September 8, 2003.

 

Omar Transfers to JU...Starts in a Few Weeks

Omar has transferred from the Princess Sumayya University for Technology to the Jordan University starting this academic year.  He is expected to start classes in a few weeks.

 

Talal Starts his Classes...Kareem Leaves in a Few Weeks

 

As for Zureikats studying in the UK: Talal, who left Amman on August 27, already started his classes.  Kareem, who will be starting his first year at Bristol, is expected to leave Amman in the next few weeks.

 

'Hill House Kindergarten' Opens its Doors Soon

Hill House Kindergarten, owned by Abla N., Mr. Ayman Saket, and Mrs. Donna Naber, is expected to open its doors in a few weeks for the start of the new academic year.  The Kindergarten is located in Abdoun.  What should be mentioned is that Abla N. and Mr. Saket also own CyberEd, a center specializing in education through the computer.

Amer and Leanne E-mail the ZW

Leanne reassures the family: Although Amer is deprived of sleep, he is never deprived of a good home-cooked meal when he gets home

 

Amer and Leanne sent the following e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly:

Hi Zureikats,

Hope all is well in Amman.

Things in Chicago are going well.  Amer is working very very hard!!  But you will all be glad to know that although he is deprived of sleep, he is never deprived of a good home-cooked meal when he gets home; thanks to the cooking lessons I was given by Aunty Reema during her visit, I am now a capable cook on my way to making larger and more complicated dishes!

Hazem, we have always admired and enjoyed the ZW, but reading it while being so far away from home allows us to travel back to Amman once a week and be with you all!

We must also mention how proud we are of Kareem.  He is no doubt going to excel at university and move on to be the best (and most charming) lawyer we know!!

We miss you all so much and can't wait to come home to see you...

All our love,
Leanne and Amer

Houston's Opens New Branch in Mecca Mall

Houston's opened a second branch in the new Mecca Mall in Amman.  The new branch started receiving guests on Saturday, September 6, 2003.  Among the first guests were, as Marwan put it, "Yacoub Shomali and family".  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Marwan and the Houston's family on the opening of the new branch and wish the restaurant all the success.

Ziad Delivers Lecture in Syria, Returns Home

Ziad left to Damascus, Syria on Tuesday, September 2, 2003, to deliver a lecture.  He returned home the next day.
