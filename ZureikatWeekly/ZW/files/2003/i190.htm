A day after Samer's departure

Talal Returns to England

A day after Samer's return to New Orleans, Talal, who is currently studying at the St. George's Hospital Medical School in England, returned to London on Monday, January 6, 2003.

Tarek Extends Stay in Yemen, Returns Monday

Tarek, who left to Yemen on Sunday, January 5, on a business trip, extended his stay for four more days.  He will be returning home on Monday, January 13, 2003.

Reem Sends an Update from the US

"I'm currently in Houston working at the VA Hospital"

Reem, who is currently in Houston, Texas, sent an e-mail to the Editor in Chief last week.  In her e-mail, Reem said, "I'm currently in Houston working at the VA (Veteran's Affair) Hospital doing a GI rotation.  It's a very good experience, and I'm learning a lot, but I'm also one of the youngest there because everyone here does a premed degree first...I'm going to go to Austin this weekend and possibly visit Samer in two weeks time."  Regarding her visit to Megève, France where she spent New Year's Eve, Reem said, "Megève was very cool.  (It's located on the border between France, Italy, and Switzerland.)  I went skiing on most days and sampled the French food at night."  Reem concluded her e-mail by wishing the family a Happy New Year and Amer good luck in his interviews.

Zureikats Celebrate Amer's Birthday

Zureikats celebrated Amer's birthday on Sunday, January 12, 2003.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Amer a very happy birthday and all the best.

Marwan Zureikat - Amman, Jordan:

Dear Sir,

I must say, at the outset, that I have enjoyed reading the “NEW” 2003 issue of The Zureikat Weekly.  The Weekly has now established itself as a professional and credible publication that makes us all a proud family.

I have taken note of the fact that you have commenced the process of selecting your team of correspondents and photographers for this year.  As you are aware, I have started some months ago an argument over the issue of appointing Abla N. as one of the Weekly photographers.  This had raised many responses from the family members.  Some supported the idea, and others opposed it.  Needless to say that the responses brought life and added value to the Weekly.  Some were even enlightening like the letter from Ziad which introduced, for the first time, the concept or science of ‘GENETIC PHOTOGRAPHY”.

The hidden message in my letter and in the responses that followed is, and will always be, LOVE.  It is for this, and many other reasons, that I write to you now to ask you to give serious consideration towards reappointing Abla N. as a Weekly photographer.  I am sure that this decision will receive the support of the majority, if not all, of the Zureikat family members and will serve as a modest recognition of her dedication and hard work during her term of service as the Weekly photographer last year.

May I take this opportunity to extend my kindest regards.

Marwan Zureikat
