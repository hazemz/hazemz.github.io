208th Issue
May 18, 2003

Abu Harran Recovering from Minor Injury

Abu Harran is currently recovering from a minor injury he obtained when he fell down at his home last week.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Abu Harran a speedy recovery and all the best.

After spending three days in DC on the way back

Hazem Returns Home

 

After completing the second semester of his first year at Macalester College, Hazem returned home on Sunday, May 18, 2003.  Hazem left St. Paul, Minnesota on Tuesday, May 13, to Washington DC, where he spent a few days with Dana and Ammar.  Celebrating Hazem's return, Zureikats gathered at Marwan's on Sunday evening.

Kareem Finishes Final Exams, Graduates May 30

Kareem finished his IB final exams on Tuesday, May 13.  He will be graduating from the Amman Baccalaureate School on Friday, May 30, 2003.

Harran and Reema Leave to Paris

 

Harran and Reema left to Paris on Sunday, May 18, on a six-day visit to France during which Harran will be attending a cardiology conference.  Harran and Reema are expected to return home on Saturday, May 24, 2003.

Ziad Visits Boston, Orlando

Ziad left to the US on Wednesday, May 14.  He first visited Ayman in Boston, where he spent one night, and is currently in Orlando, Florida.  Ziad is expected to return home on Friday, May 23, 2003.

Hassan Leaves to Lebanon

Hassan left to Lebanon on Sunday, May 18, 2003, on a business trip.  He is expected to return home later this week.

Mona and Gero Return from Gotland Island

Dag returns from Belgium...visits Lisbon on Tuesday

Mona and Gero returned from the Island of Gotland on Sunday, May 18.  They had earlier left to the island by boat on Friday, May 16.  During the two-day visit, Mona attended the annual meeting for the honorary consuls in Sweden.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Mona said, "We met lots of people and the weather was perfect.  The city of Visby is the capital of the island.  It was built in the 11th century and is full of places worth while seeing."  In her e-mail, Mona also said that Dag returned from Belgium on the same day that she and Gero left.  She also said that Dag will be leaving to Lisbon on Tuesday, May 20, 2003.

Deena Gets 1st Prize in Poetry Recital

Deena won the first prize in a poetry recital for the famous poet Bader Shaker Al-Sayyab.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Deena on her prize and wish her all the best.  What should be mentioned is that Dalia and Yacoub S. made their debut early this month in a play also about Al-Sayyab.

Dalia and Yacoub Camp in Ajloun

 

Dalia and Yacoub S. spent the weekend in Ajloun on a school camp.  They both enjoyed the camp immensely.  Activities included were exercising in the morning, hiking in the mountains, singing, water fights, and, finally, a dancing party under the stars.  Their only complaint was the lousy food!!  Dalia and Yacoub left Amman on Friday, May 16, 2003, and returned the next day.

Shomalis Bid Farewell to their Infamous Lada

Amongst tears and sobs, the Shomalis had to finally say goodbye to a loyal member of the Shomali family: The White Lada Station Wagon.  The Lada was sold on Wednesday, May 14.  The decision to sell the car came after months of it being neglected by the Shomalis when a newer, brighter car was bought (Bashar's Opel).  We wish the Lada all the best and may it live a healthy life with its new owner.
