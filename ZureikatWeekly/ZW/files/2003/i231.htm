Leanne Hired at PR Company

More than four months after moving to Chicago, Leanne has been hired at Ogilvy Public Relations, one of the biggest PR companies in the US.  She is expected to start her new job on Monday, October 27, 2003.  In an e-mail she sent to the Editor in Chief, Leanne said, "Things are looking up for us.  Needless to say, I will have to dedicate less time to cooking, and, in my defense, I must say that Amer's silence regarding my cooking is by no means proof of my 'bad' cooking.  The reason he has not commented is simply due to the fact that he has not had a day of [rest] in a few weeks and is so exhausted!  You will be hearing from him soon, and once I get back I shall invite all Zureikats for a dinner which I shall host personally, and the menu will consist of several creative dishes that I have invented over the past few months!!"

With Amer, Leanne, Samer, and Ayman, it would have included all the Zureikats in the US.  Hala and Naief hosted Dana, Ammar, and Hazem for dinner (mlookhyyeh and fish) on Saturday, October 25.  This came as Hazem made a four-day visit to Washington, DC.  What should be mentioned is that Rockville, Maryland (where Hala and the family live) and Arlington, Virginia (where Dana and Ammar live) are both suburbs of Washington, DC.  Hazem, who left to DC on Wednesday, October 22, returned to St. Paul, Minnesota on Sunday, October 26, 2003.

Another small reunion...in Sweden

Reem Visits the Hennings

Reem returned to London on Sunday, October 26, after spending the weekend with the Hennings in Sweden.  She had arrived in Stockholm on Friday, October 24, 2003.  "We had a late dinner on Friday beside the open fire," said Mona in her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief.  "She has been to parties with Helene, and today, Sunday, visited the National Museum.  I think she [is having] a good time."

Kareem Sends his First E-Mail from Bristol

Kareem, who is currently studying at the University of Bristol, sent his first e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly after arriving in Bristol late last month.  Following is the full text of Kareem's e-mail:

A month has passed, and I am finally settled in.  Everything is just the way I wanted it to be, and I am enjoying the student life.  I have come to realize that I owe my family a thanks for making me the person I am.  I would like to thank my mother and father for everything they have done; without you, I wouldn't be the person I am.  You have made such a positive impact on my life, and I will be forever grateful.  It is true you never realize how important something is until you finally leave it.  I thank you for everything you have given and shared.  You are what I have always wanted to be, and it is through you that I find my strength, willpower, and determination to reach for the highest goal.  I will achieve it.  Zraigo and Leanne, I love you guys.  Amer, after all the beatings I have suffered and all the hardships you suffered in university, I now learn the meaning of being a man.  As I have said, you are more than a brother, but also a figure which I look up to.  Alia, it is your ability that made me see my disability.  You have been through so much yet always managed to keep a smile on your face.  You are a true idol and it is through you that I see the impossible as a mere state of mind.  All the Zureikats, I love you all.  Each and every one of us is lucky to have been brought up in such a family.  Tahya lil Karak wa Ajloun.

Source: Jordan News Agency (Petra)

Reema Holds Annual Breakfast for FFSPD

Guests included the Superstar Diana Carazon

Reema held the annual breakfast charity event for the Families and Friends Society for Persons with Disabilities (FFSPD) at the Le Royale Hotel in Amman on Saturday, October 25, 2003.  The event was patronized by HRH Princess Mona Al-Hussein and was attended by HRH Princess Majda Raad and Jordanian singer and Superstar, Diana Carazon.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Alia said, "The event was centered around the portage program (one of the society's 6 programs) which is the home-visiting program and its newly established KG, which was initiated by Suad Aloul, one of FFSPD's members, who died 2 months ago from cancer.  Reema was the main speaker, talking about Suad and her endless energy and how much she gave, quoting Gibran Khalil Gibran on several occasions, followed by two mothers of children with disabilities, who spoke about how the society, Reema, and Suad have given meaning to their children's lives and theirs!  Princess Mona also awarded several members of FFSPD, the two mothers, children, and staff who have contributed in one way or another, in addition to a number of donors and supporters."
