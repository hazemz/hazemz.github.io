'A Royal Wedding' in the US Capital

 

 

Left: The Mediterranean evening at Nayla Restaurant / Right: The Sunday brunch at Sequoia

August 2, 2003...another milestone in Zureikat history, as family and friends from all over the world gathered in Washington, DC to attend the wedding of Dana and Ammar (dubbed by many as 'a royal wedding').  The festivities began two days earlier, when Marwan and Abla hosted a Mediterranean evening at Nayla (a Lebanese restaurant in Washington).  A bridal shower was also held at the Ritz Carlton the next day.  Ammar's parents, Mr. Fahhad and Mrs. Ayda Daoud, hosted a post-wedding brunch on Sunday, August 3, 2003, at Sequoia Restaurant in the Washington Harbor area.

August 2: The Wedding Day

Church service

At about 4:15pm, buses and limousines left the Latham Hotel in Georgetown for the St. Sophia Greek Orthodox Cathedral.  The service began a bit after 5pm.  Ammar's parents entered, followed by Abla and Tarek, the five groomsmen (who included Tarek and Hazem), the five bride's maids, the best man (Basheer Daoud, Ammar's brother) and the maid of honor (Lara Ghaleb Zureikat).  Then, of course, came the bride accompanied by Marwan.

Reception

After the church service, the wedding party moved to the City Museum of Washington, DC, where cocktails and canapés were served on the top floor.

Dinner

Everyone moved to the bottom floor of the museum at about 7:30pm for the seated wedding dinner.  Dana and Ammar then entered the hall and danced on Tonight (by Frank Sinatra).  The dance was followed by a speech given by Marwan in which he talked about what the treasure of love can do in bringing two people, a family, or even a whole organization together.  He said that love, to him, is the "king of kings and the lord of lords".  A blessing by Father George followed the speech, and dinner was served.  Shortly after dinner, Dana and Marwan danced on Unforgettable (by Nat King Cole).  This was followed by the cake-cutting ceremony.  Dancing continued until the wedding dinner ended at midnight.

Dana and Ammar's wedding has been added to the Zureikat Calendar.

Tarek Leaves to London on Monday

Reema, Alia, and Leanne Arrive in Chicago

Mona and Dag Back in Sweden

Besides Marwan and the family, Mona, Reema, Dag, Alia, and Leanne attended Dana and Ammar's wedding in Washington, DC.  Reema and Alia arrived in Chicago on Tuesday, July 29, then left to Washington the next day accompanied by Leanne.  Mona and Dag also arrived in DC on Wednesday, July 30.  Shortly after the Sunday brunch, Reema, Alia, and Leanne returned to Chicago, where Reema and Alia will be spending the next few days, while Mona and Dag went back to Sweden.  As for Marwan and the family: Marwan, Abla, and Hazem are expected to leave to New York City on Monday, August 4, to spend one night there before flying back to Amman on Tuesday.  Tarek, however, will be leaving to London, also on Monday, to spend one week with his friends there.  Other relatives and friends who attended the wedding include Dr. Yousef Nusseir, Dr. Najla Bashour, Miss Bana Bashour, Dr. Shabib Ammari, Dr. Anwar Battikhi, Mr. Wael Zu'rub, his wife and daughter, Mr. Kamel Saba and his wife, and Mr. Mansour Hareek and his wife.  What should be mentioned is that Amal and Amer were both not able to make it to the wedding.

In Memory of Umm Harran

July 30, 2003: The 6th anniversary of the passing away of Umm Harran

Hala Leaves to Maryland Next Saturday

Naief and the children to follow on the 23rd

Hala, Naief, Dalia, Deena, and Salem will be spending the next academic year in Maryland, in the US.  Hala is expected to leave to Maryland on Saturday, August 9, 2003, while Naief and the children will follow on August 23.  This comes a few weeks after Hala was awarded the Hubert Humphrey fellowship to attend the class of 2003-2004 at the University of Maryland at College Park.  Naief, who had earlier received the Distinguished Scholarship Award from the Arab Fund for Social and Economic Development (based in Kuwait) to teach and do research in Columbus, Ohio managed, in the last few weeks, to move his award to Washington, DC (near Maryland).
