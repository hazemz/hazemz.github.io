Abu Harran Starts Physiotherapy

Amal: My father is doing so well. His speech improved; his walking is far better.

A few days after leaving the hospital on Saturday, January 18, Abu Harran started his physiotherapy exercises and "is doing so well", according to what Amal said in her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief.  In her e-mail, Amal also said, "His speech improved; his walking is far better than last week.  He started to feel his right hand.  He is doing his exercises daily, and, hopefully, he will be back to normal in four weeks."  On the other hand, some family members had lunch at Abu Harran's on Friday, January 24, 2003.  Lunch was brought by Dian.

Amer and Leanne Set May 15 as their Wedding Day

Amer and Leanne have set May 15, 2003 as their wedding day.  Amer and Leanne officially got engaged on July 4, 2002.  More details about the first wedding among the Zureikat grandchildren in the coming issues...

Amal Starts Physiotherapy Soon

Amal suffered a flu with a fever and bronchitis last week

Amal, who injured her back last November, will be starting her physiotherapy soon.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Amal said, "My back is getting better.  Hopefully, I can start my physiotherapy in the next few days."  In her e-mail, Amal also said, "I had a terrible flu with fever and bronchitis.  I was on antibiotic up
till now which prevented me from going to the clinic.  Hoping I would be better by the end of this week."  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Amal a speedy recovery from her back and her flu.

Tarek Hosts 20 People on a Magloobeh

Tarek invited 20 people on Friday, January 24, 2003, to taste his very own Magloobeh.  Bana, who was among those invited, commented on this event in her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief:

"As you all know, some of us were honored to be asked to taste Tarek's Magloobeh on Friday evening.  I would just like to say that Tarek's Magloobeh was one of the tastiest I had in a long time.  What was so unique about the Magloobeh, as one of Tarek's friends pointed out, was that every bite tasted different than the one before it.  The reason, according to Tarek, was that he spiced the food according to the tempo of the songs he was listening to (i.e. a disco song would mean he virtually spilled nut meg all over the rice, and a romantic song would mean hardly no spicing at all).  Anyway, I would like to congratulate Tarek on this amazing feast.  He managed to disregard all the phone calls from the Zureikat women to "help out" and give free tips, and cooked a great meal for 20 people.  Tarek, we love you and we can't wait to taste your next dish."
