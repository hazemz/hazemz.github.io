Hala and Family Leave to Germany

       

Naief left to Germany on Saturday, July 21, to start a three-month research program as part of a Dad Scholarship he received.  Hala, Dalia, Deena, and Salem also left to Germany a week later, on Saturday, July 28, 2001.  They (Hala and the children) are expected to return home in one month.  Just before releasing this issue, Hala sent an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly.  In her e-mail, Hala said, "Our trip was comfortable...Naief was at the airport of Frankfurt to receive us.  The trip by fast train to Gottingen from Frankfurt was 2 hours.  Our
residence is nice and has a beautiful and big garden with huge old trees.  Its location is only 10 minutes walk from the center of the town and on the side 10 minutes walk from two big complexes of swimming with many
facilities for children to play.  We spent all of last Sunday at the swimming...The children are happy here, and we got a football, cycle, and scooter for them to play with.  My regards to (...) everyone."

Editor in Chief Returns from the US

 

Left: Hazem giving part of his group's presentation at the end of the program \ Right: Hazem's group

 

Left: This is where Hazem worked (the USGS building in Caltech) \ Right: This is where Hazem lived (Erdman Hall, Occidental College)

The Editor in Chief returned home on Sunday, July 29, 2001, after a two-week visit to California in which he joined the Apprenticeship Program of the Institute for Educational Advancement at the United States Geological Survey (USGS) in the California Institute of Technology (Caltech), Pasadena, California.  During the two weeks, Hazem stayed at Erdman Hall, Occidental College, Los Angeles, California.  The group of seven students, which Hazem was part of, conducted a study and gave a presentation at the end of the program.  The sites that Hazem visited during the two weeks include Universal Studios, Venice Beach, the Getty Museum, and Wallace Creek.

Abla Z. and Yacoub returned home after a two-week visit to Sweden.  Hopefully, we'll be getting more details about the trip next week.

Zureikats celebrated Bashar's birthday on Wednesday, July 18, and Dian's birthday on Friday, July 20, 2001.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish both Bashar and Dian happy birthdays and all the best.
