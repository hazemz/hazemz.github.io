92nd Issue

Sunday, February 4, 2001

After completing all the requirements late last month

Bashar Now has a BA Degree

Commencement ceremony to be held in June

After three and a half years of studying at the University of Jordan, Bashar completed all the requirements for his BA degree in Finance & Accounting on January 23, 2001.  The official commencement ceremony will be held in June 2001.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Bashar and wish him all the best in the future.

 

Bana and Omar Finish Their Finals...Start February 11

  

On the other hand, Bana, who also studies at University of Jordan, and Omar, who studies at the Princess Sumayya College, finished their finals last week and will start the 2nd semester on Sunday, February 11, 2001.

Zureikat School Students Start the 2nd Semester

Zureikat school students in Jordan started the 2nd semester of the academic year 2000-2001.  Also, among those who started the 2nd semester are Abla and Abla.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish the Zureikat students (and the Ablas) all the best in the 2nd semester.

Talal in London Soon for Interview with St. George's

Amal will be meeting Talal in London at the end of this month

Talal, who is currently studying in the UWC of the Adriatic in Italy, got an interview with St. George's Medical School in London.  In a phone call with The Zureikat Weekly, Amal said that the interview will be on March 1.  She said that she will be meeting with Talal in London on February 27.  Amal also said that she and Talal will be attending Grease, a play produced and directed by Reem, who also studies at St. George's, on February 28, 2001.  She said that Talal will be leaving to Venice on March 2, 2001 to join his friends on a project week in Crete Island, while she will spend a few days with Reem and then return home.

Dana's 4th Story Airs on JTV

Dana's 4th story for Jordan Television aired on the news on Thursday, February 1, 2001.  This story was about the 4th anniversary for the partnership between British Mediterranean Airways and British Airways and the conference which was held on this occasion in the Intercontinental Hotel in Amman.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to congratulate Dana on her 4th story and wish her all the best.

Hassan Returns from Beirut

Hassan returned to Amman on Saturday, February 3, 2001, after a brief business trip to Beirut, Lebanon.

Reema Returns Home...Sunday

Reema is expected to return home on Sunday, February 4, 2001.  Reema is currently in New York attending the meeting for the Preparatory Committee for the Special Session of the General Assembly on Children.

A letter that Marwan received from Tarek's group director at the Southwestern Company, Dave Causer:

Dear Mr. Zureikat,

Although we've never had a chance to meet, I just wanted to take a few minutes today to let you know about a special recognition your son, Tarek has received at the Southwestern Company.  As you may be aware, we have approximately 3,000 students who work with us each year.  These are the most talented university students we can find from all around the world.  They are selected from a group of over 30,000 students that are exposed to the Southwestern program each year.

Each year, five of these students are nominated for Southwestern's Student Excellence Award.  It's the award given to the most outstanding students in each Director Group.  This year, Tarek has been nominated from our part of the company to represent us for this prestigious award.

At this time, I'm not sure whether Tarek will win the final Student Excellence Award or not, but just that he has been nominated as one of the final five out of the 3,000 students should let you know how much we think of the outstanding job your son has done in the Southwestern program.

I personally had a chance to see this in person last summer as I visited the Virginia area that Tarek was working in.  He personally sacrificed his own sales and income to help manage and make sure the students he brought out for the summer were successful.  There wasn't any manager in the Southwestern Company who did more to help their students be successful than Tarek did last year.

Proof of this was the great success that Tarek's team had.  Although they were below average at the start of the summer, by the end they were a team who was competing for the top recognition spots at Southwestern.

Tarek has developed excellent communication skills and sales ability over the previous summers here at Southwestern.  He has become an exceptional leader and has a great future.

I want to thank you personally for raising such a fine outstanding young individual as Tarek.  He is a rare individual who will become a great leader in the future.

Although Jordan is a long way from here, hopefully some day I will have a chance to visit.  At that time, I hope to be able to thank you in person for sending such an outstanding young man to participate in our program.

If there is anything I could ever do to assist Tarek, I hope you would feel free to call or write.

Sincerely, Dave Causer, Director of Sales
