Abla Appointed in Board of Trustees for New Museum

Abla N. has been officially appointed in the Board of Trustees for a new children's museum to be established at the Al-Hussein Public Parks in Amman.  The board is chaired by HM Queen Rania.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Abla on her new position, wishing her all the best.

Bana Promoted at Fastlink

Bana has been promoted from Senior Pricing Officer to Business Planning Team Leader

Bana, who is currently working at Fastlink, the largest mobile phone service provider in Jordan, got promoted from Senior Pricing Officer to Business Planning Team Leader.  Bana has been working at Fastlink for three years now, ever since her graduation from university.  This is her second promotion.  She was promoted from Pricing Office to Senior Pricing Officer just over a year ago.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Bana on her promotion, wishing her all the best.

Local

  • Iraq has recalled its ambassador to Jordan in a tit-for-tat move following Jordan's earlier decision to pull its envoy out of Baghdad.  Jordan withdrew its charge d'affaires Damai Haddad saying that anti-Jordanian protests outside its Baghdad embassy had made it unsafe for him to remain.  Shia protesters say that Jordan had a role in a suicide bombing last month.  Now Iraq has pulled its envoy out of Amman for consultations, saying relations were "in crisis mode".  Protests were held outside the Jordanian embassy in Baghdad on Friday, and an Iraqi flag was raised over the building, while Jordanian flags were burnt.  Shia groups are angry after reports that the family of a Jordanian, Raed Mansour al-Banna, held celebrations after he carried out a suicide bombing which killed 125 people in Hillah, south of Baghdad, on 28 February.  Nearly all the victims of that attack were Shia police and army recruits.  The Jordanian flag was burnt during protests.  However, al-Banna's family have now denied that he carried out the Hillah attack, saying only that he was responsible for a suicide attack somewhere in Iraq.

  • King Abdullah II will not attend the upcoming Arab League summit, scheduled to begin Monday in Algeria, Foreign Minister Hani al-Mulki announced Sunday.  According to al-Mulki, King Abdullah had previously committed to meeting with major American investors.  King Abdullah is currently on a visit to the United States, and had intended to continue to directly to the summit.  Despite al-Mulki's denial, it is clear the king's decision is related to the refusal of the Arab League to adopt his proposal, by which the Arab states would normalize relations with Israel prior to a comprehensive Israeli-Palestinian peace agreement.  Jordan accepted amendments to its contentious proposal that aimed to revise the long-standing Arab strategy to normalize relations with Israel, after failing to persuade other Arab countries that it was not trying to dilute the conditions Israel must meet.

  • King Abdullah II is currently on a working visit in the US.  He met with President George W. Bush earlier last week.

International

  • A leading member of the Lebanese opposition has rejected the president's call for dialogue to resolve the political turmoil gripping the country.  President Emile Lahoud appealed for an immediate dialogue between Lebanon's anti-Syrian opposition and pro-government factions.  But Druze leader Walid Jumblatt said Mr. Lahoud was too closely tied to Syria.  Mr. Lahoud's call came after a car bomb in a predominantly Christian suburb in the capital Beirut injured 11 people.  It is not clear who the target was. But the opposition says Damascus supporters are keen to stir unrest to justify the presence of Syrian troops in Lebanon.

  • Israel says it will hand control of the West Bank city of Tulkarm to Palestinian forces on Monday, following the return of Jericho last Wednesday.

  • An investigation has begun into a suicide car bombing in the Gulf state of Qatar that killed a British man and injured 12 other people.  Authorities in Qatar blamed an Egyptian man for carrying out the attack outside a theatre near the capital, Doha, on Saturday night.
