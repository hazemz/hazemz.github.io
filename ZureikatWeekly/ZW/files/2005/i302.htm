Local

  • Israeli officials on Sunday said their country may release Jordanian prisoners in its jails as Foreign Minister Hani Mulki concluded his visit to the Palestinian Territories and Israel.

  • In another move to delineate the scope of activities of the country's professional associations, the government on Wednesday said it was including an article to a draft law that would sever the unions' ties with their branches in the Palestinian territories.  The proposed law, which was approved last night by the Cabinet, authorizes the Audit Bureau to inspect the associations' financial records, according to Minister of Interior Samir Habashneh, who on Tuesday presented an outline of the draft law to parliamentary blocs. The minister, who yesterday met with independent deputies, said association funds should be spent only in Jordan and on activities that upgrade professional standards. He said the law was necessary to advance the professional mandate of the associations, expand the scope of participation and achieve fair distribution of association funds to all governorates. The draft also permits these associations to open branches in the Kingdom's governorates.

International

  • Syria will start pulling back its forces in Lebanon on Monday, and the United States warned it would accept no half measures and wanted the Syrians gone before May elections.  Under intense international pressure, Syrian President Bashar Assad announced plans on Saturday for a complete troop withdrawal from Lebanon but said Damascus would play a role in its neighbor's affairs.  This came after Lebanon's pro-Syrian Prime Minister Omar Karameh resigned on Monday in the face of mass protests.  During the week, Saudi Arabia joined international calls for Syria to withdraw its troops from Lebanon.

  • The Palestinian Authority won ringing support Tuesday from the European Union, the United States and other world powers for an ambitious raft of reforms intended to create a viable Palestinian state once an elusive Middle East peace becomes reality.  The endorsement from 23 nations and six major international organizations, including the United Nations, came during a day of talks in London haunted by a suicide bomb attack in Tel Aviv four days earlier that killed five people and shattered a fragile Israeli-Palestinian ceasefire.

  • Gunmen killed a judge and lawyer working for the tribunal that will try Saddam Hussein and members of his former regime, a day after the secret court referred five of the ousted president's aides to trial for alleged crimes against humanity, officials and a relative of the slain men said Wednesday.

  • Freed Italian journalist Giuliana Sgrena has been hurt by US troops firing at a car taking her to Baghdad airport soon after her release.  An Italian secret service agent was killed in the shooting at a checkpoint.
