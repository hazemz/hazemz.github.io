Amer and Leanne Return to Chicago

 

After spending almost three weeks in Amman on their second visit after moving to the US, Amer and Leanne returned to Chicago on Monday, January 17, 2005.

Ayman Returns to Boston for Last Semester at Tufts

After spending his winter break in Amman, Ayman returned to Boston on Tuesday, January 18, 2005, to start his last semester at Tufts University.  Ayman will be graduating this May with a Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering.

 

Hazem Returns to Minnesota

Hazem is expected to leave to St. Paul, Minnesota on Sunday, January 23, 2005, where he will be starting the second semester of his third year at Macalester College.

Amal and Ziad Leave to Moscow

 

Amal and Ziad left to Moscow, Russia on Friday, January 21, 2005, where Ziad (a current member of the Jordanian Senate) will be part of a delegation representing HM King Abdullah II.  Both Amal and Ziad are expected to return home on Monday, January 24.

Marwan Visits Denver, Returns to DC

Marwan made another business trip to Dubai before leaving to the US last week

Marwan, who is currently in the US, made a short working visit to Denver, Colorado.  He left Washington, DC on Thursday, January 20, 2005, and returned on Friday, January 21.  Before leaving to the US last week, Marwan made another business trip to Dubai, UAE.  He left Amman on Monday, January 10, and returned on Thursday, January 13.

Harran, Reema, Alia Spend Eid Holiday in Aqaba

   

Harran, Reema, and Alia left to Aqaba on Thursday, January 20, 2005, and returned to Amman on Saturday, January 22.  The three-day vacation came as Jordan, along with Arab and Muslim countries around the world, celebrated Eid Al-Adha.

Local

  • A widely-representative committee is to be formed next week to draw up a new scale of state priorities over the coming decade, including plans to reform government performance, a senior official announced.

  • The government on Tuesday banned a professional associations seminar on Iraqi elections, a day after Interior Minister Samir Habashneh warned unions against meddling in politics. Meanwhile, a member of the Jordan Engineers Association, who is standing trial at the Court of First Instance for slandering the government, on Tuesday said he was only exercising his constitutional right. Ali Hattar is being tried on charges of violating Article 191 of the Jordanian Penal Code, following a Dec. 14 lecture entitled “Why we boycott America” at the Professional Associations Complex.

International

  • Israeli and Palestinian leaders decided Wednesday to hold security talks on a joint effort towards halting surging violence, officials said, heading off the possibility of a large-scale Israeli invasion to stop rocket and mortar fire in Gaza. The decision came as Palestinian leader Mahmoud Abbas edged closer to securing a ceasefire commitment from the factions, a participant in truce talks said, and the Palestinian police commander ordered a deployment of police along the border to stop rocket and mortar attacks.

  • Dozens of people were killed in attacks around Iraq last week, one of which was a seven-car bomb rampage targeting the Australian embassy on Wednesday. Meanwhile, Iraqi insurgents who were holding eight Chinese laborers hostage in Iraq have released the men. In another development, images of British troops allegedly abusing Iraqi civilians were beamed around the world last week.

  • Democrats hammered Condoleezza Rice on Wednesday as misleading the American people on reasons for going to war with Iraq. But her approval by the Senate to be secretary of state was later secured.

  • Muslims around the world celebrated Eid Al-Adha on Thursday.
