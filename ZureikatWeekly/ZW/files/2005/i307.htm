Debate heats up as date for final issue approaches

Chief Correspondent Slams Editor's Decision, Suggests Biweekly Alternative

"Though we all appreciate Yacoub's initiative of a 'new' weekly, I feel that the current one should continue for another two to three years."

Hi Hazem,

Following our phone conversation yesterday [Friday, April 8], I am writing to urge you to reconsider.  As I said, several members of the family are shocked, including myself.  I think that's because we have always felt that the newsletter is indispensable, while others may have taken it for granted, assuming that no matter what happens, it will always be there.

I have received several phone calls from a number of Zureikats, some of whom have urged me to persuade you to change your mind, saying that, although they have written to you protesting against your decision, it seems that you are adamant to terminate the Weekly.

I believe that, contrary to what you have previously mentioned, we still "need" the ZW.  It is still a source binding us together, especially to those members abroad.

In light of the above, I am proposing that we change the journal from weekly to biweekly or tri-weekly, or even once a month.  By doing this, we would continue documenting our important and historic events for future generations to come.  Though we all appreciate Yacoub's initiative of a 'new' weekly, I feel that the current one should continue for another two to three years, during which time you can begin handing over the responsibility one step at a time to another team of 'managers' as you see fit.

As Editor in Chief, the decision is yours to make.  I just felt like sharing my thoughts with you, in hope that you will, at the very least, postpone the discontinuation of a valuable six-year initiative that has brought us joy, pride and inspiration, reminding us time and time again of how lucky we all are to have such a wonderful family.

Take care for now.

Alia

 

Letters from Other Zureikats Protesting the Editor in Chief's Decision

In an e-mail she sent to the Editor in Chief, Amal said, "I felt so sad that the ZW will stop soon.  Reem called...and she was [also] very sad...She told me that she waits to read it every week."  In her e-mail, Amal also asked the Editor in Chief to, at least, postpone his decision until he graduates.

 

[Sent before the Editor in Chief announced his decision] "Please never give up on us, because, believe me, the mere thinking of having the ZW even if I am late in reading or responding, gives me a great comfort and pleasure.  We tend to take good things in life for granted,  but isn't that part of family and love ingredients?"

 

"Our busy schedules, our chores and responsibilities are not an excuse to end The Zureikat Weekly.  On the contrary, we should hold on to The Zureikat Weekly as the only regular attempt for all of us to pause, think of and remember our family members, here and abroad."

 

In other developments

Yacoub Announces New Weekly to Start May 1

In an e-mail he sent to the family, Yacoub said that he will be releasing the first issue of his new weekly newsletter immediately after the current one is discontinued.  "I've decided that I will release a ZW with a starting date of May 1, 2005," he said in his e-mail.  Yacoub also asked for volunteers who wished to work as correspondents for his newsletter, which will most probably be sent out by e-mail every week.

International

  • Palestinian group Hamas fired mortar shells at Israeli targets in the Gaza Strip on Saturday, shaking a fragile peace in place since January after Israeli soldiers shot dead three Palestinian youths.  The violence came amid fears of a banned protest by Jewish extremists in Jerusalem's Old City.

  • Islamist Shiite Ibrahim Jaafari was named as Iraq's next prime minister on Thursday, moving the country a step closer to its first democratically elected government in more than 50 years.  Jaafari announced his own nomination shortly after Iraq's new president, Kurdish former guerrilla leader Jalal Talabani, was sworn into office in parliament, along with two deputies.
