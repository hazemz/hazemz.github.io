Hazem Becomes Member of Economics Honor Society

The membership will be officially conveyed in a ceremony on April 21

Hazem, who is currently in his third year at Macalester College in St. Paul, Minnesota, was invited to become a member of Omicron Delta Epsilon, an international honor society in economics.  The membership will be officially conveyed at Macalester's Economics Department's Honors Reception on April 21.  "Omicron Delta Epsilon is one of the world's largest academic honor societies.  The objectives of Omicron Delta Epsilon are recognition of scholastic attainment and the honoring of outstanding achievements in economics; the establishment of closer ties between students and faculty in economics within colleges and universities, and among colleges and universities" (Source: ODE website).  Hazem is currently working towards a Bachelor of Arts with a major in Economics and minors in Mathematics and Computer Science.

2 Snow Storms Hit Jordan

The storms were minor compared to those of previous years; however, schools were delayed on Wednesday and Sunday

Two snow storms hit Jordan last week, one on Tuesday, February 8, 2005, and the other on Friday and Saturday, February 11 and 12.  The two storms were not as strong as those of previous years.  Schools in Amman, however, were delayed for two hours on Wednesday, February 9, and Sunday, February 13.

Local

  • The Lower House endorsed the 2005 state budget on Wednesday with a wide majority after four days of speeches and analysis of the government's financial and administrative performance.

  • King Abdullah II on Sunday attended the opening here of the International Defense Exhibition and Conference (IDEX), the Middle East region's largest military show, in Dubai.

  • The Jordan Telecom Group (JTG) announced Saturday its “roadmap” for introducing new technologies designed to “change the way Jordanians live, and the way local businesses operate.” The roadmap, dubbed 'Vision 2005', includes the 'PC @ every home' initiative, a new ADSL solution, a Push-To-Talk service, which will be offered by MobileCom, a national roaming Wi-Fi (wireless) network, and a Wide Area Network Service (WINS) portfolio of outsourced and managed network solutions.

  • The Arab Bank on Monday decided to “gradually” close its New York branch, saying that the current operation environment in the US is not in line with the financial institution's strategy.

International

  • Palestinian President Mahmoud Abbas and Israeli Prime Minister Ariel Sharon on Tuesday declared a joint ceasefire at a landmark summit in Sharm El-Sheikh, Egypt, ushering in a new hope for restoring peace in Middle East. The summit was attended by Egyptian President Hosni Mubarak and King Abdullah II.

  • Israeli Prime Minister Ariel Sharon on Sunday ordered law enforcement agencies to crack down on Jewish extremists opposed to the planned withdrawal from the Gaza Strip after a series of threats and attacks on government ministers. Despite those concerns, Sharon's Cabinet approved a list of 500 Palestinian prisoners to be released in coming days in line with agreements reached at a Mideast summit last week. Palestinian leader Mahmoud Abbas, meanwhile, was to present a new Cabinet to his Fateh movement for approval Tuesday. Abbas was expected to appoint new interior, foreign and information ministers, but keep on many members of the current government, officials said. On Saturday, Abbas held key talks with leaders of the Islamic group Hamas Saturday in a bid to persuade them to sign up to a permanent ceasefire with Israel. On Thursday, Abbas had fired his top security commanders reacting forcefully to Hamas' rocket and mortar fire in Gaza in defiance of a fresh ceasefire with Israel.

  • Iraq's long-oppressed Shiite majority scored a resounding victory in the first vote since Saddam Hussein's downfall, setting the stage for the first Shiite government in an Arab state in 1,000 years. Although it mustered less than half of the votes, the counting method laid out by the country's electoral law means the main Shiite list backed by powerful spiritual leader Grand Ayatollah Ali Sistani is on track for an absolute majority in parliament. The Kurdish alliance was poised to become the country's second political force, while the list of incumbent Prime Minister Iyad Allawi was a distant third.

  • Saudi men went to the polls on Thursday in a landmark municipal election.

  • CNN's chief news executive Eason Jordan resigned Friday, saying he wanted to protect the network from controversy over remarks he made during the World Economic Forum last month about the death of journalists in Iraq. According to some participants — he denies it — Jordan told an audience at the gathering in Davos, Switzerland, that US forces had deliberately targeted journalists.
