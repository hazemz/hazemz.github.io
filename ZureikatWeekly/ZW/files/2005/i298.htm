Second Semester Begins

Zureikats in Jordan return to school

       

     

The second semester of the 2004-2005 school year began in Jordan on Sunday, February 6, 2005.  Sabine (Modern Schools) is currently in 11th grade; Dalia (Baptist School) is in 10th; Nadine (Modern Schools) and Yacoub S. (Jubilee School) are in 9th; Deena (Amman National School) is in 8th; Yacoub Z. (Modern Schools) is in 7th; and Salem (Amman National School) is in 5th.  Abla Z., English department coordinator at Amman National School, also returned to work on Sunday, while Abla N., director of the Jubilee School, is expected to resume work upon her return from the US next week.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish all Zureikat school students, as well as Abla and Abla, all the best in the new semester.

 

Lunch at Houston's to Celebrate Last 'Free' Day before School

Marwan, Hala, Sabine, Dalia, Nadine, Yacoub, Deena, Yacoub and Salem had lunch at Houston's restaurant on Saturday, February 5, 2005.  "This lunch was in celebration of our last day as free children, due to the fact that tomorrow [Sunday] is school," Dalia said in her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief.  "The food was (bista7)and very, very delicious.  We had an exquisite lunch there.  We look forward for another invitation to Houston's by another kind person like uncle Marwan!"

Karadshehs Spend 3 Days at the Dead Sea

Hala, Naief, Dalia, Deena and Salem spent three days at the Dead Sea.  They left Amman on Wednesday, February 2, 2005, and returned on Friday, February 4.  "It was a great trip," Dalia said in her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief.  "We stayed at the Marriott Hotel with my cousins.  When we arrived, we just hanged around the pool and swam there.  On Thursday, we swam in the Dead Sea...and put some mud all over our body."

Local

  • HM Queen Rania gave birth last Sunday to HRH Prince Hashem.

  • Syrian President Bashar Assad visited Amman and held talks with the King.

  • Lower House deputies criticized increased reliance on foreign aid in the
    2005 budget and the government's alleged “endowments” to its advocates,
    particularly in the media.

  • Canadian Foreign Affairs Minister Pierre Pettigrew arrived in Amman Saturday on the first leg of a one-week Middle East tour.

  • A Royal Decree was issued on Monday appointing members of the Royal Committee tasked with examining and presenting proposals to implement the Royal vision included in His Majesty King Abdullah's address to the nation last week. The appointed members are: Senate President Zeid Rifai, Senator Fayez Tarawneh, MP Abdur-Ra'uf S. Rawabdeh, Lower House Speaker Abdul Hadi Majali, King's Special Adviser Aqel Biltaji, former Deputy Prime Minister Marwan Hmoud, former Interior Minister Rajai Dajani, former Deputy Prime Minister Awad Khleifat, MP Mamdouh Abbadi, former Justice Minister Hisham Tal, former Interior Minister Nayef Qadi, and Jordan River Foundation Director General Maha Khatib.

International

  • Visiting US Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice on Sunday urged Israel to
    take "hard decisions" for peace as she kicked off a renewed diplomatic push
    by Washington in the Middle East. Meanwhile, Israel on Thursday approved a troop pullback from West Bank cities and the release of 900 Palestinian prisoners, measures crucial to the success of a summit with the Palestinians in Egypt next week. Both sides said they hoped to declare a formal halt to violence at the talks in the Red Sea resort of Sharm El Sheikh next Tuesday, which will mark a dramatic return to peace-making after more than four years of bloodshed. The summit will be attended by Abbas, Sharon, Mubarak and HM King Abdullah II.

  • The spiritual leader of Iraqi Shiites, who are set to dominate the new government, on Sunday demanded a major say for Islam in lawmaking as the abduction of four Egyptians heightened fears for foreigners.

  • Kuwait's parliament Tuesday passed a law giving security forces wider powers to search for and seize illegal firearms following a series of deadly clashes with militants apparently linked to Saudi extremists.
