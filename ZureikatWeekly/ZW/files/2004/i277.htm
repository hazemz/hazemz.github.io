Ayman Returns to the US

This is Ayman's final year at Tufts University

After postponing his departure for three days, Ayman returned to Boston on Tuesday, September 7, 2004, after spending part of the summer in Amman.  What should be mentioned is that this is Ayman's final year at Tufts University, where he is currently studying Mechanical Engineering.

Marwan and Abla Return from Sharm El-Sheikh

 

Marwan and Abla, along with Dr. Yousef and Mrs. Shadia Nusseir, left to Sharm El-Sheikh on Thursday, September 9, 2004.  They returned to Amman on Sunday, September 12.  Sunday was a holiday in Jordan and the Muslim world on the occasion of Eid Al-Isra' wa Al-Mi'raj.

Ziad Visits Alexandria, Returns Home

Ziad left to Alexandria, Egypt on Wednesday, September 8, 2004, to attend a meeting.  He returned to Amman on Friday, September 10.

Local News:

  • HRH Prince Ali and HRH Princess Rym, daughter of UN diplomat Lakhdar Brahimi, were wed
    in a small ceremony in Amman on Tuesday.

  • The altercation between the government and members of the Muslim Brotherhood Movement
    was defused Sunday after the group pledged its clerics will abide by the Preaching and
    Guidance Law, both sides said. Earlier last week, the Interior Ministry had issued
    subpoenas for 11 clerics, who, it said, violated the law by conducting unauthorized
    sermons. Nine were detained and later released.

  • Three Jordanian truck drivers kidnapped in Iraq were released Monday.

World News:

  • Almost 100 Iraqis were killed in violence around the country last week.

  • Dozens of international aid agencies considered quitting Iraq on Wednesday following the
    abduction of two Italian women, and as the US military death toll rose above 1,000.

  • The Lebanese parliament bowed to Syrian pressure and voted in favor of changing the
    constitution to allow Emile Lahoud to remain as Lebanon's President for three more
    years. Later last week, Lebanese Prime Minister Rafik Hariri announced Thursday that
    his government would resign this month, bringing to ahead a political crisis over Syrian
    domination of the country's domestic affairs. His announcement came amid mounting
    tensions between Lebanon's political masters in Damascus and the United States.
