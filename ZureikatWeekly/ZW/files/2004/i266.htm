Big news from Washington!

Dana and Ammar Expecting a Baby by the End of January

The first Zureikat great-grandchild is on the way!  Dana is now a couple of months pregnant; she and Ammar are expecting a baby by the end of January.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Dana and Ammar and the whole family on the great news.  Dana and Ammar got married on August 2, 2003 and currently live in Washington, DC.

Amal and Ziad Return from China

 

Amal and Ziad returned home on Wednesday, June 23, 2004, after a ten-day visit to China in which Ziad traveled with some members from the Jordanian Senate.

Tarek in London for Weeklong Vacation

Tarek left to London on Thursday, June 24, 2004, for a weeklong vacation, during which he will attend an event organized by his previous employers, the Nashville, Tennessee-based, Southwestern Company.  Tarek is expected to return home on Thursday, July 1.

Bana Returns from Beirut after Business Trip

Bana left to Beirut on Tuesday, June 22, 2004, on a business trip.  She returned home on Friday, June 25.

Yacoub Starts Orientation at Jubilee School

After being accepted to enroll at the Jubilee School starting the next academic year, Yacoub S. started a six-week orientation program at the school on Sunday, June 27, 2004.

Zureikats Celebrate Hazem's Birthday

Zureikats celebrated Hazem's birthday on Wednesday, June 23, 2004.

Comari Leaves Jordan after Years of Service with Abu Harran and Umm Harran

After spending many years with Abu Harran and the late Umm Harran, Comari returned to Sri Lanka for good on Friday, June 25, 2004.  Ayyuni, an Indonesian who previously worked for the Karadshehs, will now be working at Abu Harran's.  Our thanks and best wishes go out to Comari.

Local News:

  • Most Tawjihi exams were postponed for one week after the Ministry of Education and the General Intelligence Department's Anti-Corruption Unit revealed evidence that several Tawjihi exams were leaked.  Exams that the students had already sat for were also canceled and are to be repeated in the coming weeks.  In a press conference on Tuesday, Minister of Education, Khaled Toukan, and the Director of the Anti-Corruption Unit, Colonel Musleh Kayed said that those responsible for the leaks include the acting head of Amman's Fourth Education Directorate, an employee at the Directorate, a guard, and some owners of private schools (such as Faris Hawwari, owner of the Jawharat Al-Orouba School).  Those responsible for leaking and selling the questions were arrested and placed in the Juweideh Correctional Facility.  Meanwhile, the question of whether or not the Minister of Education will resign in light of this scandal is still unanswered.
