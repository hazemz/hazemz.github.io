The Eid Al-Adha holiday begins

Marwan and Abla Leave to Luxor

 

Marwan and Abla left to Luxor, Egypt on Saturday, January 31, 2004, to spend four days on a cruise that will take them to Aswan (further south).  This comes as the Eid Al-Adha holiday begins in many Arab and Muslim countries around the world.  The holiday in Jordan started on Saturday, January 31, and will end on Wednesday, February 4.  Marwan and Abla are expected to return to Amman on Wednesday.

Zureikats Celebrate Reema's Birthday

Breakfast and afternoon coffee at Blue Fig; dinner at La Maison Verte

Zureikats celebrated Reema's birthday on Wednesday, January 28, 2004.  In an e-mail she sent to the Editor in Chief, Reema said, "I am writing to tell how lovely this birthday was.  Abla N. was kind to initiate and host a breakfast on the occasion in my favorite cafe, Blue Fig.  She invited Abla Z. and Dian too, and we had a lovely chat and delicious manageesh and cheese pastries.  It was different and everybody enjoyed it.  I wished everybody was there.  Funny enough, my classmates invited me for coffee to the same place in the afternoon!!  Other members of the family either called or showed up.  The subject of my age was not discussed!"  Also celebrating this occasion, Harran, Reema, Alia, and Reema's sister (Rita Ayoub) and her daughter had dinner at La Maison Verte restaurant.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Reema a very happy birthday and all the best.

  • In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Dalia updated us with the latest from Maryland.  "The weather is still bad," she said.  "We have tons and tons of snow.  We also have to walk very carefully so as not to fall down.  The second semester started on January 28, and for all of us here our grades were fine, between A's and B's.  As for me, the second semester is quite a change, again as if it is a new beginning for me.  I have the same subjects, with different teachers and different students.  Our French is getting better and better.  Salem will be joining the science club at school which is part of the after-school activities.  As for our social life, my parents got to know many French couples here, and we were invited last weekend to one of them.  We had nice dinner with their kids.  We have American friends now also.  We invited some of them yesterday night [Saturday, January 31], and they appreciated my mother's cooking.  My mother also started her classes.  She managed to have all her activities in the morning so that she will be home in the afternoon with us.  She is happy because we help her a lot in housework so that she can put more time for her fellowship."
