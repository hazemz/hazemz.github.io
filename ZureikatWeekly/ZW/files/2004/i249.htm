February 29, 2004

Dana Transferred to TV Station

Dana, who has been working with Washington, DC-based Radio Sawa since November 2002, was transferred to the newly-established TV channel 'Al-Hurra' a few weeks ago.  The channel, which is the first Arabic-speaking, US-run TV channel, started broadcasting on Saturday, February 14, 2004.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Dana on her new job, wishing her all the best.

Bana Promoted to Senior Officer

Bana, who is currently working at Fastlink, Jordan's leading mobile telephone service provider, has been promoted to Senior Officer.  What should be mentioned is that Bana started working for Fastlink as a Pricing Officer in May 2002.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Bana on her promotion, wishing her all the best.

Dag Traveling Around Europe to Market DagTel

In the past four weeks, Dag has been in Germany, Austria, the UK, Estonia, and Italy. He will also travel to Switzerland later this week.

Dag has been traveling around Europe throughout the past month to market his company 'DagTel'.  In an e-mail he sent to the Editor in Chief, Dag said, "I have been in Hamburg and Düsseldorf in Germany, Vienna and Salzburg in Austria, UK, and Tallinn in Estonia.  Tomorrow [Tuesday, February 24], I will fly to Milan, Italy to meet Italians biggest Operator with 30,000,000 customers.  Later this week, I will visit Lugano in Switzerland.  My business is going well.  A couple of weeks ago, we signed a big contract with my UK distributors.  This means they will sell, market, and promote DagTel products in UK, Ireland, and Spain, soon, also, in Portugal, France, and South Africa.  I will attend the world biggest Telecom Fair at CeBit in Hanover, Germany mid-March.  I hope your guys are fine.  Give my love to everybody."

Spring Weather in Amman

Abu Harran spends Friday in Umm Al-Amad

 

 

Left: Bana--the new Senior Officer--doing some work. \ Right: Marwan and Bashar preparing lunch.

 

Left: Abu Harran walking in the parking area. \ Right: Marwan looking at the trees.

Less than two weeks after a snow storm that closed down schools, universities, and businesses around the country, Zureikats in Jordan enjoyed spring weather last weekend with temperatures exceeding 25 degrees Celsius.  Abu Harran, Marwan, Abla, Tarek, Bashar, and Bana took this opportunity to spend Friday, February 27, 2004, in Umm Al-Amad.  Abu Harran, after a long winter, resumed his usual activity of walking around the parking area and the entrance of Marwan and Abla's farm.

Dian Recovers from Flu

Dian recovered from a flu from which she had been suffering for more than a week.

Dalia Presents the ZW to her Class

Hala: Dalia's teacher said that the ZW is a "unique thing to have in the family"

Dalia, who is spending this academic year with her family in the US, presented The Zureikat Weekly to her English class last week as part of a project on family relationships.  In an e-mail she sent to the Editor in Chief, Hala said, "Everyone was surprised to know that we have a weekly magazine, so was the teacher, who added that this a unique thing to have in the family.  Dalia explained everything about The Zureikat Weekly [and] its start.  [She] talked about Teta Umm Harran, and so on.  She had a great applause at the end.  The teacher said that it was the best presentation she ever had in her class.  So bravo Hazem; continue the good work!"

Jordanian Senate Discusses Budget

Part of Ziad's speech appeared in newspapers Friday

From Al-Ra'i Daily Newspaper - Friday, February 27, 2004

The Jordanian Upper House of Parliament (Senate) convened on Thursday, February 26, 2004, to discuss the government's proposed budget plan for the 2004 fiscal year.  Part of Ziad's speech in this session was published in several newspapers the next day.  What should be mentioned is that the Senate unanimously passed the proposed budget plan at the end of Thursday's session.

Zureikats Celebrate Reem's Birthday

Zureikats celebrated Reem's birthday on Wednesday, February 25, 2004.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Reem a very happy birthday and all the best.

  • Abla, Abla, Reema, and Ammar's mother (Mrs. Ayda Daoud) attended a dinner on Saturday, February 28, 2004, hosted by the Ahliyyah School for Girls' Alumni club.  The dinner was held at the Grand Hyatt Hotel in Amman.

   

  • Hala attended a talk about democracy in Jordan at the Woodrow Wilson Center on Friday, February 27, 2004.  The talk was given by a University of Maryland professor who is a specialist on Jordanian affairs.  Hala and Naief are also expected to attend a talk by Lynne Cheney (wife of US Vice President Dick Cheney) on Sunday, February 29.

 

  • Samer, who is currently in his last year at Tulane University, was visited by two of his friends last weekend (one who studies in St. Paul, Minnesota and the other in Boston, Massachusetts) as New Orleans celebrated the Mardi Gras carnival.

  • US Democratic Presidential hopeful John Kerry visited Macalester College in St. Paul, Minnesota (where Hazem is currently in his second year) on Wednesday, February 25, 2004.  Hazem, however, could not attend Kerry's rally due to limited space.
