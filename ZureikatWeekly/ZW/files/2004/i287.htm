Ayman Becomes Member of Engineering Honor Society

Tau Beta Pi is the largest honor society for engineers in the US

Ayman, who is currently is his last year at Tufts University in Boston, Massachusetts, was nominated and selected to be a member of Tau Beta Pi, the largest honor society for engineers in the US.  An initiation ceremony was held for the new members on Wednesday, November 17, 2004.  Tau Beta Pi is the America's second-oldest society, founded at Lehigh University in 1885 to recognize students of distinguished scholarship and exemplary character.  There are now collegiate chapters at 228 US colleges and universities.  The primary criterion for election is the student's rank in the Engineering School; senior electees are among the top 1/5 of their class.  Being elected to membership in the national engineering honor society is a great honor, not only because of the high standards for scholarship and character, but also because these outstanding qualities have been recognized in the student by other fellow engineering students.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Ayman on this great honor, wishing him all the best.
