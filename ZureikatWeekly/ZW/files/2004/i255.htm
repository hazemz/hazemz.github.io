Near the Statue of Liberty

Karadshehs Return from New York

 

At the Empire State Building

 

Left: At Ground Zero, the site of the September 11 attacks / Right: At the United Nations headquarters

Karadshehs returned to Maryland on Wednesday, April 7, 2004, after spending three days in New York City.  The family had left to New York on Sunday, April 4.  What should be mentioned is that Dalia, Deena, and Salem are currently on vacation.  Their spring break started on Saturday, April 3, and will end on Monday, April 12.  Following is Dalia's weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief:

"Last weekend (the one before we went to NY), we went to Washington to see the "Cherry Blossoms" festival, and also it was a beautiful day for flying kites.  We bought kites for Deena and Salem.  There were a lot of people near the Washington Monument.  The Cherry Blossoms were so beautiful!  Next Sunday, we drove to New York!  On Monday, my mom went with her Humphrey fellows group to visit the Rockefeller Center and the NBC studios.  They also visited the Committee to Protect Journalists and ate lunch there.  While we went and ate lunch in a restaurant and met my mom there.  Mom's friend also joined us.  After lunch, we went to the Empire State Building.  We got up to the highest floor (86th).  Later in the afternoon, we had a nice walk till Broadway.  On Tuesday, my mom went to the ABC newsroom (World News Tonight) and the New York Times, while we took the tour bus from the hotel early in the morning.  We visited a lot of places!  First, we went to Central Park, then the Rockefeller Center.  We saw people ice skating there.  In front of the Rockefeller Center was an old beautiful Catholic church.  After the church, we went to Chinatown, and we saw the apartment building were they shot the comedy series "Friends".  We saw a courthouse and another park were they shot movies in too.  After the break, we saw the Twin Towers Memorial.  After that, we took the boat to Ellis Island (the entry point of all European immigrants).  We stayed there for while, and then we went by boat to the Statue of Liberty.  On Wednesday, my mom went to the United Nations.  We joined her there, and visited the General Assembly.  We sat in the place where the Jordanian delegation sits.  We had lunch at the cafeteria of the UN.  We walked on to Broadway again, and we saw the MTV building, where there were famous singers, and it was surrounded by a lot of people who wanted to meet them.  And also in Times Square, there were some preparations for a shooting a film.  We left New York at 7pm with the hope that my dad will take us again there because we just loved it!  It is really a crazy city!  We would like to wish Jiddo and all family members a very happy Easter.  We miss them all."
