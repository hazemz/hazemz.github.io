Reema and Alia Honored at NPAC Launching

The National Plan of Action for Children (NPAC) was launched on Monday in a ceremony patronized by the King and Queen

 

Reema and Alia were honored at the launching of the National Plan of Action for Children (2004-2013).  The event was held on Monday, October 25, 2004, under the patronage of Their Majesties King Abdullah II and Queen Rania.  The NPAC was developed by the National Council for Family Affairs (NCFA), where Alia currently works, the Ministry of Planning and International Cooperation, and UNICEF.  Participants in the development of the plan (including Reema and Alia) were presented with a shield by the Minister of Planning and International Cooperation Bassem Awadallah.  All line ministries were present, in addition to members of the parliament and representatives working in the field of family affairs.  Prime Minister, Faisal Fayez, gave a speech during the event emphasizing the government's commitment to implementing the NPAC.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Reema and Alia on this recognition, wishing them all the best.

Local News:

  • Nine British tourists and two citizens were killed Thursday after their bus overturned and crashed into a pick-up truck on the Amman-Aqaba highway, a senior official said. The truck's driver was also killed.

  • Ten years ago last Tuesday Jordan made the historic move of signing a peace treaty with Israel. But of the dreams and hopes that followed the signing, few have become a reality and the “warm peace” that the late King Hussein and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhak Rabin sealed in 1994 has grown “cold,” politicians and analysts say. On Tuesday, hundreds of unionists and opposition leaders protested in Amman urging the government to tear up the peace treaty.

  • HRH Prince Charles, the Prince of Wales, arrived Tuesday in Amman on a four-day working visit on the last leg of a tour that has already taken him to Turkey and Italy.

  • A few days after its first reshuffle, the government of Prime Minister Faisal Fayez held a retreat at the Dead Sea.

  • Jordan's total population at the start of this month stood at 5.29 million, according to the initial results of the General Population Census, conducted earlier this month by the Department of Statistics.
