Alia's TV appearance postponed for one week

Alia Visits Disabled Students at Hashemite University

As part of her work at the National Council for Family Affairs and given her personal experience at university, Alia visited disabled students the Hashemite University in the north of Jordan on Tuesday, July 27, 2004, to listen to their problems and assess the current facilities available to them.  A number of recommendations will be presented by Alia and the Council to the University's President and Dean of Students.  On another note, Alia's appearance on Jordan Television has been postponed from Wednesday, July 28, to Wednesday, August 4, due to JTV's broadcasting of events at the Jerash Festival for Culture and Arts.

Please note that the program in which Alia will be appearing is called 'Laha wa Lahu' (For Her and for Him), and not 'Lahu wa Laha' (as mentioned in the previous issue).  The program will air on Wednesday, August 4, at 7pm Jordan summer time (4pm GMT).

Local News:

  • Jordan's national football team was eliminated from the quarter-finals of the 13th Asian Cup in China, but not after putting up a big fight in their match against Japan.  Playing their best game to date, Jordan was first to score and had the better overall ball possession, most shots and set the pace during most of the match against the defending champions.  However, the match ended 1-1 and overtime did not break the tie.  A penalty shoot-out would have the final say.  King Abdullah II attended the match, which was held at the Chongqing stadium.

  • Two of the four Jordanian truck drivers kidnapped by an Iraqi armed group late last week contacted their families on Saturday and informed them that their release is expected soon.  Two other Jordanians were also kidnapped by militants in Iraq last Monday.

  • The first issue of a new daily newspaper, Al-Ghad (www.alghad.jo), was released on Sunday, August 1.

World News:

  • More than 120 people died in a series of bombings and attacks in Iraq Wednesday.

  • The Democratic Party's National Convention ended after the party officially announced John F. Kerry as its nominee for the November Presidential election.  Kerry, his running mate John Edwards, as well as other key Democrats spoke at the convention in Boston.

  • Palestinian Prime Minister Ahmed Qureia ended a paralyzing crisis in the ranks of the Palestinian leadership Tuesday by burying the hatchet with Yasser Arafat and agreeing to stay on as premier.  Qureia's announcement, which came 10 days after he tendered his resignation, followed extensive mediation efforts by MPs who said they had secured an agreement by Arafat to implement reforms and crack down on corruption.

  • The UN Security Council gave the Sudanese government a 30-day ultimatum to crack down on militia in the western region of Darfur.
