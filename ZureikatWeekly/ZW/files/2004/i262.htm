Alia Writes from New York

"There are around 110 countries participating in this convention...It is a unique experience."

Alia, who, along with Reema, is currently attending the United Nations International Convention on the Protection and Promotion of the Rights and Dignity of Persons With Disabilities in New York City sent the following e-mail to the Editor in Chief:

My day starts at 8am and ends at 8pm.  Our official meetings take place from 10am to 6pm at the UN headquarters, where delegates deliberate 3 to 4 articles a day (there are 25 draft articles in the current document).  The rest is all informal meetings between the various delegations and the NGOs participating (e.g. Arab, Asian and Latin American caucuses).  There are around 110 countries participating in this convention, along with 250 NGO representatives.  Within those, 40% are people with various disabilities.

It is a unique experience.  This is a vital convention, which is considered to be the 'missing piece' of the human rights puzzle, according to human rights activists.

Mum has been very obedient for a change, following my rules and regulations!!  That is, after my constant threats to have her deported back to Jordan!!  Seriously, though, she has been truly amazing as the Jordanian delegation's lobbyist.  Everyone loves her!  A great weapon to have at a time like this!!

Well, this is all I have for now.  Give my love to all back home.

Ziad Buys his First Laptop from New Orleans

"Looking at so many laptops, each with its own specification, was confusing to me"

Ziad sent the following e-mail to the Editor in Chief a few days after returning from New Orleans, where he attended Samer's graduation and bought his first laptop:

To ZW Editor in Chief,

I bought my first laptop in the USA

It was an exciting moment for me to take advice from Samer, and subsequently I took advantage of Hazem being with us in New Orleans, to get his expertise on the subject.  Hazem, Ayman and myself went to buy the laptop in the West Side of New Orleans, where we had to take a taxi and cross the river.  Looking at so many laptops, each with its own specification, was confusing to me, but to Hazem was a straightforward problem, and out of these many, we picked the Toshiba model.

During that morning and afterwards, when we went to have lunch, I was close to Hazem.  And during the next few days I could not but admire this young man for his passion, courteous behavior and respect to others.  And above all his sense of humor.

I am really proud to know Hazem.  GOD BLESS YOU.

Yours sincerely,
Ziad Sharaiha

Local News:

  • Jordan celebrated the wedding of HRH Crown Prince Hamzah and HRH Princess Noor Hamzah.  A motorcade carrying the two princes drove through the streets of Amman on Thursday, May 27, 2004.  This was followed by a reception at the Zahran Palace near the third circle.  On Friday, May 28, the King and Queen hosted a dinner party at the royal palace in Aqaba.  The events were attended by a number of foreign dignitaries and royals including the Sultan of Brunei, the King and Queen of Sweden, the Queen of Spain, and the recently-wed Spanish royal couple.

World News:

  • Six people were killed in clashes between protestors and police in Beirut on Friday.  This came after several organizations in Lebanon called for a day of strike to protest the increasing oil prices and the government's policies.

  • A hostage standoff in Khobar, Saudi Arabia ended on Sunday with a number of hostages killed during the clashes between police forces and apparent members of Al-Qaeda.  The standoff started after seventeen people were shot dead on Saturday near the headquarters of a foreign oil company Khobar.  The dead included Saudis and foreign nationals.

  • Three Palestinians were killed after an Israeli helicopter strike in Gaza early Sunday morning. The dead include members of Hamas in the Gaza Strip.

  • The Sudanese government and rebel Sudan People's Liberation Army (SPLA) signed an accord in Kenya on Wednesday on how to share power and manage three disputed areas, lifting key hurdles to a final deal to end 21 years of war in the south.  A formal peace deal is expected later this year.
