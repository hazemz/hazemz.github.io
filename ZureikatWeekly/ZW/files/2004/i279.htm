Harran and Reema Leave to DC

Amer and Leanne to meet with them in Washington next weekend

 

Harran and Reema left to the US on Sunday, September 26, 2004, for an eight-day visit to Washington, DC, where Harran will be attending a conference.  Amer and Leanne are expected to meet Harran and Reema in DC next weekend.  They will be leaving Chicago on Friday, October 1, and returning on Sunday, October 3.

Amal and Ziad Leave to Prague, London

 

Amal and Ziad left to Prague, the Czech Republic on Saturday, September 25, 2004, where Ziad will be attending a medical meeting.  Both will be leaving Prague for London on Wednesday, September 29, and returning to Amman on Saturday, October 2.

The Zureikat Weekly Removed from the Internet

On Thursday, September 23, 2004, the Editor in Chief deleted all issues of The Zureikat Weekly from the internet.  This came after a number of ZW issues became searchable on internet search engines, allowing anyone to get access to these issues.  The Editor in Chief sent an e-mail to the family after deleting the issues explaining what had happened.  In his e-mail, the Editor in Chief also said that, until he finds a strong password-protection system for the newsletter, ZW issues would be sent via e-mail.

Zureikats Celebrate Rana's Birthday

Zureikats celebrated Rana's 25th birthday on Sunday, September 26, 2004.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Rana a very happy birthday and all the best.

Local News:

  • Foreign Minister Marwan Muasher and his Israeli counterpart Silvan Shalom on Wednesday held talks on the Jordanian prisoners in the Jewish state and bilateral relations. Attending the 59th Session of the UN General Assembly at the United Nations, Muasher told Shalom that an Israeli withdrawal from the Gaza Strip should be in line with the roadmap and the first step towards a complete pullout of the Palestinian territories, paving the way for establishing an independent state.

  • Minister of State and Government Spokesperson Asma Khader on Saturday said Jordan was “deeply concerned over future dangerous impacts” of Israel's ageing nuclear reactor Dimona. But Khader, who confirmed that a UN team will visit Jordan “within a few weeks” to conduct a survey on radiation levels upon the government's request, reiterated that the Jordan Nuclear Energy Commission's round-the-clock tests currently shows no abnormal radioactivity.

  • Prosecution witnesses testify in Tawjihi leaks case.

  • Officials are investigating the death of a 55-year-old blind patient at the University of Jordan Hospital on Tuesday, official sources said. Hospital staff found the victim, who was not identified by officials, with a fruit knife lodged in his chest at around 6:00am, the source said.

  • In front of an audience of about 3,500, Fairouz made her fourth performance in Amman at the Amman University Arena on Wednesday. The Lebanese artist had arrived in Jordan on Monday and was greeted by international, regional and local photojournalists.

  • HRH Princess Basma's daughter, Farah Daghestani, got married on Thursday to Saud Abdul Aziz Suleiman from Saudi Arabia.

World News:

  • Two Israeli border policemen were killed and 17 other people wounded Wednesday when a teenage Palestinian suicide bomber blew herself up at a police checkpoint next to several bus stops in Jerusalem.

  • US aircraft pounded the insurgent enclave of Fallujah killing several Iraqis in the past week.

  • Israeli army bulldozers razed 35 homes in a Palestinian refugee camp Saturday, a UN official said, a day after a resident of a nearby Jewish settlement was killed by mortar fire from the area.

  • Two US hostages kidnapped in Iraq were beheaded. The fate of a third UK hostage is still unknown.

  • Lebanon said on Thursday Syria would redeploy some 3,000 troops from the outskirts of Beirut east towards the Syrian-Lebanon border, in an apparent bid to ease US-led pressure over its influence on Lebanon.

  • Iraqi Prime Minister Iyad Allawi told a joint session of the US Congress on Thursday that elections would be held in Iraq as scheduled in January, and that despite a tough struggle "we are succeeding."  Allawi also addressed the UN General Assembly earlier in the week.

  • Iran said it will react “most severely” to any Israeli action against its nuclear facilities, issuing the warning after Israel said the United States was selling it 500 bunker buster bombs.

  • US President George W. Bush on Monday removed most economic sanctions on Libya, clearing the way for huge compensation payments to the families of victims of the 1988 Lockerbie bombing.
