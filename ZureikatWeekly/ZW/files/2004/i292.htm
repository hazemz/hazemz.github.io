Family Observes First Christmas after Abu Harran

Zureikats from Amman and Rabba visited Marwan's house on Christmas Day

 

 

Zureikats observed the first Christmas after the passing of the late Abu Harran in October.  Relatives from Amman and Rabba gathered at Marwan's house on Christmas Day, where they offered their Christmas greetings and condolences to the family.  Later that day, the family had lunch at Marwan's.

Christmas eve at Harran's

On the previous night, the family gathered at Harran's for the annual Christmas Eve gathering.  During the gathering, Marwan gave his annual speech, which focused on the importance of tolerance and acceptance.  Marwan said that each person has his own kingdom in which he lives, and that people should accept and respect each other's kingdom's.  He said that, even in a family, being brothers and sisters does not necessarily mean having common kingdoms but rather having mutual love and respect for one another.  Marwan, as well as other family members, also welcomed Rana, who was attending her first Zureikat Christmas eve gathering.  What should be mentioned is that this year's gathering did not include singing Christmas carols nor a visit by Santa Claus.

World News:

  • One of the most powerful earthquakes in history hit Asia on Sunday, unleashing a tsunami which devastated coastal areas of Sri Lanka, India, Indonesia and tourist isles in Thailand, killing more than 11,300 people.

  • Palestinians on Thursday voted in their first municipal election in almost 30 years. The first stage of the municipal elections (which was held on Thursday) included several towns in the West Bank. Election results have confirmed that Hamas made significant inroads against the mainstream Fatah movement. Palestinian Authority officials said that Fatah still held a majority of council seats, but that Hamas had scored victories at least eight municipalities.

  • Saudi Arabia has recalled its envoy to Libya over an alleged plot to assassinate Crown Prince Abdullah.

  • British Prime Minister Tony Blair launched his quest Wednesday to bring peace to the Middle East, unveiling plans for a conference to prepare the Palestinians for statehood, during a landmark West Bank visit. After paying his respects at the graveside of Yasser Arafat in Ramallah, Blair told his likely successor as Palestinian president, Mahmoud Abbas, that he wanted to help turn the vision of an independent state into reality by hosting a conference in London in March.

  • Secretary General Kofi Annan on Tuesday admitted allegations about the UN's oil-for-food program in Iraq had “cast a shadow” over the United Nations but played down the question of resigning.
