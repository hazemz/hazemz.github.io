Zureikats Mark 40 Days Since the Passing of Abu Harran

The family gathered for lunch at Marwan's on Thursday

Zureikats marked forty days since the passing of the late Abu Harran last month.  On this occasion, the family gathered at Marwan and Abla's for lunch on Thursday, November 11, 2004.

Hala Turns 50

Zureikats celebrated Hala's 50th birthday on Tuesday, November 9, 2004.  On this occasion, the family gathered at Hala and Naief's on Tuesday.  "It was a nice surprise for my mother," Dalia said in her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief.  "The whole family gathered at our house."  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Hala a very happy 50th birthday and all the best.

Talal in Spain for 5 Weeks

Talal will be taking an intensive Spanish course in Valencia

After finishing his final exams, Talal, who is currently studying at the St. George's Hospital Medical School in London, left to Valencia, Spain on Sunday, November 14, 2004, where he will be taking an intensive, five-week Spanish course.  "They sorted me out completely with accommodation and everything," he said in a message to the Editor in Chief.

Amal Leaves to Orlando, Houston

Amal left to Orlando, Florida on Thursday, November 11, 2004, to attend an annual meeting regarding periodontal diseases and implants.  Amal will then visit Houston on Wednesday, November 17, for a medical checkup and will return to Amman the following Wednesday, November 24.  Samer and Ayman are both expected to meet with Amal during her visit to the US.

The Eid El-Fitr holiday begins

Harran, Reema, Marwan, Abla in Sharm

     

Harran, Reema, Marwan, and Abla left to Sharm El-Sheikh, Egypt on Friday, November 12, 2004.  They are expected to return to Amman on Tuesday, November 16.  This trip comes as Jordan and the Muslim world celebrate Eid El-Fitr with a four-day holiday.

 

Hassan and the Family Spend a Day at the Dead Sea

       

Also on the Eid holiday, Hassan, Dian, Sabine, Nadine, and Yacoub left to the Dead Sea on Saturday, November 13, 2004.  They returned to Amman the next day.

Zureikats celebrated Leanne's birthday on Monday, November 8, 2004.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Leanne a very happy birthday and all the best.

A Poem by Nadine
dedicated to Jiddo Abu Harran

 It might be hard to understand,
That you had lost someone,
Who is now buried under the sand,
After he considered you a son.

He gave me a push for everything,
In happiness, and in sadness,
So I didn't need to think,
Of the consequence.

Although he is gone,
I still feel it is unfair,
You might think now,
That it is whispers in the air.

I had lost my first one,
So I tried to protect the other,
But in the end,
We will still stay for each other.

I should be happy now,
Because now Jiddo is with Teta Shafi'a
But I just wonder how,
That pain would leave,
And let me in relief.
After all, he will stay in my heart,
And I will never leave it apart.

Local News:

  • Jordan declared three days of public mourning after the death of Yasser Arafat. Thursday was also declared a public holiday.

  • Jordan marked the late King Hussein's birthday on Saturday.

  • In His Majesty's presence, Chief Islamic Justice Sheikh Izzeddine Khatib Tamimi delivered on Tuesday the “Amman Message” in defense of Islam as a faith of moderation and tolerance that rejects extremism and terrorism and believes in dialogue. Jordan called for holding an Islamic conference next year to discuss and adopt the concepts included in the Amman Message.

  • Three Jordanian truck drivers held hostage in Iraq since last week have been freed.

  • Two pilots were killed Tuesday in a plane crash in Irbid.

World News:

  • Yasser Arafat, the resistance icon turned Nobel Peace Prize winner who ended up isolated and locked in renewed conflict with Israel, died on Thursday, his dream of a Palestinian state unfulfilled. He was 75. A service was held for Arafat on Thursday before his body left Paris. A military funeral was held in Cairo on Friday, after which Arafat was buried in Ramallah. As stated in the Palestinian Constitution, Palestinian Legislative Council Chair Rawhi Fattouh was sworn in as the new President of the Palestinian National Authority. Former Prime Minister Mahmoud Abbas was elected as the new leader of Fatah, while Ahmad Qureia retained his post as Prime Minister. Elections for a new leader of the PNA are expected to be held on January 9.

  • Iraq said Saturday that the battle to retake the rebel bastion of Fallujah was over, with more than 1,000 fighters killed, but that the country's most wanted man, Abu Mussab Zarqawi, had fled.

  • Iraqi Prime Minister Iyad Allawi's 75-year-old cousin Ghazi Allawi, the cousin's wife and their daughter-in-law were seized by gunmen near their home in Baghdad on Tuesday, an Allawi spokesman said.
