Ammar Appointed as Member of Prestigious Business Society

"The membership...is the highest recognition a business student anywhere in the world can receive"

Ammar, who will be graduating from Georgetown University with an MBA in April, has been appointed as a member of Beta Gamma Sigma, an honor society serving several business programs around the world.  "The membership in Beta Gamma Sigma is the highest recognition a business student anywhere in the world can receive," said Dana, quoting the society's magazine in a phone call with the Editor in Chief.  Dana added that an 'installation ceremony' officially appointing Ammar in the society will be held on May 6.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Ammar on being appointed in this prestigious society, wishing him all the best.

Sylvia Arrives in Cameroon

Dr. Henning will be spending the next two months volunteering for 'Doctors without Borders'

Less than one month after officially graduating from the University of Vienna, Sylvia arrived in Cameroon on Friday, March 5, 2004, where she will be volunteering with Doctors without Borders (Médecins Sans Frontières), an organization that provides worldwide medical relief and "emergency aid to victims of armed conflict, epidemics, and natural and man-made disasters, and to others who lack health care due to social or geographical isolation" (quote from the organization's website).  Sylvia is expected to leave Cameroon at the beginning of May.

Leanne Becomes Full-Time Employee

After working as an intern at Ogilvy Public Relations, one of the biggest PR companies in the US, Leanne was officially appointed as a full-time Assistant Account Executive at the company last week.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Leanne on her full-time position, wishing her all the best.

Harran and Reema in the US

Harran and Reema arrived in Chicago on Wednesday and left to New Orleans on Saturday

 

Harran and Reema arrived in Chicago, Illinois on Wednesday, March 3, 2004, on the start of an eight day visit to the US in which Harran will be attending a conference in New Orleans, Louisiana (where Samer is currently studying).  After spending a few days with Amer and Leanne, Harran and Reema left Chicago to New Orleans on Saturday, March 6.  They are expected to return to Amman on Thursday, March 11.

Amal and Ziad Return from Egypt

 

Amal and Ziad left to Egypt on Tuesday, March 2, 2004.  They returned home on Friday, March 5.

Mona Returns from Oman

Mona returned to Sweden on Saturday, March 6, 2004, after attending the Muscat International Book Fair in Muscat, Oman.  Mona had left to Oman on February 22.
