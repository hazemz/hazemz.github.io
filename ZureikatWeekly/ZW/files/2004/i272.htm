Tarek Becomes Certified Management Accountant

After passing four tests he has been sitting for during the past year, Tarek is now a Certified Management Accountant (CMA).  The highly-recognized CMA program is administered by the Institute of Management Accountants.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Tarek on this achievement, wishing him all the best.

JTV Airs Special Report on Alia

"Alia Harran Zureikat - the Will and the Challenge" was the title of a special aired on Jordan Television last Wednesday, August 4, 2004. During the special, which was part of a program dedicated to persons with disabilities, Alia spoke from her office at the National Council for Family Affairs about her childhood, her life at college, and about her work. Alia also described her experience at the conference she recently attended at the United Nations in New York. At the end of the special, Alia sent a message to the viewers and to society, saying that people with disabilities are, above all, human beings who can work and contribute to society just like anyone else.  What should be mentioned is that a rerun of the program was aired on Saturday, August 7.

Zureikats Celebrate Dana and Ammar's First Wedding Anniversary

Dana and Ammar are expecting their first child by the end of January

Zureikats celebrated Dana and Ammar's first wedding anniversary on Monday, August 2, 2004.  Both currently living in Washington, DC, Dana and Ammar got married on Saturday, August 2, 2003, at the St. Sophia Greek Orthodox Cathedral in DC.  The wedding celebrations, attended by nine Zureikats from around the world as well as a number of relatives and friends, included an Arabic dinner party at Nayla (a Lebanese restaurant in Washington, DC) hosted by Marwan and Abla, a wedding reception and dinner at the City Museum of Washington, DC, and a brunch at Sequoia Restaurant in the Washington Harbor area hosted by Mr. Fahhad and Mrs. Ayda Daoud   What should be mentioned is that Dana and Ammar are expecting their first child by the end of January 2005.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Dana and Ammar a very happy anniversary and all the best.

Local News:

  • King Abdullah on Tuesday criticized the Palestinian leadership for its wavering demands and called for urgent reforms at the top level in order to move the peace process back on track.  “We want the Palestinian leadership to declare clearly what it wants and not surprise us every now and then with some decisions or by accepting things that it did not accept before,” King Abdullah told the Saudi-based Al Arabiya satellite channel in an interview, adding that what had been rejected before has unfortunately become a great achievement by some people.

World News:

  • Sporadic explosions and gunfire echoed through the city of Najaf killing hundreds as Shiite leaders appealed for a renewed cease-fire to end two days of bloody battles between insurgents and Iraqi and US forces in Shiite communities across Iraq.  Meanwhile, Iraqi interim Prime Minister Iyad Allawi signed an amnesty Saturday that was intended to persuade Iraqis fighting a 15-month-old insurgency to put down their weapons and join in government efforts to rebuild the country.  But the law, which pardoned only minor criminals, not those guilty of killings or terror attacks, appeared unlikely to dampen the violence, and some insurgent leaders immediately rejected the move as "insignificant."  Also Saturday, the government ordered the offices of the pan-Arab television station Al Jazeera closed for 30 days, accusing it of inciting violence.

  • Tens of thousands of Sudanese, chanting anti-US and anti-British slogans, demonstrated in Khartoum, Sudan Wednesday in protest at the UN Security Council resolution on Darfur and to warn against any Western military intervention in the war-torn province.
