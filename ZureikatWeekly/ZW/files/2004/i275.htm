It's a Boy!

 

Dana and Ammar announced Thursday that the baby they are expecting next January is a boy.  Shortly after making the announcement, Dana and Ammar e-mailed a sonogram image of the baby to the family in Amman.  Following is a letter from Marwan addressed to Dana and Ammar:

Dear Dana and Ammar,

The news coming at the weekend was fantastic.  The image you sent to our computer screen filled us with joy.  For the first time, what was expected is now a reality.  He certainly has a big head (smart).  We are told also that he has two arms, two legs (some say three) and a smiling face if you are lucky to attract his attention while the images are taken.

It is going to prove to be an exciting time waiting for this new 'traveler' to arrive.  He is a unique person who travels without any travel documents or luggage.  He does not even have a name, and if he does, he will not be able to know it until he arrives.  I understand that you talk to him.  If you do, please ask him if he is aware of the amount of life he has brought to our living.

I shall be waiting at the station.

Marwan

Karadshehs Arrive Wednesday

Dalia: We've been busy for the last 15 days in selling our furniture

       

After spending more than one year in the US, Hala, Naief, Dalia, Deena, and Salem will be arriving in Amman on Wednesday, September 1, 2004.  They will be leaving Washington, DC on Tuesday.  "We've been busy for the last 15 days in selling our furniture and the car and packing up our stuff," Dalia said in her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief.  "We've been also seeing our friends from school and have been spending time with our neighbors and friends from church."

Alia's Planned Trip to New York...Canceled

Alia's planned trip to New York to attend the second session for the United Nations International Convention on the Protection and Promotion of the Rights and Dignity of Persons With Disabilities was canceled at the last minute.  This came after the Jordanian Ministry of Social Development decided not to send a delegation representing the kingdom.  Alia, who attended the first session of the convention in May, was scheduled to leave to New York on Sunday, August 22, 2004.

Zureikats Celebrate Ayman and Talal's Birthday

 

Zureikats celebrated Ayman and Talal's birthday on Monday, August 23, 2004.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish both Ayman and Talal a very happy birthday and all the best.

New Novel Released by Dar Al-Mona

Secret of the Fire, a new novel by Henning Mankell, one of Sweden great modern authors, has been published in Arabic by Dar Al-Mona.  The book is in 160 pages hardcover, illustrated by the German artist Patrick Hespeler.

World News:

  • Several thousand Palestinian prisoners in Israeli jails pressed on with a fortnight-long hunger strike, buoyed by a token concession on the part of Israel, as a suspected Palestinian collaborator was shot dead by gunmen in the West Bank. On Friday night, the Palestinian prisoners' association said some 800 detainees at Ashkelon prison had temporarily called off their strike after some of their demands for better conditions were met. The other 3,200 prisoners are maintaining the strike, the association said. Some 8,000 Palestinians are currently held in Israeli prisons.

  • Lebanon's Cabinet approved a bill Saturday that would allow President Emile Lahoud to seek reelection, pleasing powerbroker Syria but opposed by a growing number of influential Lebanese and several major foreign powers. The bill, which would amend the constitution to enable Lahoud to stay on for only three more years, rather than the normal six, must now be submitted to parliament, which also elects the president from among the Christian community.

  • Shiite militants and US forces battled throughout the Baghdad slum of Sadr City, and a mortar barrage slammed into a busy neighborhood in the capital in a new wave of violence Saturday that killed at least five people and wounded dozens of others.  US warplanes and tanks later bombarded targets in the volatile city of Fallujah, and US forces exchanged gunfire with insurgents along the city's eastern outskirts and the main highway running to neighboring Jordan, witnesses said. The fighting left at least 14 people injured, hospital officials said. The new fighting came as residents of Najaf began digging the holy city out of the rubble and debris left by three weeks of fierce fighting between militants and US forces. The crisis ended Friday when the militants withdrew under a peace deal brokered by Iraq's most senior Shiite cleric, Grand Ayatollah Ali Sistani.

  • US authorities are investigating an aide to a senior Pentagon official who allegedly passed secrets to Israel with the help of employees of a powerful pro-Israel lobby. The Federal Bureau of Investigation believes the man passed classified White House policy documents on Iran to Israel and received no money, but acted out of ideological support for the Jewish state, said a top US official who spoke on condition of anonymity.

  • Israel plans to build 530 new settler homes in the occupied West Bank after Washington softened its opposition to growth in Jewish enclaves, a policy shift Palestinians say will make peace impossible.
