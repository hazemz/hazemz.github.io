Local News:

  • King Abdullah II ended his trip to the US after meeting with President George W. Bush and receiving the Golden Plate Award.

  • The Ministry of Education rejected claims that questions from the General English Tawjihi exam were leaked the night before the exam.  The Ministry also announced new measures that would prevent such "rumors" from being spread.

  • Prime Minister Faisal Al-Fayez visited Singapore.

  • Jordan's military court has convicted 15 men -- all but one of whom remains at-large -- for a terror conspiracy targeting unspecified American and Israeli interests in Jordan as well as Jordanian intelligence officials. (Headline from CNN.com)

World News:

  • US forces launched an airstrike in Fallujah, Iraq that killed 18 people.  US military sources said the strike was aimed at a safe house for Abu Musab Al-Zarqawi.  Meanwhile, several car bombs exploded in Baghdad last week, the most deadly of which killed 35 people.

  • An American hostage kidnapped in Saudi Arabia last week was beheaded by a group calling itself 'Al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula'.  Self-proclaimed leader of the group, Abdel Aziz Al-Muqrin was killed in a shoot-out with Saudi police a few hours after the beheading.

  • An Israeli airstrike in Gaza last week injured several Palestinians.

  • The Iraqi interim health minister survived an assassination attempt.
