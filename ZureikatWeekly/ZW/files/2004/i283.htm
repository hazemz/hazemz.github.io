Mona Returns to Sweden

After attending Abu Harran's funeral services and receiving condolences from relatives and friends in Jordan, Mona returned to Sweden on Tuesday, October 19, 2004.

Dana and Ammar Move to New House


Dana and Ammar's new house in McLean, Virginia

Dana and Ammar bought a new house in McLean, Virginia, near Washington, DC.  With Abla N.'s assistance, the two moved into the new house on Thursday, October 21, 2004.  Dana and Ammar, who are expecting a baby boy in January, currently live in Washington and will be moving to Dubai in a few months.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to congratulate Dana and Ammar on their new house, wishing them all the best.

Abla, Bana Return from US

 

After visiting Dana in Washington, DC, Abla returned to Amman on Saturday, October 23, 2004.  She arrived home late Sunday.  On the other hand, Bana, who attended a conference and visited Amer and Leanne in Chicago, returned to Amman on Tuesday, October 19.

Zureikats Celebrate Dalia's Birthday

Zureikats celebrated Dalia's birthday on Tuesday, October 19, 2004.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Dalia a very happy birthday and all the best.

Local News:

  • Prime Minister Faisal Fayez reshuffled his year-old cabinet on Sunday.  A royal decree was issued appointing the following ministers: Dr. Marwan Muasher as Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of State for Prime Ministry Affairs and Government Performance, Dr. Hani Mulki as Minister of Foreign Affairs, Dr. Salah Bashir as Minister of Justice, Dr. Hazem Nasser as Minister of Water and Irrigation, Dr. Alia Hattough-Bouran as Minister of Tourism and Antiquities, Eng. Raed Abu Saud as Minister of Public Works and Housing, Asma Khader as Minister of Culture and Government Spokesperson, Nayef Hadid as Minister of State for Parliamentary Affairs, Dr. Ahmad Hindawi as Minister of Industry and Trade, Dr. Yousef Shureiqi as Minister of the Environment, Nadia Saeed as Minister of Information and Communications Technology, Fahd Abul Athem Ensour as Minister of State for Legal Affairs, Soud Nsairat as Minister of Transport, Dr. Munther Shara as Minister of Political Development, Dr. Ahmad Masaadeh as Minister of State for Public Reforms, and Sharari Shakhanbeh as Minister of Agriculture.  The reshuffle, which introduced a previous minister and nine first-timers, raised the number of cabinet members in Fayez's government from 21 to 28.  It also raised the number of women ministers from an unprecedented three to a new record of four.

  • King Abdullah II visited Libya and met with Mubarak in Aqaba.

  • Hundreds of citizens marched from Al Husseini Mosque to the Greater Amman Municipality headquarters after Friday prayers to protest against daily attacks by Israeli and US troops in both Palestine and Iraq.

World News:

  • Almost 50 new Iraqi soldiers were gunned down in an execution in a remote area of eastern Iraq, officials said Sunday, while a US diplomat was killed in a mortar attack on a military base in Baghdad.  Meanwhile, dozens of Iraqis were killed in violence around the country during the past week.

  • Pro-Syrian Lebanese President Emile Lahoud named former Premier Omar Karameh Thursday to succeed Prime Minister Rafik Hariri, who resigned a day earlier amid international criticism of the influence Damascus wields over its tiny neighbor.

  • Ahead of the planned Gaza withdrawal, Prime Minister Ariel Sharon's Cabinet passed a bill on Sunday for compensating Jewish settlers who leave and punishing those who resist.

  • The US said Wednesday it would be "concerned" by Iran's acquisition of any new nuclear technology, signaling opposition to a reported European offer to give Tehran a light-water reactor it proves it is not secretly developing atomic weapons.
