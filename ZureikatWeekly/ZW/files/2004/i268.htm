Local News:

  • A moderate earthquake measuring 4.6 on the Richter scale shook Amman and mountainous areas overlooking the northern Jordan Valley on Wednesday, but caused no casualties.  The epicenter of the tremor, which occurred at 5:35pm, was to the north of the Dead Sea, 20 kilometers below the ground.

World News:

  • The International Court of Justice (ICJ) ruled Friday that the 700-kilometre West Bank barrier violates international law and that those parts that encroach on Palestinian territory should be dismantled.  Meanwhile, several Palestinians were killed last week in a number of Israeli offensive against Palestinian cities.

  • A bomb exploded near a bus stop in Tel Aviv killing one Israeli and wounding twenty.

  • A few days after his return to Egypt from Germany, President Hosni Mubarak appointed 52-year-old technocrat Ahmad Nazief as Prime Minister following the resignation of former Prime Minister Atef Obeid.

  • The chief of the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA), Mohammad Al-Baradei, ended his visit to Israel with no change in the Israeli government's nuclear policy.
