Karadshehs Return

Zureikats gathered at Abu Harran's on Wednesday to welcome Hala and the family back

Dalia, Deena, and Salem start school in Amman

After spending one year in the US, Hala, Naief, Dalia, Deena, and Salem returned to Amman on Wednesday, September 1, 2004.  Celebrating this occasion, the family gathered at Abu Harran's on Wednesday night for a Kharoof dinner hosted by Hassan and Dian.  Dalia, who has now moved to the Baptist School, along with Deena and Salem, who are still at Amman National School, started school in Amman this week.  The Karadshehs had spent one year living in Rockville, Maryland where Hala attended classes at the University of Maryland at College Park after being awarded a Hubert H. Humphrey fellowship.  Naief, on the other hand, did research at the George Washington University as part of the Distinguished Scholarship Award which he received from the Arab Fund for Social and Economic Development (based in Kuwait).

Reem, Talal, Hazem Return to UK, US

Ayman leaves Tuesday

   

Hazem returned to St. Paul, Minnesota on Saturday, September 4, 2004, to start his third year at Macalester College.  Reem and Talal, who had only spent a couple of weeks in Amman, returned to the UK on Sunday, September 5.

Harran and Reema Return from Munich

 

Harran and Reema returned from Munich, Germany early Thursday, September 2, 2004.  The two had left to Germany on Friday, August 27.

Replacing Yacoub S.

Nadine Appointed as New ZW Correspondent

The Editor in Chief appointed Nadine as a new ZW Correspondent representing Hassan, Dian, Sabine, Nadine, and Yacoub Z.  Nadine replaces Yacoub S., who has been the Correspondent for Hassan's family for the past year.  The Editor in Chief would like to take this opportunity to thank Yacoub S. for all his efforts and to welcome Nadine on board the ZW team.
