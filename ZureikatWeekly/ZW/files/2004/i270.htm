Abla Recognized at Graduation Ceremony after Spending 10 Years at ANS

Abla at her office in ANS.  (From The Zureikat Weekly's archive)

Abla Z. was recognized at Amman National School's graduation ceremony after spending ten years at the school. Before Abla got on stage to receive a gift from the director general of the school, the host of the graduation ceremony described how Abla had a special relationship with her students, and how her students anxiously awaited her class and the challenges she had in store for them. The graduation ceremony was held at the school on Monday, July 19, 2004, and was attended by Hazem and Basil's sister, Rose. The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Abla on this recognition, wishing her all the best.

Local News:

  • Jordan's national team has boosted its chances of a first time showing in the second round of Asian Cup finals after their dramatic 2-0 win over Kuwait on Friday which put them atop Group B with favorites South Korea.  Khaled Sa'ad and Anas Zboun scored a minute apart in injury time to shock the Kuwaiti team, fans and tournament participants to earn a crucial win although Jordan was down to 10 players when Faisal Ibrahim was ejected from the match in the second-half.

  • King Abdullah on Tuesday sent a letter to Prime Minister Faisal Fayez reminding him of his expectations for advancement on the domestic front.  In the letter, King Abdullah acknowledged “achievements over the past months,” but added that “it is important to focus on several ideas to help you and your team accelerate the pace of work and progress we stressed in the Letter of Designation.”  The King reiterated that the priority of his mandate to the Fayez government, which took office in October 2003, was to “improve the living standards of citizens."

  • Iraq's interim Prime Minister Iyad Allawi visited Jordan last week.

  • King Abdullah on Thursday laid the cornerstone for the new King's Academy, a co-educational boarding school that will replicate the US-based Deerfield Academy where he completed his high school.

  • King Abdullah is currently on an Asian tour which takes him to South Korea, Hong Kong, China, and Thailand.

World News:

  • Israel vowed on Wednesday to press ahead with construction of its West Bank barrier despite a UN resolution demanding it be torn down, but Palestinians called for international sanctions to force compliance.

  • Israeli Prime Minister Ariel Sharon was told Monday he is unwelcome in France in an escalating diplomatic row sparked by his appeal to French Jews to leave the country immediately, Israeli television reported.

  • A bomb killed a senior member of Lebanese group Hizbollah in the southern suburbs of Beirut on Monday in an attack the group blamed on Israel.

  • A train derailed from its tracks in Turkey on Thursday, killing at least 36 people, including 4 Jordanians.

  • A Filipino truck driver held hostage in Iraq for two weeks was freed unhurt on Tuesday, a day after Manila withdrew its troops in response to demands from kidnappers who had threatened to behead him.

  • The US could not protect its citizens from the Sept. 11 terrorist attacks because it failed to appreciate the threat posed by Al Qaeda that exploited such a failure to carry out the deadliest assault ever on American soil, the chairman of the Sept. 11 commission said Thursday.
