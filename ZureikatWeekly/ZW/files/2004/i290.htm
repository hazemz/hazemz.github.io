Bana Awarded Fulbright Grant

Bana will leave to the US next fall to complete her Master's degree

Bana has been awarded the Fulbright Grant to complete her Master's degree in the US starting fall (September) 2005.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Bana on her scholarship, wishing her all the best.  Details of the grant and university will be available in the coming month.

Kareem Returns Home

Kareem, who is currently in his second year at the University of Bristol, arrived in Amman on Sunday, December 12, 2004, to spend his Christmas holiday with the family.

Ammar Leaves to Dubai

Ammar will be returning to DC next week to spend Christmas with Dana

Ammar left to Dubai, UAE on Monday, December 6, 2004, where he will be working as the General Manager of the 'Arabian Coffee Company'.  The new company will start and manage branches of Caribou Coffee (based in the US) in the Middle East.  Ammar will be returning to Washington, DC next week to spend Christmas with Dana.

Hassan, Dian, Hala Visit Beirut

Hala returned to Amman on Saturday

   

Hassan and Dian left to Beirut on Wednesday, December 8, 2004.  They are expected to return home in a few days.  On a separate working visit, Hala left to Beirut, also on Wednesday.  She returned to Amman on Saturday, December 11.

Zureikats Celebrate Salem's 10th Birthday

 

 

Zureikats celebrated Salem's 10th birthday on Saturday, December 11, 2004.  On this occasion, a number of family members gathered at Hala and Naief's on Saturday evening.

 

...and Tarek's Birthday

The family also celebrated Tarek's birthday on Wednesday, December 8, 2004.  Bashar, Bana, Omar, Alia and Rana joined Tarek's friends to celebrate his birthday on Thursday*.  The celebration took place at Vertigo restaurant.  It included music, singing, birthday cake and enough champagne to go around.

*What should be noted is that, although Tarek's real birthday was on Wednesday, he refused to see anyone because there was a Liverpool football match on that evening.

Houston's Opens Branch in Aqaba

Houston's opened its third branch about two weeks ago.  The new branch is located in the Aqaba Gateway commercial center in Aqaba.  Houston's currently has two other branches: the main branch in Shmeisani and a smaller branch in Mecca Mall (both in Amman).  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Marwan and the Houston's staff on the opening of the new branch.  What should be mentioned is that Tarek visited the new branch in Aqaba last weekend.  He left Amman on Friday, December 10, and returned the next day.

Local News:

  • The King arrived in Japan after visiting the US and meeting with President George W. Bush. In two interviews with CNN and the Washington Post during his visit, the King warned against any change in the balanced Sunni-Shiite geopolitics in the region or an external interference in the polls, particularly from Iran.

  • The European Commission on Thursday adopted the first Action Plan for Jordan within the framework of the European Neighborhood Policy. Under the plan, Jordan will receive increased financial support that target actions envisaged in the Action Plan — mainly to support the political reform and economic modernization agenda of the Jordanian government, a statement released by the European Commission Delegation said.

  • Royal Jordanian (RJ) has decided to suspend flights to Casablanca in Morocco as of the beginning of February 2005 due to economic reasons.

World News:

  • Palestine Liberation Organization Chairman Mahmoud Abbas apologized Sunday to Kuwait for the organization's pro-Baghdad stand during Iraq's 1990 invasion of the emirate, at the start of a landmark visit aimed at healing a 14-year rift.

  • Four Israeli soldiers were killed Sunday when Palestinian fighters blew up a tunnel under an army post in Gaza, as jailed Intifada leader Marwan Barghouthi pulled out of the Palestinian election.

  • American warplanes pounded Fallujah with missiles Sunday as insurgents fought running battles with coalition forces in the volatile western Iraqi city.

  • A US soldier was sentenced to three years in prison for killing a severely wounded Iraqi teenager, the military said Saturday.

  • Shimon Peres was preparing the ground Saturday to lead his Labor Party into Ariel Sharon's new coalition government after the Israeli prime minister crushed internal opposition to his Gaza pullout plan.

  • Arab commentators were left unimpressed Sunday by the US-backed reform conference in Rabat and said it failed to address the Israeli-Palestinian conflict which held the key to change in the region.

  • The Bush administration has dozens of intercepts of Mohamed ElBaradei's phone calls with Iranian diplomats and is scrutinizing them in search of ammunition to oust him as director general of the International Atomic Energy Agency, according to three U.S. government officials.

  • A powerful explosion ripped through a market packed with Christmas shoppers in the southern Philippines on Sunday, killing at least 15 people and injuring 58 others, the military said.
