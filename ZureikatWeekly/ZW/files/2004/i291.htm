Hala Awarded Ordre de Merite Medal

A reception was held at the French ambassador's house in Amman

Hala was awarded the Ordre de Merite medal by the French Ambassador to Jordan.  Hala officially received the medal at a reception held at the ambassador's residence in Amman on Monday, December 13, 2004.  The reception was attended by several Zureikats.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Hala on this prestigious medal, wishing her all the best.

Ayman, Hazem Return Home; Talal Returns Monday

Reem and Samer will not spend Christmas in Amman this year

   

Ayman and Hazem will be leaving to Amman on Saturday, December 18, 2004, where they will spend their Christmas vacations with the family.  Both are expected to arrive in Amman on Sunday.  Talal, who has been in Spain for the past five weeks, is expected to return home on Monday, December 20.  On the other hand, Reem and Samer will not be spending Christmas in Amman this year.  Reem is expected to stay in London, and Samer in New Orleans.

 

Amer and Leanne to Visit Amman on the 28th

 

Amer and Leanne are expected to arrive in Amman on Tuesday, December 28, 2004, on their second visit to Jordan after they moved to the US in June 2003.

Mona Returns to Sweden after Gulf Visit

According to an e-mail she sent to the Editor in Chief a few weeks ago, Mona concluded a 22-day visit to Kuwait and Qatar and returned to Sweden on Tuesday, December 14, 2004.

Last issue in 2004 to be released next week

Christmas Eve at Harran's; Christmas Day at Marwan's

Zureikats around the world will be observing Christmas at the end of this week.  On this occasion, the family in Jordan are expected to gather at Harran and Reema's on Christmas Eve (Friday night) and at Marwan and Abla's on Christmas Day (Saturday).  Meanwhile, the last 2004 issue of The Zureikat Weekly will be released next Sunday, December 26.  The Editor in Chief welcomes any suggestions for a new logo and layout or any other improvements to the ZW website.

  • Bana made a short working visit to Beirut.  She left Amman on Tuesday, December 14, and returned on Thursday, December 16.

Local News:

  • The King concluded a five-nation tour which took him to Libya, the US, Japan, Singapore and Qatar.

  • The State Security Court on Wednesday adjourned the case of nine men accused of plotting the first-ever Al Qaeda chemical attack in Jordan until Dec. 22, because the defendants refused to respond to the tribunal's questions “until their prison conditions were improved.”

  • Nineteen patients with typhoid-like symptoms from Deir Alla and one from Karak are still hospitalized, but no new cases have emerged, according to a Health Ministry report for Wednesday.

World News:

  • Palestinian leader Mahmoud Abbas said his people should drop their weapons in the struggle for a state, marking out a clear change of strategy for peace with Israel after Yasser Arafat's death last month.

  • Saddam Hussein's top "henchmen" will start going on trial for crimes against humanity next week, Prime Minister Iyad Allawi said Tuesday, as he warned violence would increase after Iraq's landmark elections in January.

  • An Italian hostage was reported killed in Iraq, adding to the violence around Thursday's start of campaigning for the first post-Saddam Hussein elections, while for the first time since his capture a year ago the ousted president met his family-appointed lawyers.

  • The European Union and Turkey struck a deal to allow Turkey to begin negotiations next year on full membership of the union. The agreement sidestepped an EU demand that Turkey immediately recognize the Greek Cypriot-led government of Cyprus.

  • France has called for an EU-wide debate after a Lebanese television station accused of spreading anti-Semitic propaganda for Hizbollah was pulled from a French satellite television network Tuesday following a French ruling banning it. The channel, Al Manar, voluntarily stopped beaming via Paris-based satellite company Eutelsat's satellite after being notified of the order issued Monday by France's highest administrative court, but complained that its right to freedom of expression was being infringed.

  • In Lebanon, opposition groups of various religious and political affiliations on Monday unveiled their first united platform since the 1975-90 civil war, calling on Syria to stay out of Lebanese legislative elections and to redeploy its army near its border.
