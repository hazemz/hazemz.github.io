Reema Holds Annual FFSPD Event

The luncheon was attended by a number of Zureikats and was patronized by HRH Princess Mona

Reema held the annual fundraising event for the Family and Friends Society of Persons with Disabilities (FFSPD).  A luncheon was held on Saturday, December 4, 2004, under the patronage of HRH Princess Mona Al-Hussein.  Amal, Abla, Abla, Hala, and Dian, as well as donors, wives of ambassadors, and a number of members of the Royal Family attended.  During the event, Reema gave a speech thanking the guests for their continuous support and encouragement.  After presenting the Society's main achievements throughout the last year, Reema introduced FFSPD's new chairperson, Mrs. Riham Omeish Abu Diyeh, who also gave a brief presentation.  The highlight of the event was the story of a mother with cerebral palsy, who made it in spite of all odds, challenging her family and friends when she fell in love with and married her husband.  The event also included a raffle, in which Amal and a number of Ayoubs won a few gifts.

Dana Visits Minnesota, Returns to DC with Ammar

Ammar completes 2-month training, leaves to Dubai on Monday

 

Ammar completed eight weeks of training in Minnesota for his new job as General Manager of the Arabian Coffee Company.  He returned to Washington, DC on Friday, December 3, 2004.  The new company, which will be based in Dubai, UAE will start and manage branches of Caribou Coffee (a company based in Minneapolis, Minnesota) around the Middle East.  Ammar is expected to leave to Dubai on Monday, December 6.  On the other hand, Dana arrived in Minneapolis on Monday, November 29, on a four-day visit.  She returned to DC with Ammar on Friday.

Local News:

  • Fearing the spread of the Sunni insurgency into the country, Jordan closed its border with Iraq. Jordanian officials said the move would block all travelers coming from Iraq into the Kingdom. Jordanians and other nationals would be allowed to enter Iraq.

  • In spite of the Israeli Air Force’s objections, the US administration over the weekend approved a deal to supply Jordan with 50 advanced air-to-air missiles.

  • The King visited Libya then left to the US on Saturday to meet with President Bush and top US officials. Libya and the US are the King's first stops in a four-nation tour which will also take him to Japan and Singapore.  Earlier last week, the King also visited Saudi Arabia.

  • King Abdullah opened the second ordinary session of the 14th Parliament on Wednesday urging a step up of national programs meant to improve citizens' lives. During the opening session, incumbent Lower House Speaker Abdul Hadi Majali was reelected by a narrow margin for a second term.

  • The Higher Education Council (HEC) on Thursday made a reshuffle that saw the dismissal of all presidents of public universities, excluding the president of the Jordan University of Science and Technology, Wajih Oweis.

  • The Cabinet on Thursday decided to appoint Major General Mohammad Jaber Aitan as Public Security Department (PSD) director as of Friday, succeeding Major General Tahseen Shurdom, who will take up a position as an adviser at the Royal Court as of Friday.

World News:

  • Despite criticism from his Fatah party, Marwan Barghouti, who is currently imprisoned in Israel, decided to run for the January 9 Palestinian presidential election.

  • Dozens of Iraqis and US soldiers died in Iraq as violence continued throughout the country in the past week.

  • Israeli-Arab businessman, Azzam Azzam, accused of spying for Israel, was released from jail in Egypt as part of a prisoner swap with Israel. The deal also included six Egyptian students imprisoned in Israel.

  • Ukraine's supreme court nulled the latest election results, paving the way for a new vote to take place.

  • US Homeland Security Secretary Tom Ridge resigned on Thursday. President Bush nominated former New York City Police Commissioner Bernard Kerik to replace him.
