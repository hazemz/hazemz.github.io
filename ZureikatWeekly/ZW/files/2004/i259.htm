After volunteering for nine weeks with Médecins Sans Frontières

Sylvia Returns from Cameroon

Sylvia arrived in Sweden on Saturday, May 8, 2004, after spending nine weeks volunteering with Doctors without Borders (Médecins Sans Frontières) in Cameroon.  What should be mentioned is that Sylvia had graduated from the University of Vienna on February 11 and had arrived in Cameroon on March 5.

Hala Completes Humphrey Program

A luncheon was held at the UMD College of Journalism on Friday; official graduation to be held during West Virginia retreat next week

 

A luncheon was held on Friday, May 7, for the Humphrey fellows at the University of Maryland.

Hala is wrapping up her year at the University of Maryland, as the 2003-2004 Hubert H. Humphrey Program comes to an end.  On this occasion, the University of Maryland's Philip Merrill College of Journalism held a luncheon for its Humphrey fellows on Friday, May 7, 2004.  During the luncheon, which was attended by Naief, Hala and the other Humphrey fellows received certificates "for successful completion of the curriculum and professional program as a Hubert Humphrey fellow in Communication and Media Management".  What should be mentioned is that the official graduation ceremony for all 137 Humphrey fellows in the US will be held next week in West Virginia, during which the fellows will receive certificates form the US State Department.

Hazem Starts Summer Holiday

Samer and Ayman finish their final exams by the end of the week

Samer graduates on May 22...more details in the next two issues

Hazem finished his second year at Macalester College in St. Paul, Minnesota on Friday, May 7, 2004.  Samer, who is currently in his fourth year at Tulane University in New Orleans, Louisiana, and Ayman, who is currently in his third year at Tufts University in Boston, will both finish their final exams by the end of this week.  Hazem is expected to stay in St. Paul until Sunday, May 16, when he will be traveling to New York City, Washington, DC, and finally to New Orleans (to attend Samer's graduation) before returning to Amman on May 23.  More details on Samer's May 22 graduation and those who will be attending it in the next two issues.

Dalia Receives Certificate, Joins Honor Roll

Dalia received a "national Blue Ribbon" certificate from her school (the Walter Johnson High School) last week after achieving "a high standard of academic excellence by maintaining a 3.14 third quarter marking period average".  By receiving this certificate, Dalia is now on her school's honor roll.  What should be mentioned is that Deena was also in the honor roll at her school for the first and second quarters of this academic year.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate both Dalia and Deena for their outstanding academic achievement, wishing them all the best.

Engicon Launches its New Website

The website reflects the company's new corporate identity

Engicon launched its new website (http://www.engicon.com/) on Saturday, May 8, 2004.  The website reflects the company's new name and corporate identity.  The company was formerly known as Jordanian Consulting Engineer, Co. and changed its name in December of last year to better position itself in the outside market and "respond to new opportunities", as Marwan, Chairman of the company, put it.  The new website was designed by Mr. Ameen Hannoun.
