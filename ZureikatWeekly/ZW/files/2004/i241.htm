Amer and Leanne in Amman

Zureikats gather at Harran and Reema's

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Amer and Leanne arrived in Amman on Monday, December 29, 2003, on their first visit to Jordan after they moved to Chicago last June.  Celebrating the visit, Zureikats and Kawars gathered at Harran and Reema's for dinner on Tuesday, December 30.  Amer, Leanne, Abu Harran, Harran, Reema, Kareem, and Dr. George Kawar and his family also gathered at Marwan and Abla's on Sunday, January 4, 2004, for lunch.  Amer and Leanne are expected to return to Chicago within three weeks.

Zureikats Celebrate Abu Harran's Birthday

Zureikats celebrated Abu Harran's 91st birthday on Thursday, January 1, 2004.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Abu Harran a very happy birthday and a long and healthy life.

Reem Returns to the UK

After spending her Christmas break in Amman, Reem returned to the UK on Monday, December 29, 2003.

The Annual New Year's Kharoof at the Shomalis'

 

 

 

 

 

 

In what has become a tradition, Zureikats gathered at Abla and Basil's on the first Friday after New Year's Day -- January 2, 2004, for the annual 'Kharoof Mahshi'.  Guests this year included Dr. George Kawar and his family.

Bashar Passes All 14 Parts of Major Test for Bank Employees

Bashar, who is currently working at the Standard Chartered Bank, was the first in Jordan to pass all fourteen parts of a major test taken by bank employees.  Bashar took the twelve-hour test a few months ago, but the Editor in Chief was told of the news just days ago.   The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this belated opportunity to congratulate Bashar, wishing him all the best.

  • The Karadshehs welcomed the new year with some of their friends.  In an e-mail they sent to the Editor in Chief, Dalia, Deena, and Salem said, "On New Year's [Eve], we went to my parents' friends' house for an early dinner.  We ate delicious dinner.  After dinner, they gave an empty ginger bread house, and they gave us the candy to decorate it.  It had lightning from the inside."

       

  • Jordanian Prime Minister Faisal Al-Fayez, along with the Ministers of Interior, Education, and Tourism and Antiquities and the Environment and the Mayor of Amman made a surprise visit to Yacoub S.'s class in Amman National School on Tuesday, December 30, 2003.  The class (the 8th grade) had earlier sent several letters to the government as part of its English class.  The Prime Minister and those who accompanied him responded to the students' questions and concerns.  Abla Z., Coordinator of the English Department at ANS, attended the discussion.

Courtesy of the Jordan News Agency (Petra)
