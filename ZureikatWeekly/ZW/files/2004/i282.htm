Relatives, Friends Pay Condolences to the Family

Three-day period ends with church service

Shortly after the funeral service last Sunday, October 10, 2004, sons, daughters, and grandchildren of Abu Harran in Amman started receiving condolences from relatives and friends from around Jordan for three days.  Men paid their condolences at Harran's; women at Marwan's.  The three-day period ended on Tuesday, October 12, with a church service marking three days since the passing away of Abu Harran.  During the service, Marwan delivered a speech (shown above).

Other News:

  • A few months after passing the CMA (Certified Management Accountant) exam, Tarek passed the CFM (Certified Financial Manager) exam on Monday, October 4, 2004.  By passing the CFM, Tarek will be receiving the highest certificate offered by the Institute of Management Accountants.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Tarek on this achievement, wishing him all the best.

  • Abla N. received the Ministry of Education's medal for her distinguished performance in the field of education throughout the past 33 years.  Abla was awarded the medal by Prime Minister Faisal Al-Fayez and Minister of Education Khaled Toukan in a ceremony held in Amman on Tuesday, October 5, 2004, on the occasion of the World Teachers Day.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Abla on her award, wishing her all the best.

  • Abla N. left to Washington, DC on Friday, October 15, 2004, where she will be spending eight days with Dana.  Hazem also left to DC on Friday and returned to Minnesota on Sunday, October 17.

  • Bana, who currently works for the largest mobile phone service provider in Jordan, Fastlink, left to Chicago on Saturday, October 9, 2004, to attend a conference.  She is expected to leave back to Amman on Monday, October 18.

  • Omar started the new academic year at the Jordan University.

  • Zureikats celebrated Dag's birthday on Friday, October 8, 2004.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Dag a very happy birthday and all the best.

  • Jordan switched back to winter time on Friday, October 15, 2004, putting the kingdom two hours ahead of GMT.  On another note, the Muslim holy month of Ramadan also started in Jordan on Friday, October 15.
