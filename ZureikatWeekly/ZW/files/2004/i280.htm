After spending a few days with Amer, Leanne, Dana and Ammar in DC

Harran and Reema Return Home

Amer and Leanne return to Chicago

 

After spending a few days with Amer, Leanne, Dana, and Ammar in Washington, DC, where Harran also attended a conference, Harran and Reema left DC back to Amman on Sunday, October 3, 2004.  They are expected to arrive on Monday.  Amer and Leanne, who left to DC on Friday, October 1, returned to Chicago on Sunday.

Ammar in Minnesota for 8 weeks

Dana and Ammar Move to Dubai in Few Months

 

Ammar left to Minneapolis, Minnesota on Sunday, October 3, 2004, where he will be spending eight weeks of training at the headquarters of the Caribou Coffee Company, which owns about 300 coffee shops in the US.  After the training, Ammar and Dana will be moving to Dubai, UAE where Ammar will be the General Manager of the new 'Arabian Coffee Company', which will start and manage several branches of Caribou Coffee around the Middle East.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Dana said that, during the next eight weeks, Ammar will be visiting Washington, DC every now and then.

Amal and Ziad Return from Prague, London

 

Amal and Ziad returned to Amman on Saturday, October 2, 2004, after visiting Prague, the Czech Republic, where Ziad attended a number of meetings, and London.

Zureikats Celebrate 2 Occasions

   

Zureikats celebrated Hala and Naief's wedding anniversary on Friday, October 1, 2004, and Bana's birthday on Sunday, October 3.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Hala and Naief a very happy anniversary and Bana a very happy birthday and all the best.

Local News:

  • More than 15,000 Department of Statistics interviewers set out Friday midnight on a weeklong general census.

  • The government on Monday decided to issue "residence cards" for thousands of Gazans living in Jordan to facilitate their daily life activities.

  • King Abdullah returned to Amman after visiting Paris and Rome.

  • King Abdullah on Sunday warned that Israel's "arrogance" and policy of assassinating Palestinians will fuel violence, instability and extremism in the region.

  • The Kingdom on Monday unveiled its long-awaited National Tourism Strategy with a well-defined plan aimed at reversing years of under-performance and lagging development within the competitive global tourism market.

  • The Meteorological Department said on Sunday that its weather monitoring stations around the country recorded the highest temperatures for the month of September since 1960.

  • The Kingdom's first national fund-raising day `Zad Al Kheir' was deemed a great success by organizers who said the event, held on Friday at King Hussein Park, attracted a around a hundred thousand people, with a coalition of 64 charity organizations taking part.

  • The Ministry of Health has pulled off the arthritis and pain medication Vioxx from the market after a study revealed an increased relative risk for cardiovascular problems.

  • French President Jacques Chirac has bestowed the Officer of Legion of Honor medal upon General Saad Kheir, the King's adviser on security issues, director of the General Intelligence Department (GID) and president of the National Security Council. The decision came within the context of the distinguished ties between Jordan and France and in recognition of the cooperation between the intelligence bodies in the two countries.

  • With a promise to reflect the lifestyle of modern Jordanian youth by way of music and live interactive shows, PLAY 99.6 FM is the latest radio station to hit the Kingdom's airwaves this week.

World News:

  • A string of bombs killed 35 children and wounded scores of others as US troops handed out candy Thursday at a government-sponsored celebration to inaugurate a new sewage plant. It was the largest death toll of children in any insurgent attack since the Iraq conflict began.

  • US and Iraqi forces battled pockets of resistance Saturday in Samarra. A US commander said 125 rebels were killed and 88 captured in two days of fierce fighting, declaring 'success'.

  • 39 Palestinians were killed and hundreds wounded, as Israeli troops struck deep inside the largest Palestinian refugee camp Thursday, battling fighters in an unprecedented campaign to stop deadly rocket fire on Israeli towns.

  • US President George W. Bush and Democratic presidential candidate John F. Kerry faced off on Thursday in the first of three presidential debates. Polls conducted afterwards showed that the debate was a clear boost for Kerry and that the two candidates are about even now.

  • A Hamas leader was killed in a car bombing in Damascus, Syria last Sunday.

  • Two female Italian aid workers kidnapped in Baghdad three weeks ago were released Tuesday along with two Iraqi colleagues, making at least 10 the number of people freed in 24 hours, including employees of Egyptian telecoms giant Orascom. Meanwhile, a weeping British hostage was shown pleading for help between the bars of a makeshift cage in a video that surfaced Wednesday.

  • A wall collapsed at an airport construction site in Dubai Monday, killing more than eight workers and injuring many more.

  • A Yemeni court Wednesday sentenced to death two Al Qaeda militants for the October 2000 bombing of the US navy destroyer Cole which claimed the lives of 17 US sailors.
