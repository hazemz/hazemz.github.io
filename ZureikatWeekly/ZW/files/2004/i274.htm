After they were not able to obtain a visa to enter Palestine

Abla and Basil Open 2-Day Funeral House

A church service was also held on Thursday in memory of Hani Shomali

After they were not able to obtain a visa from the Israeli Embassy in Jordan to enter Palestine, Abla and Basil opened a funeral house at their home in Amman on Tuesday and Wednesday, August 17 and 18, 2004, to receive condolences on the passing away of Basil's nephew, Hani Majdi Shomali.  Shomali, 20, had died in a bus accident near Hims, Syria on Thursday, August 12.  A church service was also held in Amman on Thursday, August 19, followed by a lunch at Abla and Basil's.  (Please note the correction to what we had mentioned in the previous issue about Abla and Basil leaving to Palestine.)

Marwan, Abla, Hassan Return from Lebanon

   

After spending five days in Bikfayya, in the Lebanese mountains, Marwan and Abla returned to Amman on Thursday, August 19, 2004.  The two had left to Lebanon on Saturday, August 14.  Meanwhile, Hassan, who had left to Lebanon on a business trip also on Saturday, August 14, returned to Amman on Tuesday, August 17.

After completing the theoretical and practical exams for Grade 1

Sabine and Nadine Receive Piano Certificates from Trinity College

Nadine and Yacoub also received certificates for outstanding academic performance in the past academic year

   

After sitting through the required theoretical and practical piano exams, Sabine and Nadine received certificates from Trinity College in the UK for completing Grade 1.  The College has a world-renowned system of grades (which goes from one thru eight) for people learning to play the piano.  Sabine passed the exams with distinction, while Nadine passed with merit.  The two will be sitting for the Grade 2 exams next month.  On another note, Nadine and Yacoub Z. received certificates from their school (the Modern School) for outstanding academic performance in the past academic year, Nadine in Math, Civics, Religion, French, and Computer and Yacoub in Math.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Sabine, Nadine, and Yacoub on their accomplishments, wishing them all the best.

A few days after returning from New York

Dana and Ammar in Cincinnati for the Weekend

 

Dana and Ammar left to Cincinnati, Ohio on Friday, August 20, 2004, on a brief visit.  They are expected to return to DC on Monday, August 23.  This comes a few days after Dana, accompanied by Ammar, made a short working visit to New York.

Local News:

  • The families of Jordanian and Palestinian prisoners in Israeli jails on Wednesday staged a sit-in outside the United Nations office in Amman to show solidarity with a hunger strike against the inhuman treatment of detainees by the Israeli authorities.

  • The government on Wednesday said it took precautionary measures against a lawsuit by the family of ex-Iraqi Governing Council member Ahmed Chalabi against Jordan. A Finance Ministry source said such measures include collecting all documents and reports related to the case of liquidated Petra Bank, founded by Chalabi in 1977. The family of Chalabi, who was convicted in absentia in Jordan in 1992 on several counts of fraud, filed a suit in Washington last week against the government claiming that it conspired with former Iraqi President Saddam Hussein to murder him and destroy his family business.

  • The only two airlines with regular passenger service to Iraq suspended flights this week because the Baghdad airport has been closed for “security reasons,” officials at both airlines said Saturday. The flag carrier Royal Jordanian (RJ) and the private Virginia-based firm Air Serv stopped their service to the Iraqi capital on Tuesday, officials told the Associated Press on condition of anonymity.

  • Deputy Raed Qaqish said Tuesday he plans to sue the Jordan Engineers Association (JEA) for “falsely accusing him of being a normalizer with Israel.” The deputy, who is also a JEA member, told The Jordan Times he would file two lawsuits against the politicized association: A slander case for accusing him of being a normalizer and another contesting JEA's decision not to “invite or honor his presence at any of its events.” The JEA took a decision to isolate the deputy earlier this month after he appeared on a TV talk show opposite an Israeli official. The move was reportedly recommended by the associations' Anti-Normalization Committee the week before.

  • Jordan Aviation announced on Monday that the ban on its aircraft was lifted by the Italian and French authorities as of Monday.

World News:

  • Fighting between U.S. forces and fighters loyal to radical cleric Muqtada al-Sadr continued into the early morning hours Sunday, with explosions heard near the Imam Ali Mosque. A series of blasts left fires burning around the shrine, one of the most sacred sites in Shiite Islam. The cleric's representatives had said al-Sadr's militia would vacate the mosque soon after handing it over to Shiite leaders.

  • Delegates at Iraq's National Conference elected the members of a new Iraqi legislative body.

  • Embattled leaders Ariel Sharon and Yasser Arafat rebuffed demands from their own backers on Thursday, holding steadfast to positions that pose great political risks.

  • Israel declared psychological war on hunger-striking Palestinian prisoners on Monday, saying it would barbecue meat outside their cells to try to break their spirit.

  • The John Kerry campaign filed a complaint at the Federal Election Commission after the so-called Swift Boat Veterans for Truth group aired TV ads blasting Kerry's involvement in the Vietnam-era antiwar movement.
