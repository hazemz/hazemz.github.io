Sylvia Graduates from the University of Vienna

Hennings return to Sweden after hosting a reception in Vienna

Dr. Sylvia Henning graduated from the University of Vienna in a ceremony held on Wednesday, February 11, 2004.  The ceremony was attended by Mona, Gero, Dag, and Helene, who also hosted a reception later that day at the Palais Pallavicini in downtown Vienna.  The Hennings returned to Sweden on Saturday, February 14.  "It was a nice ceremony at the university," said Mona in her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief,  "and the speech of the professor was in Latin language.  But we understood that Sylvia really graduated as he gave her the degree."

Tarek in Baghdad

This is Tarek's second trip to Iraq after the war

Tarek left to Baghdad on Tuesday, February 10, 2004, on his second business trip to Iraq after the war.  Tarek's trip is expected to last for about a week.  What should be mentioned is that Tarek's first visit to Iraq was between July 1 and July 6, 2003.

Amman Covered in Snow

Snow storm hits Jordan...Zureikats take Sunday off

 

At Marwan and Abla's

 

Left: Downtown Amman \ Right: Abdoun Circle (Sunday, February 15)
Photos courtesy of The Zureikat Weekly Photographer, the Jordan News Agency (Petra), and Jordan Telecom

The first snow storm in this winter season hit Jordan on Saturday and Sunday, February 14 and 15, 2004, causing schools, universities, and businesses to close on Sunday.  No damages to Zureikat homes were reported to The Zureikat Weekly before releasing this issue.  The effect of the cold front causing the snowfall weakened Sunday evening, and the Jordan Meteorological Department said that there was little chance of snowfall on Monday.  In an e-mail she sent to the Editor in Chief, Reema said, "Of course, both Harran and Alia are on top of the world.  Cora has been squeaking with joy and amazement ever since she saw the snow!!  She's been taking pictures the whole time."

 

4.9 Magnitude Earthquake Shakes Jordan...
No Damages or Casualties

School students return home shortly after the earthquake

An earthquake measuring 4.9 on the Richter scale hit Jordan and surrounding countries on Wednesday, February 11, 2004, at about 10:15am.  No damages or casualties were reported; however, due to widespread panic that followed the earthquake, the Ministry of Education asked all schools in Jordan to take the rest of the day off.

Omar Returns Home after 3-Day Trip to Syria

Omar left to Damascus with some of his friends on Wednesday, February 11, 2004.  He returned to Amman on Friday, February 13.  Omar is currently in his mid-year break and will resume classes next week.

Zureikats Celebrate 2 Birthdays

Alia: My birthday this year was unique - to say the least!!

 

Zureikats celebrated Basil's birthday on Thursday, February 12, 2004, and Alia's birthday on Saturday, February 14 (the day the snow storm hit).  "My birthday this year was unique - to say the least," said Alia in her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief.  "I was meant to have a birthday dinner at home, but it got cancelled because of the snow...Still, I had a great day.  Tarek called today [Saturday] at 7:30am from Iraq to wish me a happy birthday, and the rest of the familia called / sent flowers.  Abla Z. came for lunch before the snow storm hit!!"  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish both Basil and Alia very happy birthdays and all the best.
