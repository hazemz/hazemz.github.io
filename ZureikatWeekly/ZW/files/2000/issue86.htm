Pre-Christmas Mansaf for Zureikat Students after their Return Home

Reem returned home on Sunday, December 17, and Samer returned home on Thursday, December 21.  Reem and Samer were the last to return home after Talal, Tarek, Alia, and Amer, who all returned during the last two weeks.  Celebrating this family reunion, Marwan and Abla N. hosted the Zureikat students on a pre-Christmas Mansaf.  The lunch was held on Friday, December 22, 2000, and was attended by Abu Harran, Harran, Reema, Amer, Alia, Kareem, Amal, Ziad, Reem, Samer, Ayman, Talal, Bashar, Omar, Yacoub S., Dr. Yousef Nusseir and his family, and Lama Bashour, Abla N.'s niece from Lebanon.

Hala Appointed Director of International and Public Relations after Major Changes in Jordan Television

Minister of Information held a press conference on Saturday announcing all the changes

Jordanian Minister of Information, Taleb Rifa'i, held a press conference in Amman on Saturday, December 23, 2000, announcing major changes in Jordan Television.  The changes include merging JTV's channels 1, 2, 3, and satellite channel into one, super, 24-hour channel.  These changes will take effect on January 1, 2001.  Hala, Director of Channel 2, which will be closed, was appointed as the new Director of International and Public Relations.  Hala is a member of the Strategic Planning Committee and the Local Productions Committee for the new channel.  In a phone call with The Zureikat Weekly, Hala said that she was 100% for this decision and that it should have been taken by previous administrations three or four years ago due to JTV's financial problems.  She also indicated that these changes are only temporary and may last for as little as one year until "we get back on our feet".

Dana's First Story Airs on JTV

The story was about a new website for Arab women launched by the UNIFEM in Amman

Dana's first story aired on JTV on Friday, December 22, 2000.  In her story, Dana, who was appointed in JTV two weeks ago, talked about a new website for Arab women (Arab Women Connect) launched by the UNIFEM in Amman last week.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to congratulate Dana on her first story and wish her all the best in the future.
