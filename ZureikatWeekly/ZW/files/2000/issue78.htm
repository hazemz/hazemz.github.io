Abla Appointed Director of the Jubilee School

Abla N. was appointed Director of the Jubilee School in Amman on Thursday, October 26.  She will be starting her new job on Sunday, October 29, 2000.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to congratulate Abla on her new post and wish her all the best.  Following is some information about the Jubilee School from its website:

"The Jubilee School is an independent, residential, co-educational secondary school providing a three-year program for outstanding students with a strong commitment to scholarship.  The project was first announced in 1977, during Jordan's Silver Jubilee celebrations of King Hussien's accession to the throne, as a tribute to His Majesty.

...The Jubilee School provides equitable educational opportunities for outstanding students, with a special emphasis on those from developed areas of the country.  The School believes that these talented youngsters represent a national wealth that should be carefully nurtured.  By offering them a unique learning experience and a curriculum tailored to their special needs and capabilities, it seeks to develop future leaders committed to serving their country and capable of addressing the constantly shifting challenges of our region and the world.

Furthermore, as a national center for excellence in education, the Jubilee School sponsors a variety of enrichment and training courses in concert with other institutions, both public and private.  Its innovative approach to learning contributes to improved national educational standards."

The Jubilee School was part of the Noor Al-Hussein Foundation but is now part of the King Hussein Foundation.  Abla N. is the second director for this school after Dr. Fathi Jarwan, who was director since the establishment of the school in 1993.

Harran and Reema return from D.C.

Reema: Harran was the only Arab who Attended

the Training Course on Medical Intervention

"I enjoyed touring Capital Hill, the (LOC), the Smithsonian Mall"

   

Harran and Reema returned home after visiting Washington D.C., where Harran attended a conference and a training course on Medical Intervention.  In a phone call with The Zureikat Weekly, Reema said that Harran was the only Arab who attended the training course on Medical Intervention.  In the phone call, Reema also said, "I also enjoyed touring Capital Hill, the Library of the Congress, the Smithsonian Mall, and most of all, Georgetown."  Harran and Reema had left Amman on Sunday, October 15, and returned on Monday, October 23, 2000.

Ziad Returns from a Three-Day Visit to Baghdad

Ziad left Amman on October 24 to attend a conference in Iraq

Ziad returned from a three-day visit to Baghdad, Iraq in which he attend a conference there on Friday, October 27.  Ziad had left Amman (by land) on Tuesday, October 24, 2000.
