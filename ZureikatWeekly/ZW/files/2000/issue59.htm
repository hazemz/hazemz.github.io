Dana Graduates from AUC with a Masters Degree in TV Journalism

The ceremony was attended by Marwan, Abla, Hazem, Reema, Alia

The Annual Awards Dinner was held before the graduation on June 13

Dana in one of the stories she had done in her second and last year at AUC

Dana graduated from the Kamal Adham Center for Television Journalism at the American University in Cairo (AUC) with a Masters degree in TV journalism.  The June 14 commencement ceremony was attended by Marwan, Abla N., Hazem, Reema, and Alia, who visited Cairo this week (More on this visit below).  The Annual Awards Dinner for the Adham Center was held on June 13, a night before the commencement ceremony.  In this dinner, Dana, along with her 9 classmates, got two gifts from the center.  The speaker in the awards dinner was president of Al-Jazeera Satellite Channel, while the speaker in the June 14 commencement ceremony was the Egyptian Minister of Finance.  Zureikats from all around the world called Dana and visited her to congratulate her on this great achievement.  Mona sent an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly to congratulate Dana.  "We would like to extend our congratulations to Dana on this fantastic achievement," she said.  The Zureikat Weekly would also like to congratulate Dana on her graduation and wish her all the best and a bright and successful future.  What should be mentioned is that Dana and her classmate, Effat Kamel, got an internship award from Showtime.  This award includes three months of training in Showtime - London, airfare, accommodation, and a monthly salary.  Dana is expected to leave for London in the beginning of July after she returns home some time this week (probably on Wednesday, June 21, 2000).  The names of the two winners of this award, Dana and Effat, were also announced in the Annual Awards Dinner by Professor Abdallah Schleifer.  Please note that cameras were not allowed in the June 14 commencement ceremony; the pictures of the commencement above were taken outside the hall.  However, AUC photographers have taken pictures of the ceremony, and we will be showing them to you as soon as they arrive to Amman.

Something about Dana in the Adham Center's In Focus Magazine and its website:

Showtime announces two graduate internships

Dana Zureikat and Effat Kamel

JUNE 13, 2000 Two of the graduates being honored at tonight's annual awards dinner have been selected as the first interns in a new yearly program for Adham Center graduates.

Gulf DTH/Showtime CEO Peter Einstein announced the establishment of the internships during his appointment as an associate , or honorary faculty member, of the Adham Center. The first two beneficiaries of the Showtime internships are Effat Kamel, who served this year as the AUC TV executive producer, and Dana Zureikat. Both will be working with Showtime in London for three months starting in July.

Both Dana and Effat see this program as an ideal opportunity for moving from their Adham Center training into their careers. "Hopefully I will learn about various aspects of the Showtime operation, learn about the working cycle of a television company and develop a thorough understanding of the nature of this business," Effat said. "I also hope that during my stay in London, I will be able to make connections that might lead me down a great career path."

"The Adham Center not only educates and prepares its students to be qualified TV journalists but goes a step further in guaranteeing them a secure career path by putting them in contact with the  industry's main players," Dana said.

 

In Focus and the Adham Center's website also included a short paragraph about each of the center's graduates.  Here is the one about Dana:

Dana M. Zureikat will start a three-month internship this July with Showtime in London. She previously worked for two years as a producer for ART in Amman and Jerusalem. She was responsible for a children's program, research work, and various live productions. In Jerusalem she worked on a special program on the resistance movement in literature.

Zureikats Return from a 

Historic 5-Day Visit to Egypt

Marwan, Abla, Hazem, Reema, and Alia made the visit to attend Dana's graduation

After attending Dana's graduation on June 14, Reema and Alia returned home on June 15, and Marwan, Abla N., and Hazem returned home on June 16.  Marwan, Abla N., Dana, and Hazem had left Amman on June 11, while Reema and Alia had left on June 13, 2000.  During this historic family visit to Egypt, Reema, Alia, and Hazem visited the Pharohic Village, which includes ancient statues and shows the way the Pharos lived, the Pyramids of Giza, the Ahmad Shawqi Museum, the house of the famous Arabic poet, Ahmad Shawqi, and the Egyptian Museum, which includes more than 15 million pieces.  Also, during their stay, Marwan, Abla N., and Hazem met with some of their friends in Egypt and with Abla's relatives there.

Tarek and Bashar Leave to the United States

The team will first go to Nashville, Tennessee then to Virginia

  

"We have opened Southwestern - Jordan," said Tarek in a phone call with The Zureikat Weekly last week after the American Embassy in Jordan had agreed to issue visas for six Jordanian students, including Bashar, to work in the United States for three months for the Southwestern Company with Tarek as their manager.  This is Tarek's third year in the Southwestern Company selling books.  The team, which includes all-in-all 25 members, will first go to Nashville, Tennessee for intensive training on door-to-door sales.  Then, they will all go to Virginia to work there.  Tarek had left Edinburgh on June 15, while Bashar and the other Jordanian members of the team left on June 17, 2000.
