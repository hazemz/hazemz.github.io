Schleifer: She could become MBC's own Christiane Amanpour

Dana Returns to Amman, Tuesday

Showtime's logo.  Showtime is a well-known satellite TV & radio network.

Dana, who is currently studying at the American University in Cairo, won the Showtime Internship Award.  The formal announcement will be made in an awards dinner on June 12 in Cairo.  President of the Adham Center, Abdullah Schleifer will announce the two winners, Dana and her friend Effat Kamel.  The Zureikat Weekly has received the following e-mail sent from Schleifer to MBC's Moataz Demerdash in London:

Dear Moataz:

It’s June 12th (the awards dinner).  Please do come.  Everyone would be honored and delighted, and I will put you up on the podium with me and the VIPs.  Incidentally, we recently made Peter Einstein of Showtime an Associate, and Peter was so proud of the appointment that Showtime pr people are placing big stories with photographs of himself and the Provost shaking hands and with Peter saying very nice things about the Center and announcing a Showtime Summer Intern program for an outstanding Adham Center graduate – three months in London, Showtime pays airfare, accommodation and modest salary.  Actually, when I formally announce the winners at the dinner, I will be able to announce that Showtime has decided to increase the Internship awards to two outstanding graduates.

One of those two winners is an exceptionally good producer-reporter.  She is from Jordan, and she is a natural correspondent and shoots her own superb pictures as well and, of course, can edit like all of our graduates.  I believe she would make a great MBC correspondent, after the Showtime Internship ends.  She is very attracted to the hard news, confrontation-coverage of competitive international journalism – she could become MBC's own Christiane Amanpour.  She would love to have a shot at the MBC Jerusalem correspondent slot, and, as an East Banker, I believe she could be sufficiently detached to do a great job and get full cooperation from the Israelis, as well as, obviously, from the Palestinians and the Jordanians.

Her name is Dana Zureikat, and I will be happy to introduce you to her at the dinner.  If you want her demonstration tape before then, let me know, and I will pass them on to the MBC bureau to send on to you.
 
In case you get more than one trip to Cairo in the next two months, you might also consider coming in on May 15th when we have the graduate projects presentation. But if you get only one trip then please make it the Annual Awards Dinner.

Yours, Abdullah

The Zureikat Weekly would like to congratulate Dana on winning this award and wish her all the best.

On the other hand, Dana is expected to return home to Amman on a brief visit on Tuesday, April 4, 2000.

Kareem Wins 1st Place in 400 M Marathon

Kareem was competing against students from 10,11, and 12th grades

Kareem won 1st place in a 400 M marathon which was held in the Hussein Sports City in Amman last Sunday.  Kareem, who is in 9th grade, was competing with students from 10th,11th, and 12th grades.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to congratulate Kareem on winning first place.  On the other hand, Kareem is expected to participate in a larger competition on April 13, 2000. 

Hazem Sits for UWC Examination

On Saturday, April 1, 2000, Hazem, who applied for the United World Colleges scholarship early this year, sat for the UWC examination in the Jubilee School in Amman.  Some 20 people from the 500 who sat for the exam today will be chosen for the interview.  Then, 7 from Jordan will get the scholarship.
