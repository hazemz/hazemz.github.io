Editor in Chief: Hazem Zureikat

51st Issue (1st Anniversary Special)

April 23, 2000

Zureikats Celebrate 

the 1st Anniversary 

of their Internet Newsletter

It has been exactly one year since the first issue of The Zureikat Weekly was released on April 23, 1999.  On this occasion, the Editor in Chief would like to thank all Zureikats for supporting him in releasing 51 issues in the past year, either by sending their news or just by reading The Zureikat Weekly.

Editor in Chief Receives Letters on this Occasion

On this glorious occasion, Editor in Chief of The Zureikat Weekly received the following letters:

The Sharaiha Family (in Amman):

     

The Sharaiha family would like to extend its heartiest congratulations on the eve of the first anniversary of The Zureikat Weekly.  Chief editor of the magazine, Hazem Zureikat, has done an excellent job during the last 50 weeks, for without his undying hard work and perseverance, this magazine would not have flourished.  We just want to point out that besides gathering news about the Zureikats and those related to them, it certainly has succeeded in bringing the family together, specially so for those who are overseas, and eager to hear our news. Well done Hazem, all the best, we are proud of your achievement.

 

Hala:

I just want to tell you that I still remember the first edition of The Zureikat Weekly when I was in the States and how lovely it was to have the news of the family far away.  I think that you have done an excellent job so far.

 

Reema:

At first, I thought that The Zureikat Weekly interests mainly the Zureikat children.  Little did I know that eventually us parents would get hooked on it too!!  I thank you GREAT Hazem for your continuous perseverance and creativity in making The Zureikat Weekly a source of "great expectations" for all.

 

Bashar:

Once again, Hazem Zureikat amazes us with his ingenuity, maturity, intelligence and perseverance.  It is an honor to the Zureikats to have a member of his caliber.

 

Alia:

There is nothing better than waking up every Sunday to The Zureikat Weekly's new edition.  Over  the last year, this has become a habit - and a good one too!! -  To those of us studying abroad, it is a source of comfort.  We never feel like we're missing out on the usual family happenings, or the weekly cartoons!!  A brilliant idea.  'Ya'eesh' Hazem Zureikat!!

 

Tarek:

The Zureikat Weekly made my life at university a lot less harder than it could have been.  Whenever I think about home and try to imagine what everyone is doing, I just open your website and read the news of the family, and then it doesn't seem like I'm that far away.  I am thankful for The Zureikat Weekly's editor and staff for providing this service.

Reem:

I think The Zureikat Weekly is a very good way to keep family ties strong.  Because of the way things are, we hardly ever meet together anymore, so it's one way where we are always in touch!!

 

Bana:

The Zureikat Weekly is something all Zureikats look forward to at the end of the week.  Its got us all on the web, eager to read the latest news that concern us and the family.  I just want to thank Hazem for his relentless energy, enthusiasm, and effort in the past year.  His creativity and hard work transcended his PC desk and connected Zureikats - both home and away - through this great newsletter.  Thank you Hazem, and keep up the good work!


Talal:

First of all, I would like to congratulate the chief editor Hazem for his splendid job during this year in publishing the weekly on time and meeting the deadlines punctually.  What I think is that it is a wonderful idea, since the beginning, and I would like to thank him a lot, for that it has made me feel close to my family, which is the most important thing a person needs when he's abroad.

 

Kareem:

To begin, I would like to thank all the Zureikat members of the family for their constant cooperation.  They are the reason for success to this newsletter.  They have filled these numerous issues with events that brought joy and content to the family, but also gave us adverse events that shocked all our Zureikat members.

The Zureikat Weekly has joined all the family together in an unbreakable bond.  It has brought life to our days.  Family members, old or young, gather up to the computer wanting to read the latest issue.  Even "Jiddo Abu Harran" wants to hear about the news through the Zureikat Weekly.

Hazem, I would like to congratulate you on this wonderful creation.  It has done all that Mrs. Reema Zureikat wanted, cut short on overseas calls!!

 

Yacoub S.:

Thank you Hazem because The Zureikat Weekly lets me know the news of the Zureikat family.  Thank you.

Family Gathers at Abla's Celebrating her 50th Birthday

Marwan, Reema, Reem, Alia, and Mona dancing

Left: Bana singing while playing on the piano - Right: Zureikats clapping while Bana played the song currently playing in the background

 

Left: The family applauding for Bana - Right: Amal and Mona listening to Bana

Abla blowing her candle

Dancing and singing filled the Shomalis' residence on Saturday night as Zureikats celebrated another special occasion, Abla Z.'s 50th birthday, which coincides on Sunday, April 23, 2000.  During the dinner party, which was attended by some guests including Jumana, Dian's sister, Sang, Reem's friend, and Salwa, Bana's friend, Abla received a gift from her family.  Now, she can be reached at any time on her new mobile at (962-79-661-203).  Zureikats also danced and sang while Bana played the piano.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Abla a very happy birthday and all the best.

Zureikats Celebrate Three Other Occasions

     

Zureikats also celebrated three other occasions, Yacoub S.'s birthday on April 18 and Abla N.'s birthday on April 20.  The family will be also celebrating Mona's birthday on April 25, 2000.  On these occasions, The Zureikat Weekly would like to wish Yacoub, Abla, and Mona each a very happy birthday.  On the other hand, Mona told The Zureikat Weekly that she will be celebrating her birthday this year in Amman.  Mona is expected to return to Sweden on Wednesday, April 26, 2000.

 

Zureikats Visit Abla on Her Birthday

Yacoub invites his classmates to a small party

Zureikats at Marwan's celebrating Abla's birthday

Celebrating Abla N.'s 52nd birthday, family members gathered at Marwan's house right after the Cyber Kids family held a surprise party for Abla on Thursday, April 20, 2000.  Yacoub also invited his classmates to a small party on the same day.  The family also visited Abla Z.'s house on Yacoub's 10th birthday celebrating this occasion.

 Abla Gets the Highest Number of Votes in Elections

Abla is now a member of the YWCA's board

On the same day of her 52nd birthday, Abla N. got the highest number of votes in the YWCA's elections.  Abla is now a member of the YWCA's board.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to congratulate Abla on winning the elections and wish her all the best in her new post.

Mona updates us on the latest from Sweden

Dag Leaves for France...Helene Returns to Sweden

Sylvia is also expected to return to Sweden on April 24

Helene, who is currently studying at Bocconi University in Milan, returned to Sweden on April 20, to spend her Easter holiday there.  Sylvia is also expected to return from Vienna on April 24.  On the other hand, Dag left Sweden for France on a vacation on Saturday, April 22, 2000.

Reem Returns...Sang Arrives on a 1 Week Visit

Reem returned to Amman last week to spend her Easter holiday.  A few days later, Sang, Reem's Korean friend, arrived in Amman on a one week visit.  Reem and Sang are expected to return to London on Wednesday, April 26, 2000.

Omar Prepares for Tawjihi Finals

Samer Prepares for IB Exams

  

Omar, who is in 12th grade, finished school on Wednesday, April 19, and will be starting the school's final exams on April 24, 2000.  The school's finals will finish on May 23.  Omar will then sit for the government's Tawjihi final exams starting June 15, 2000.  On the other hand, Samer, who is also in his last year, is currently preparing for the IB exams which will be held between May 2 and May 17, 2000.
