179th Issue - October 27, 2002

Reema Returns Home after Undergoing Operation

Reema underwent hysteroscopy a day after arriving back from London

Alia: She's fine and recovering well now

A day after returning from London, Reema was hospitalized and underwent a gynecological operation, a hysteroscopy, on Sunday, October 20.  Reema left the hospital on Tuesday, October 22, 2002.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Alia said, "She's fine and recovering well, now, after spending two days in hospital."  In her e-mail, Alia also said, "As a sort of home welcoming gesture, several members of the family had dinner at our house on Wednesday [October 23].  The menu consisted of Greek Salad, Mtabbal, etc, and Farroujna - yum!"  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Reema a speedy recovery and all the best.

Dana and Abla Leave to Washington...Monday

Hazem will meet Abla and Dana in DC next weekend

   

Dana and Abla N. will be leaving to Washington DC on Monday, October 28, where Dana will be starting her new job at the Voice of America on Friday, November 1.  On the other hand, Hazem, who is currently studying at Macalester College in St. Paul, Minnesota, is expected to leave to Washington DC on Friday, November 1, to meet Abla and Dana there.  He will be returning to Minnesota on Monday, November 4, 2002.  Sources told The Zureikat Weekly that Abla might also be returning to Amman on the same day (Monday, November 4).

 

Next Issue to be Released on Monday, Nov. 4

Since the Editor in Chief will be visiting Washington DC next weekend, the 180th issue of The Zureikat Weekly will be released on Monday, November 4, 2002, instead of Sunday.

A few days after her return from Hemel Hempstead,

Alia: London was great, although it was quite tough!

"There's no drastic deterioration, but I do need to work harder to maintain what I have achieved so far."

A few days after returning from a week of intensive physiotherapy at Hemel Hempstead in the north of London, Alia sent an e-mail to the Editor in Chief.  In her e-mail, Alia said, "London was great, although it was quite tough!  Very intense... Basically, there's no drastic deterioration, but I do need to work harder to maintain what I have achieved so far.  Harder said than done, I know, but, inshallah, we'll get there.  Besides the physio, I also managed to see my friends from university!  We had a mini reunion, which was lots of fun!I  Also got to see my cousins and uncle, which was great!  Now, I'm back at work, which is kind of depressing!!"

Amal and Ziad Return Home

 

After visiting Geneva and London, Amal and Ziad returned home on Friday, October 25, 2002.

Hala Returns from Cairo, Holds Party for Dalia

Hala, who was attending a EuroMed conference in Cairo, returned home on Monday, October 21, 2002.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Hala said, "Since I came from Cairo I was very busy, because my friend Nancy, who is the head of our project on archive, came with me, and I was very busy with her and her team until Friday night.  My work is taking too much time now with the new Director General.  I am involved some how in the programs again."  Regarding Dalia's birthday, which was on Saturday, October 19, 2002, Hala said, "Dalia's friends did a very nice surprise party on October 20, while I was in Cairo, so, to say thank you to them, we celebrated again Dalia's birthday yesterday [Saturday, October 26].  I had almost 25 persons at home. She was very happy."

Abu Harran and Tarek Visit the Jordan Valley

 

Abu Harran and Tarek visited Harran and Marwan's farms in the Jordan Valley on Friday, October 25, 2002.  After their trip, Abu Harran and Tarek had lunch at Abla Z.'s.

Leanne Sends an E-mail to The Zureikat Weekly

Leanne sent the following e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly last week:

Hi everyone,

It's been a while since I've written to the weekly.

It was great to finally meet Dag in Amman and to see Auntie Mona and Helene again!  I'm looking forward to one day meeting the rest of the family!!

As for the decision to appoint Auntie Abla as a photographer for the Weekly, Ammo Marwan, I'm afraid I'm going to have to disagree with you on this one.  I witnessed first hand Auntie Abla's skills at a dinner one night at Ammo Harran's, and, in her defense, I must say that she is truly a pro!!

I know I've already said this before, but every time I read the Weekly, I am more and more impressed by Hazem and his ability to connect the Zureikats on a "virtual" level!  I'm sure the weekly will continue to do so for generations to come!!

Abla and Abla Pickle 10 Kilos of Umm Al-Amad Olives

 

Abla and Abla pickled 10 kilos of Umm Al-Amad olives last week.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Bana said, "Abla Z and Abla N. got together at the Shomali kitchen on Saturday [October 26] evening to pickle 10 kilos of Umm Al-Amad olives.  This is the second batch of olives that Abla Z. has pickled so far this year.  Soon the olives will ripen and turn black and the whole Shomali family will engage in the annual olive picking.  Volunteers from the Zureikat family are more than welcome to participate!!  And, according to Jiddo's salary plan, Basil will pay volunteers "Girsh 3al sei3a"."
