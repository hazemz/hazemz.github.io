142nd Issue - February 3, 2002

After completing all the requirements on Saturday

Bana Now has a BA in Economics

Bana's university years are finally over!  After completing all the requirements on Saturday, February 2, 2002, Bana now has a BA degree in Economics from the Jordan University.  Bana is expected to graduate next summer with the highest GPA in her department.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Bana on her BA degree and wish her all the best in the future.

Tarek Wins Two Awards at the Annual 'Freddies'

Tarek, who is currently working for the Southwestern Company, won two awards at the 'Freddies', which is an annual awards ceremony held in Europe this year for the first time.  The first, and most important, award Tarek won is the Student's Excellence Award, which is awarded based on the overall performance in the fields of recruiting, sales, curricular and extracurricular activities, etc.  Tarek was nominated for this award last year.  The second award, which was awarded to Tarek's organization, Al-Nashama, is the Biggest In-School Organization Award, which is awarded to the most productive organization headed by a student.  Mohammad Atout, a member of Tarek's Al-Nashama organization, also won the Top In-School Recruiter Award.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Tarek and his organization on these awards and wish them all the best in the future.

Mona Visits Amman...Wednesday

Mona and the Swedish Embassy in Amman will be preparing for an event commemorating the late Astrid Lindgren

Mona will be arriving in Amman on Wednesday, February 6, 2002.  During her weeklong stay, Mona and the Swedish Embassy will be preparing for an event to be held in Amman commemorating the late Astrid Lindgren.  This event will be held sometime next April.

Astrid Lindgren Dies in Stockholm at Age 94

Astrid Lindgren, a Swedish author whom many of her books were translated to Arabic by Dar Al-Mona, died in Stockholm on Monday, January 28, 2002, at age 94.

Marwan Returns from Sudan

Marwan returned from Sudan on Friday, February 1, 2002, after making a three-day business trip to the capital, Khartoum.  On his way back, Marwan spent a day in Cairo.

Amal and Ziad Return from Egypt

 

Amal and Ziad returned from Egypt on Friday, February 1.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Amal said, "We left to Cairo on Monday, January 28, and spent the night there.  The next day, we left to Ghardaqa where Ziad attended a three-day conference.  After that, we returned to Cairo on Friday, February 1, 2002, had lunch, spent a few hours, and returned back to Amman in the evening."

Zureikat School Students Start the 2nd Semester

Zureikat school students, and the Ablas, will be starting the second semester of the 2001-2002 school year on Sunday, February 3, 2002.  Kareem, who studies at the Amman Baccalaureate School, already started school last week.

Bashar Returns from Aqaba

Bashar returned from a business trip in Aqaba on Wednesday, January 30.  Bashar had left Amman on Sunday, January 27, 2002.

Dana's 18th Story Airs on JTV

Dana's 18th story aired on JTV on Friday, February 1, 2002.  This week's story was about Her Majesty Queen Rania's participation in the World Economic Forum in New York.

Zureikats Celebrate Reema's Birthday

Zureikats celebrated Reema's birthday on Monday, January 28, 2002.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Reema a very happy birthday and all the best.
