154th Issue - April 28, 2002

The Zureikat Weekly Celebrates its 3rd Anniversary

Editor in Chief receives congratulations on this occasion

Zureikats celebrated the third anniversary of their internet newsletter, The Zureikat Weekly, on Tuesday, April 23, 2002.  On this occasion, the Editor in Chief received the following letters of congratulation:

(In the order that they were received)

Samer

I would like to thank Hazem for his tremendous achievement.  Managing a family newsletter for 3 years requires a lot of responsibility and dedication, bringing the latest family news before us.  Furthermore, I would like to congratulate Hazem upon earning those merit-based scholarships.  HAZEM, WE ARE PROUD OF YOU!!!!  P.S. Thanks to KHALO MARWAN FOR BEING THE NUMBER 1 SPONSOR TO THE ZUREIKAT WEEKLY.

Talal

Well, Hazem, what can we say.  A brilliant idea, committed and endured for 3 years...I say, not bad!!! Only few people have such entrepreneurship qualities.  Got to hand it to you, congratulations.  Good on you.  I hope this newsletter continues to be a legendary quality of our family history.

Amal

Your magazine reflects commitment, perseverance, hard work, which only comes from a person with rare abilities.  You have proved to be a successful journalist, a warmhearted person who managed to keep all family news accessible to every member of the Zureikat.  Well done Chief!!!

Ayman

To tell you the truth, I never expected this to go far, but thanks to your glaring perseverance and strapping will power.  All the Zureikats around the world enjoy this lovely newsletter.  I for one never realized the true meaning of it until I moved to study in the States, of course I used to enjoy it in Jordan to see other people's news, but I'm more into it now, and I appreciate it more and more by the week.  Keep up the good work man.

Reem

I know I've been really slack.  But I do want to say how much I appreciate the "Weekly".  I've been abroad for five years now, and yet for most of the time, I feel I haven't missed a thing, with your pictures and captions...Congratulations on the third anniversary.

Mona and Family

I find it extremely difficult finding words to describe this enormous project that you have been running for the past 3 years with great success.  Dear Hazem, you have connected the family together especially members of the younger generation.  We in Sweden are very thankful to you and wish you, together with The Zureikat Weekly, continued success and prosperity.

Reema and Alia

 

Three years later, the Zureikat Weekly is still achieving its goal of uniting the family.  Once again, we thank you, Hazem, for all your efforts and perseverance.
P.S. Reema: "Had it not been for the Weekly, I wouldn't have learned how to open a computer, let alone use one!"

The Zureikat Weekly...

Where will it go from here?

Will we ever celebrate a 4th anniversary??

As the Editor in Chief prepares to leave for college next fall, a very important question comes to the fore: What is the future of The Zureikat Weekly?  Many suggested assigning a new editor or creating an editing committee to take over.  Others suggested turning the ZW's website into a message board, where Zureikats can send their own news and read other news.  If you have any suggestions regarding this matter, please tell us in the next few weeks, as we will be making the final decision soon.

Samer Breaks his Hand after Martial Arts Injury

Samer, who is currently studying in the US, broke his hand last week.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Amal said, "Samer broke his hand while playing martial arts."  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Samer a speedy recovery.

Mona Arrives in Amman

Mona arrived in Amman on Wednesday, April 24, to participate in the Swedish Children's Literature Exhibition entitled "Meet the Child".  She is expected to return to Sweden on Tuesday, April 30, 2002.

Talal Returns to London after Spring Break

Talal returned to London on Tuesday, April 23, 2002.  During his three-week spring vacation to Amman, Talal underwent a successful operation and visited Aqaba with Amal for three days.

Amal and Ziad in Lebanon after Ziad Visits Syria

 

Ziad left to Syria on Tuesday, April 23, to attend a conference there.  He then left to Lebanon on Thursday, April 25 where he was met by Amal.  Amal and Ziad are expected to return home on Sunday, April 28, 2002.

A few days after returning to Edinburgh from Bristol

Tarek: I was Selling Books in England for Three Weeks

A few days after returning to Edinburgh from Bristol, Tarek sent the following e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly: "Since I left Jordan, I was selling books in England for three weeks.  Since I will be managing people selling in the UK this summer, my company thought it would be a good idea to sell during the Easter break.  So I joined 6 other people in that same position and was selling in Buckinghamshire.  I had a tough start, but eventually, I did really well there.  I was nicknamed the Escalator, because I was improving everyday until the very last day when I felt could sell as much as I can in the US.  I would like to say happy birthday to my mom, and congratulations for Amer and Leanne.  Ogbali."
 

Abla and Abla Participate in 11th PSC Conference

 

Abla and Abla participated in the 11th Annual Conference for the Private Schools Council which was held at the Modern Montessori School in Amman on Thursday and Friday, April 25 and 26, 2002.  During the two-day conference, Abla N. presented a paper on the "Schoolwide Enrichment Program" on Thursday and had a idea-exchanging session on the same subject the next day.  Abla Z. also had an idea-exchanging session on Friday entitled "Revision: Breaking the Code.  Helping Students Edit Their Own Writing".  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Abla Z. said, "I was approached by some teacher who attended the session asking me for help in their schools."

Hassan Returns from Short Trip to Syria

Hassan returned home on Friday, April 26, 2002, after making a three-day business trip to Syria.

Dana's 32nd Story Airs on JTV

Dana's 32nd story aired on JTV on Friday, April 26, 2002.  This week's story was about an agreement signed between the Jordanian Ministry of Waqf and Islamic Affairs and the UNICEF office in Jordan.
