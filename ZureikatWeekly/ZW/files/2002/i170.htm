170th Issue - August 18, 2002

'A New Era'

Correspondents send their news as the Weekly prepares for major changes

Editor in Chief appoints two photographers

Hazem and Abla leave to Minnesota Friday

No issue to be released on the 25th

     

   

The new ZW correspondents sent their news for the first time as part of the major changes The Zureikat Weekly is undergoing as it prepares to move to the US next month.  The Editor in Chief is expected to leave to Minnesota, accompanied by Abla N., on Friday, August 23.  No issue will be released on Sunday, August 25, 2002.  On the other hand, the Editor in Chief appointed Bana and Kareem as the official Zureikat Weekly photographers.  They will both be responsible for sending pictures of family events in Jordan.

A note from the Editor in Chief:

The Editor in Chief would like to stress once more that having correspondents DOES NOT at all mean that other Zureikats cannot send their news.  We welcome, and even recommend, Zureikats who are not correspondents, especially those who are living\studying away from their families, to send their own news.

Dian and the Children Return

     

After spending almost one month in Syria, Dian, Sabine, Nadine, and Yacoub Z. returned home on Monday, August 12.  Dian and the children had left to Syria on July 15, 2002.

Amer Leaves...Kareem Returns

 

After spending two days in Amman for an interview with the US ambassador, Amer returned to Dublin on Monday, August 12.  On the other hand, Kareem, who spent three weeks in Oxford on a pre-IB course, returned home on Saturday, August 17, 2002.

Marwan, Abla, Abla, Basil Return from Vacations

     

Marwan and Abla N., who left to Marmaris, Turkey on Monday, August 5, returned home on Monday, August 12.  Upon their return, Marwan and Abla said that they enjoyed their trip very much; they described Marmaris as a "beautiful" place.  Also, Abla Z. and Basil who left to Beirut on Sunday, August 11, returned home on Wednesday, August 14, 2002.   The four-day trip to Lebanon was a gift from the Shomali kids for Basil and Abla on the occasion of their 25th anniversary.  Despite the heat, humidity, and the "nassabeen" (quote Basil) Lebanese, Abla and Basil enjoyed the trip very much.  They spent the days shopping, and by night, they relaxed at the "Rawsheh" while eating seafood.

Mona and Gero Enjoy the "Midnight Sun"

Mona: I was 500km from the North Pole

Mona and Gero returned to Stockholm after spending five days touring Sweden.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Mona said, "I was 500km from the North Pole.  Gero and I took the train which travels inside Sweden for five continuous days.  We passed the Polar Circle, and we saw the midnight sun.  The light was different from the usual light we see during the day."

Dag, Sylvia, and Helene All in Stockholm

Sylvia is preparing to leave to Vienna

Dag, Sylvia, and Helene are all in Stockholm now.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Mona said, "Helene is living with us for a while as she is looking again for a flat of her own.  Sylvia is also home before she returns to Vienna to start working in internal medicine at Vienna University Hospital, and Dag, who is looking for another job, is helping me in datorizing Dar Al-Mona so that it becomes easy to run the business."

Ayman and Hazem Undergo "Wisdom Tooth Extractions"

 

Ayman and Hazem both underwent surgical extractions of impacted wisdom teeth on Tuesday, August 13, 2002.  Both operations were conducted at Dr. Amal's clinic.  This is Hazem second wisdom tooth extraction in two months.  Amal described Hazem's second extraction as being "very tough".

Dana's 48th Story Airs on JTV

Dana's 48th story aired on JTV on Friday, August 16, 2002.  This week's story was about the First Economic Forum for Ambassadors held in Amman last week.

Burhan Turns 30

Mona: I hope Burhan will make the difference one day

Burhan, the character we have seen in many of Dar Al-Mona's books, is turning thirty this year.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Mona said, "Burhan is becoming thirty years old, and the author who is now 60 years old is giving a big celebration for this 90 years together.  And on this occasion, Dar Al-Mona will print again all the titles of Burhan.  So I hope Burhan will make the difference one day."

  • After breaking his leg almost six weeks ago, Talal removed his cast and now has a temporary removable one.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Amal said, "Talal is swimming almost everyday to strengthen his weakened muscles."  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Talal a speedy recovery.

  • Celebrating the beautiful weather in Stockholm, Helene held an "English Drink Party" for her friends.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Mona said, "Today we had a beautiful day in Stockholm.  It has been so the last two weeks.  In Europe, it is raining everywhere, but not in Stockholm, so Helene had all her friends invited to what we call an 'English Drink Party' between 14:00 and 18:00.  Gero and I and Nick left the house to the youth and had a lovely walk downtown Stockholm in this 25 degrees weather.  Very unusual...It is 22:00 hours now and still light outside.  I am sure we will miss these summer days when the winter comes."

  • Bashar is now on a two-week vacation from work.  He is spending it sleeping and seeing his friends...A well deserved vacation for the hardworking Shomali.

Chandra and Jeena...now both in Sri Lanka as Zureikats await their return next month.
