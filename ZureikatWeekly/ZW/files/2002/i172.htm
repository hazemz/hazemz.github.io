172nd Issue - September 8, 2002

Hennings Enjoying the 'Summer of a Lifetime'

Mona: I will be with Gero in Germany next week over the weekend

The Hennings are enjoying a summer Sweden hasn't seen for nearly the past two hundred and fifty years.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Mona said, "We are having still a great summer Sweden has not seen since 1756.  People cannot believe it.  We have a plumb tree which carried fruits since we planted it 17 years ago.  Every year, when it should get ripe the night frost come and the sour taste never changed to sweet.  This year we are enjoying its sugary taste for the first time."  Regarding the Henning's latest news, Mona said, "Dag is enjoying his work, and I had him with Helene for lunch we had a nice little grill party for the four of us.  Sylvia is working from 7 o'clock in the morning and enjoying her patients.  I will be with Gero in Germany next week over the weekend."

Editor in Chief Starts Classes

Hazem is taking 17 credit hours this semester

Macalester College is listed as one of the 12 'hottest' colleges and universities in the US

The Editor in Chief, who arrived in Saint Paul, Minnesota more than two weeks ago, started his classes on Wednesday, September 4.  Hazem is taking four four-credit courses this semester and one one-credit course (a total of 17 credit hours).  The courses are: Computer Science I, Elementary Statistics, Principles of Art, and French 1; the one-credit course is called Quantitative Reasoning.  Hazem's roommate, Peter Anderson-Sprecher, who is from Laramie, Wyoming, arrived in Saint Paul on Saturday, August 31, 2002.  What should be mentioned is that Hazem's college, Macalester College, was listed by Kaplan/Newsweek as one of the 12 'hottest' colleges and universities in the US.  You can read more about this by clicking HERE.

Chandra Return to Amman...Jeena Returns Soon

Before Chandra's return, Amer sent an e-mail wishing Chandra and Jeena a 'very speedy return'

Chandra and Jeena (From The Zureikat Weekly's archive)

After spending two months with her family in Sri Lanka, Chandra returned to Amman on Thursday, September 5, 2002.  Jeena is expected to return soon.  A couple of days before Chandra's return, Amer sent an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly.  In his e-mail, Amer said, "Dear all.  I ask you to join me in wishing Chandra and Jeena a very speedy return to Amman where they are - without a doubt - much needed and missed.  I'm sure life is grinding to a halt without them.  All is well in Dublin."

Whisky and Rocks moved to Anjara

'Tipsy' Joins the Family

Reema calls Tipsy "the first decent dog we've had since Buster"

Harran and the family welcomed a new addition to the family last week, Tipsy Zureikat!  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Alia said, " A week ago, we welcomed the arrival of a new family member, a Golden Retriever named Tipsy.  We bought her from Mr. Bassam Mufleh.  She's beautiful, very calm.  Even Mom loves her!  The two German Shepherds, Whisky and Rocks, have moved to Anjara.  Mom describes Tipsy as the "first decent dog we've had since Buster!"  I guess that's because - or so it seems - her grandmother is at HRH Prince Hassan's!!!  Now, at last, Mom and Dad can stop fighting and Nick, the Hennings' dog, has finally got a cousin!!!!!"
