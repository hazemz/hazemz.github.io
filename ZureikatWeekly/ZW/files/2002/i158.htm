158th Issue - May 26, 2002

The Zureikat Weekly Moves to Minnesota Next Fall!

Considering all the success this newsletter has made, it would be "very sad" to close it

Hazem graduates July 11 and leaves to Minnesota in August

Editor in Chief to appoint "correspondents" soon

For the past few months, the question of what will happen to The Zureikat Weekly in the future has been asked by many.  Today, and after many consultations with family members, the Editor in Chief of The Zureikat Weekly has decided not to suspend the family's newsletter.  The decision came, as the Editor in Chief said, "after considering the success our newsletter has made throughout the past three years".  He said, "It would be very sad, after considering all this success, to even think of closing The Zureikat Weekly."  The Editor in Chief, who will be graduating on July 11 and studying at Macalester College in St. Paul, Minnesota starting next fall, also said that the future Zureikat Weekly would need more support from the family.  He said that he will be appointing correspondents for The Zureikat Weekly around the world to send him the latest news every week starting next September.

Bana Appointed "Pricing Officer" at Fastlink's Marketing Department

Bana was appointed a Pricing Officer in the Marketing Department at Fastlink, the leading mobile phone company in Jordan.  Bana, who got her BA in Economics from the Jordan University last February, started her new job on Tuesday, May 21, 2002.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief just hours before releasing this issue, Bana said that her new job was "challenging" and "rewarding" and includes "a lot of hard work".  At the end of the phone call, Bana said, "I have some advice to the young Zureikats: Enjoy school while it lasts!"  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Bana on her new job and wish her all the best in the future.

"Motto of the Week"

Before speaking with Bana on the phone to get more information about her new job, the Editor in Chief spoke with Abla Z. who refused to give any information regarding Bana's new job.  She said, "Children are individuals, and they should have their own privacy."  She asked us to put this as The Zureikat Weekly's "Motto of the Week".

Amer Gets a New Job at Beaumont

Amer got a new job at Beaumont Hospital in Dublin, the same hospital which he has been working at for the past few months.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Reema said, "Amer got a good job at the same hospital, Beaumont.  He called me and said "I got the job!"."  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Amer on his new job and wish him all the best in the future.

Harran Visits Paris, Returns Home

Harran returned home on Saturday, May 25, after visiting Paris, where he attended a conference.  Harran had left Amman to Paris on Tuesday, May 21, 2002.

Tarek in Edinburgh after Visiting Bradford...Again

"The busiest Zureikat" is still recruiting students for this summer

Tarek returned to Edinburgh on Friday, May 24, 2002, after visiting Bradford.  Tarek, who called himself "the busiest Zureikat", has been making many visits to Bradford in the past few weeks as part of his job to recruit students for this year's summer work.

Dana's 35th Story Airs on JTV

Dana's 35th story aired on JTV on Friday, May 24, 2002.  This week's story was about the no-smoking signs in public places and how many in Jordan don't comply with these signs.

Stay Up-to-Date with The Zureikat Weekly for the Latest World Cup Scores

The Zureikat Weekly will be bringing you the latest scores from the 2002 Fifa World Cup in Korea and Japan starting next week.
