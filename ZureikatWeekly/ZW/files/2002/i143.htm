143rd Issue - February 10, 2002

Jordan Mourns Mu'nis Al-Razzaz

On Friday, February 8, Jordan and the Arab World lost one of their well-known and most respected novelists, Mu'nis Munif Al-Razzaz.  Al-Razzaz, a father of three and a very close friend to the Zureikat family, died in a hospital in Amman at the age of 51.  His funeral was held on Saturday, February 9, 2002.  Our deepest and most sincere condolences go out to Al-Razzaz's family, friends, and all those who loved him...

Mona Arrives in Amman

ZW to hold exclusive interview with Mona soon

Mona at Marwan's house upon her arrival

Mona arrived in Amman on Wednesday, February 6, 2002, for a weeklong visit in which she and the Swedish Embassy will be preparing for an event to be held in Amman commemorating the late Astrid Lindgren.  The Zureikat Weekly will be holding an exclusive interview with Mona within the next few days.

Family and Friends Attend Wael Zureikat's Wedding

 

 

Family and friends attended the wedding of Wael Zureikat and Jezel Zumot on Friday, February 8, 2002.  The event started at about 3pm when members of the Zureikat family visited the bride's family to "ask for her hand".  Then, a wedding procession drove through the streets of Amman and finally arrived at the church in which the wedding ceremony was held.

Amer Returns from Providence to Dublin after Interview with Brown

Reema: Amer immediately resumed his work in Dublin.  He is on-call all the time.

Amer returned to Dublin on Thursday, February 7, 2002, after an interview with Brown University Hospital in Providence, Rhode Island, USA.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Reema said, "Amer immediately resumed his work in Dublin.  He is on-call all the time, and he hasn't had any free time since he returned.  His days are nights, and his nights are days!"

Dana's 19th Story Airs on JTV

Dana's 19th story aired on JTV on Friday, February 8, 2002.  This week's story was about the olive oil industry in Jordan and the problems that it is facing.
