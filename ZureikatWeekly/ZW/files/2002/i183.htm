Breaking News:

Hassan Spends Few Hours at ICU after Sugar Level Drops

On Saturday, November 23, 2002, in the afternoon, Zureikats were going to attend a church service for Issa Oweiss when Hassan felt dizzy.  He was immediately taken to the Jordan Hospital spending a few hours at the ICU there.  Hassan returned home Saturday night.  Doctors said that Hassan's blood sugar level had dropped which caused the dizziness.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Hassan a very speedy recovery and all the best.

Amal, Abla Z., Hala, and a nurse at the hospital on Saturday, November 23, 2002.

Amal Hospitalized after Fracturing 3 Transverse Processes

Amal fell down as she was going down the stairs to the basement

Amal was hospitalized on Friday, November 22, 2002, after fracturing the transverse processes of the vertebrates L3, L4, and L5.  This happened as Amal was going down the stairs to the basement on Friday morning.  Doctors said that Amal is okay, and that she only needs to rest.  Sources told The Zureikat Weekly that Amal will be staying in the hospital "for few days".  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Amal a very speedy recovery and all the best.

Dana Shares her Experience in Washington DC

"I'm undergoing the most intensive program I've ever been in"

"Who would have thought that I'd be mastering the Arabic language in Washington DC!"

In a phone call with the Editor in Chief last week, Dana gave the following statement to The Zureikat Weekly:

Dear family,

I'd like to tell everyone back home how much I miss them, and how much I think about every single person in the family and think, in this big city, how, at the end of the day, you need your family and that family is the most important thing in one's life.

Reading the ZW every week has been a delightful experience as tears roll down my face as I laugh really hard.  I'd like to thank Mona, Alia, Reem, and Bana for their words of encouragement I received through the ZW.

I would also like to tell the family what a great experience this is.  I'm undergoing the most intensive program I've ever been in in my life, but I also feel it's going to be the most rewarding experience of my life.  I keep asking myself, "Who would have thought that I'd be mastering the Arabic language in Washington DC?"  It's overwhelming.  I'm undergoing training for two months on broadcasting, editing audio, reading and writing journalistically in Arabic, the art of reading for radio, which is quite different than television.  The training program I'm undergoing requires me to wake up at three o'clock in the morning.  I shower, get ready (I think of Amal and miss her every time I brush my teeth!), and I leave the house at 4:30.  It takes me half an hour to get to the office.  I get there at five, early enough to read the newspaper, check my mail, and get myself some coffee.  My training starts at six, and I get done at 4:00, 4:30, sometimes 5:00.

I'm already making a lot of good friends (nothing like family though!).

Hugs and kisses to everyone...

Dana

Family Gathers at Harran's for Lunch

 

Zureikats gathered at Harran's house on Friday, November 22, 2002, in a lunch hosted by Harran and Reema in Tarek's honor.  Lunch included kabseh, wara' dawali, chicken pie, and arayes, followed by atayef for dessert.  Amal and Ziad were unable to attend because Amal was in the hospital at that time.  After lunch, several members of the family visited Amal.

Bashar Buys New Opel Astra 2000

Finally...after three years of driving a so called "car" (the Lada), Bashar buys a new car!  The car is a beautiful silver Opel Astra, in excellent condition.  Of course, Bashar has long disappeared since the arrival of the new car, as he has been driving it 24/7, showing it off to all his friends.  The ZW will provide a picture of the new car once Bashar returns home.  As for the Lada, Omar has inherited the mess of metal.  We all wish him good luck with that car and all the problems that come with it.

Deena Holds Pajama Party on her Birthday

Zureikats celebrated Deena's birthday on Thursday, November 21, 2002.  Celebrating this occasion, Deena held a pajama party for 25 of her classmates on Thursday.  The party, which went on till 12am, included games which Deena prepared all by herself.  Zureikat youngsters were also among those who attended.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Deena a very happy birthday and all the best.

Hala Applying for Humphrey Scholarship...Prepares for TOEFL

Hala sat for the preparatory TOEFL test (ITP) on Thursday

Hala had a one-week break from work...She will be resuming work on Sunday

Hala is currently preparing for the computer-based TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language), which she will sit for on Wednesday, November 27.  Hala sat for the ITP TOEFL (which is the preparatory TOEFL test) on Thursday, November 21, 2002.  This comes as Hala prepares to apply for the Hubert Humphrey Fellow Scholarship.  What should also be mentioned is that Hala has been on a one-week break from work (doing housework!); she is expected to resume work on Sunday, November 24, 2002.

The Shomali Olives Have Finally Been Crushed!

 

After weeks of hard work, the Shomali olives have finally been crushed.  Basil spent three days (as forecasted by the ZW) going back and forth between the Shomali residence and the oil press waiting for his turn to crush the olives.  Finally, the day came on Monday...The result: A whopping four tanks of pure olive oil fresh from the Shomali garden.  All Zureikats are welcome to indulge in zeit ou za'tar at the Shomalis'!!
