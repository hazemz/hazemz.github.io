The Electronic Age and Umm Al-Amad

A letter from Marwan Zureikat

Dear Webmaster,

The question as to what are the limitations, if any, on the potentials of this electronic age was never as striking in my mind as it was this morning, Sunday April 30. 2006. Before I get into the reason why this has been the issue for me today, I want to remind you that Abla and I are miles away from being considered computer illiterates. We both do spend good times every day working behind the computer screen. I may also remind you that we have a facility to have access to the internet concurrently, thanks to a device that Hazem had installed in our (SYSTEM) last year.

This morning, Abla was preparing to go to our farm house in Umm Al-Amad, when she declared that she was going to take her laptop with her. When I laughingly pointed out that this would not serve any purpose because we do not have an internet connection there, she quickly dismissed the issue, saying that someone in Umm Al-Amad must have a wireless connection and that she would sneak through and use it. She reminded me that Hazem used to use the Reuters network. Reuters, as you know, are our next door neighbors in Amman.

Before I surrender to this, I wanted to check with you. I have managed to convince Abla to hold on this adventure until we hear from you. For those who do not know, Umm Al-Amad is a near 10,000 sq. km area, occupied mainly by Bani Sakher, who live in tent-like houses at an average of one house per sq. km.

Marwan

Hazem Attends Lunch with Kofi Annan, Receives Econ Award


Hazem posing with Elaine Pilon, founder of the Elaine Gartner Pilon Award

Hazem attended a luncheon at Macalester College held in honor of its visiting 1961 alumnus, UN Secretary General Kofi Annan. The lunch was preceded by a convocation and a talk by Annan, during which Hazem carried the Jordanian flag.

In other news, Hazem received the Elaine Gartner Pilon Award from the Economics Department at Macalester on Monday, April 17. The award, established by Macalester's 1945 alumnus Elaine Pilon, is given to the senior economics major with the highest overall and economics grade point average. It includes a cash prize and a year's subscription to Fortune magazine.

Hazem Accepts Offer from MIT, Rejects Berkeley

Hazem has decided that he will be attending the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) for his graduate studies this coming September.

After applying to the departments of Civil Engineering at several school last fall, Hazem received offers of admissions from MIT, the University of California, Berkeley, Cornell University, the University of Minnesota, and the University of Maryland. His final decision came after visiting both UC Berkeley and MIT.

Hazem will be enrolling at MIT's Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering to work towards a Master of Science degree in Transportation and, possibly, a doctoral degree in the same field.
