Hazem to Attend UC Berkeley Next Year

Hazem received a full scholarship to the MS/PhD program in Transportation Engineering

Hazem has been accepted at the University of California, Berkeley to complete his Master's and Doctoral degrees in Transportation Engineering at the university's Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering starting this coming fall semester.

Hazem, who is graduating from Macalester College in May, has received an offer of admissions that includes a full scholarship, as well as an additional annual salary.

UC Berkeley is located in Berkeley, California, near the city of San Francisco. Both the university's undergraduate and graduate programs in civil engineering are rated #1 in the country by the weekly newsmagazine "US News & World Report".

The MS/PhD program at UC Berkeley typically takes anywhere between three and a half and five years to complete.

Marwan and Abla Buy New House

The house is located down the street from their current residence

Move expected early May

 

Marwan and Abla have bought a house just down the street from their current residence in Jabal Amman. The deal was sealed on Monday, January 23, and the move is expected by early May, according to Abla. The new house is a one-floor, three-bedroom villa which was previously owned by the late Jordanian statesman Ahmad Tarawneh.

Marwan, Abla, and Hazem (as well as Dana and Tarek at a certain point) have been living in their current rented house since 1990, after moving there from their apartment in Shmeisani. The Zureikat Web would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Marwan, Abla, and the family on the new house, wishing them all the best.

Posted Friday, January 6, 2006 (News item #0001)

Zureikats Celebrate Christmas

The family gathered at Harran's on Christmas Eve and at Marwan's on Christmas Day

First Christmas for Jad

 

 

Click here for more Christmas pictures

Zureikats in Jordan gathered at Harran and Rima's on Christmas Eve 2005 and at Marwan and Abla's on Christmas Day. This year's festivities included the usual Christmas carols, as well as a speech by Marwan and another by Reem.

In his speech, Marwan explained how there are only few moments that a person can vividly remember when looking back at his life and that Christmas is one of those moments. He talked about the importance of such family gatherings and the importance of appreciating these gatherings and preserving this tradition. Marwan also welcomed Dana, who was set to arrive at Harran's a few minutes after the speech on her first Christmas Eve celebration with the family in four years.

What should be noted is that this is the first Christmas for Jad, who was born on January 14, 2005. Jad, Dana and Ammar, who currently live in Dubai, celebrated Christmas and the new year in Amman.
