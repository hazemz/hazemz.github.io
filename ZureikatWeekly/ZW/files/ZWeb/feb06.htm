Posted Sunday, February 26, 2006 (News item #0037)

Reem Accepted as 2nd Year Resident at Cornell University

Reem has been accepted without match as a second year resident at Cornell University. She will officially enroll in July 2006 as a resident in internal medicine.

Located in Ithaca, in upstate New York and considered a member of the 'Ivy League' of colleges and universities, Cornell prides itself with one of the top medical programs in the US.

In a phone call with the Managing Editor and Webmaster, Amal said that Reem has been informed of the admission decision by the director of the program to which she applied. Amal also said that Reem will be spending most of her time at the university's hospital in New York City.

On another note, Ayman has sent an e-mail to the ZW congratulating Reem on her acceptance. "I  would like to take this opportunity and congratulate my sis on this miraculous result," he said. "I am a very proud brother right now! Way to go Reem and best of luck with all your other endeavors."

The Zureikat Web, in turn, would also like to congratulate Reem on this great achievement, wishing her all the best in the years to come.

Abla, Hassan and Families Have Lunch at the Dead Sea

     

Abla, Basel, Yacoub S., Hassan, Dian, Sabine, Nadine and Yacoub Z. all had lunch at a fish restaurant near the Dead Sea on Tuesday, January 31.

In an e-mail he sent to the ZW, Yacoub S. said, "During the day, we enjoyed a fabulous lunch, in which we had the most amazing fish in the world. And as an activity, we (the kids) played a round of football in a deserted mine field near the border with Israel. It was most dangerous and fun also.
