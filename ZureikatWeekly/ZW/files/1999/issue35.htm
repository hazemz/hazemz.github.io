Zureikats Rejoice

on Christmas

Mona sends her season's greetings

Click HERE to go to the Christmas Pictures Page

Zureikats all around the world celebrated Christmas last week.  The family gathered at Harran's house on Christmas Eve.  Santa Claus attended the family gathering, as well as a few guests, including Dian's sister, Jumana, Mr. Talal Al-Hassan, his wife, his daughter, Dr. Shabeeb Ammari, and Basma Ammari, daughter of the late Nabil Ammari.  Family members, young and old, sang Christmas carols celebrating this joyous occasion.  During the Christmas Eve gathering, Amal read a letter, in front of all the family, which was sent on August 31, 1939 by Farah Al-Jiryes, Umm Harran's mother, to Abu Harran before he knew Umm Harran.

The family also gathered on Saturday, December 25, at Marwan's house for lunch celebrating Christmas Day.  Some Zureikats, including Harran, Amal, and their families, made a brief visit to Marwan's after celebrating Christmas with Reema's family and Ziad's family.  Among the guests who attended Christmas Day's lunch were Mr. Mahmoud Lababidi, his wife, and his two daughters.

Celebrating Christmas, Mona also sent an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly.  The following is Mona's e-mail:

We would like to send our Christmas greetings and the wonderful wishes to all the Zureikat family.  At the same time, we send our congratulation to the Editor in Chief for his marvelous job in keeping this weekly alive.  Hurra, Hurra, Hurra.

Helene Starts a 1 Week Visit to Amman

Helene and Kristiansson will be celebrating the millennium in Amman

Helene and her friend Louise Kristiansson arrived in Amman on Tuesday, December 28, 1999 at 2:20 AM.  The two guests were received at the airport by Amer, Tarek, and Reem.  Helene and Louise visited Mount Nebo, the Dead Sea, Marwan's farm in the Jordan Valley, and Umm Qais.  They are also expected to visit Petra on December 31, 1999.  Helene and Louise will be also celebrating the new year with Amer on Friday night.  Both are expected to leave Amman on Tuesday, January 4, 2000.

Zureikats Prepare for the Millennium

Source: No family gathering on this occasion, as usual

Although the third millennium and the 21st century technically start on January 1, 2001, the whole world, including the Zureikat family, are celebrating them this New Year's Eve.  A source told The Zureikat Weekly that there won't be any family gathering on this occasion, as usual; each Zureikat will be celebrating the New Year in his own way.

New Changes in The Zureikat Weekly

on the New Millennium

The Zureikat Weekly may be released every Sunday starting February

As the new millennium approaches, The Zureikat Weekly is planning some new changes.  There will be a new logo for the magazine and a whole new look for its website.  Another major change may be taking effect in February.  The Editor in Chief is expected to send an e-mail to the Zureikats, after students return to their universities abroad asking them if they prefer The Zureikat Weekly to be released on Sundays instead of Fridays.  If they agree, the 40th issue of The Zureikat Weekly would be released on Sunday, February 6, 2000.  This decision comes after noticing that most of the magazine's readers visited the site of The Zureikat Weekly on Mondays after their weekends, and after the Jordanian government decided to switch the weekend in Jordan from Thursday and Friday to Friday and Saturday.

The Zureikat Weekly welcomes your suggestions on this matter at marwanz@go.com.jo

Hala Appears on Al-Ra'i Daily Newspaper

Hala spoke about JTV's Ramadan and Millennium programs

Hala appeared on the December 24, 1999's issue of Al-Ra'i Daily Newspaper in Jordan.  Hala spoke about Jordan Television's Ramadan and Millennium programs.  Hala also said that JTV will be cooperating with BBC to cover the millennium celebrations from all over the world.

Ayman and Kareem Finish Their Exams

Hazem and Dalia start January 12...Omar starts his Tawjihi finals

Ayman and Kareem, who study at the Amman Baccalaureate School, finished their final exams for the first term before Christmas and are currently in the second week of their three week winter holiday.  Omar and Samer, who are in their last year of school, started their exams.  Omar is currently doing the Tawjihi finals.  Hazem and Dalia are expected to start their finals on January 12, 2000, and finish on January 20, 2000.
