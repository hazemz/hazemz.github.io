Helene Remembers Her Grandmother

"She always told me it was her sense of humour which kept her going"

Almost one week after her grandmother's death, Helene sent an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly.  In her e-mail, Helene said, "I would like to take this opportunity of telling you a little about my grandmother who newly faded into history.  Her name was Wally Henning, but we all called her Oma, which means "granny" in German.  She has had a long and at times hard life (she has survived two world wars).  She always told me it was her sense of humor which kept her going and I really believe her!  I have this poem I have written some time ago.  I would like to dedicate it to this proud and kind lady, who I had the honor of calling "Oma".  The poem is called "Do you remember":

Do you remember the days before that day?
I remember that I was crying,
because you were leaving me you were going away,
I felt that you were dying.

Do you remember the very moment before,
before you left for the other side?
You looked at me with eyes that I could not ignore,
You told me some words, then you died.

Suddenly I did not feel the pain anymore,
I was liberated, I felt free,
It was as if the pain had been opened by a door,
to which your words were the key.

Today, you are watching me from above,
your eyes are filled with fragile tears,
I am filled with childish love,
Your words are ringing in my ears:
Do you remember? Do you remember?"
