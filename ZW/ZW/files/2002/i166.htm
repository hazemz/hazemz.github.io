166th Issue - July 21, 2002

Amer Accepted at Cleveland Clinic, the #1 in Cardiology

Amer has been accepted at the Cleveland Clinic to do his internship and specialization in internal medicine and cardiology as part of a three-year contract.  The clinic, located in Cleveland, Ohio, is the number one in cardiology and internal medicine.  More details about when Amer will start working at the Cleveland Clinic in the coming issues.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Amer on his acceptance and wish him all the best in the future.

Dana Appointed as Reporter at the Voice of America

Dana will be practicing her new job alongside her two other jobs

Dana has been appointed as a reporter for the Voice of America.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Dana on her new job and wish her all the best.  What should be mentioned is that Dana will be practicing her new job alongside her two other jobs at Jordan Television and the Jordan Movie Channel (ArabMedia).  As a reporter at the Voice of America, Dana will be preparing two reports every week to be broadcast at the recently-launched "Radio Sawa".  (Radio Sawa will soon be added to our "JordanLIVE" section.)

Dian and the Children Start a Month-long Visit to Syria

Dian, Sabine, Nadine, and Yacoub Z. left to Syria on Monday, July 15, 2002.  Sources told The Zureikat Weekly that Dian and the children will be spending about one month in Syria.

Amal and Ziad Visit France

 

Amal and Ziad left to France on Tuesday, July 16.  They are expected to return home on Sunday, July 21, 2002.

Abla Delivers Speech at Jubilee School Graduation

The Jubilee School's class of 2002, along with Abla, HRH Crown Prince Hamzah, and some members of the school's Executive Committee

As the Director of the Jubilee School, Abla N. delivered a speech in the school's 6th graduation ceremony which was held on Wednesday, July 17, 2002, under the patronage of HRH Crown Prince Hamzah bin al-Hussein, the Regent.  The Jubilee School, which is part of the King Hussein Foundation, was established as part of Jordan's celebrations marking the silver jubilee of His Majesty the late King Hussein's accession to the throne.  Abla is about to start her third year as the Director of the Jubilee School.

Dana's First Story in Arabic Airs on JTV

Two other stories for Dana (in English) also aired last week

Dana's 43rd story, her first in Arabic, aired on the six o'clock news on JTV on Thursday, July 18.  This story was about Souq Ukaz, a cultural activity held in Jordan as part of the "Amman - the Arab Cultural Capital for 2002" celebrations.  Her 42nd and 44th stories (which were in English) also aired last week.  The first, which aired on Monday, July 15, was also about Souq Ukaz.  The second, which aired on Friday, July 19, 2002, was about the EJADA Loan Guarantee Program.  EJADA is the Euro-Jordanian Action for the Development of Enterprise.

Zureikats Celebrate 2 Birthdays

 

Zureikats celebrated Bashar's birthday on Thursday, July 18, and Dian's birthday on Saturday, July 20, 2002.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish both Bashar and Dian very happy birthdays and all the best.

  • Abu Harran, Harran, and Reema had lunch at Ra'uf Abu Jaber's house on Saturday, July 20, 2002.  The invitation came after Mr. Abu Jaber and his wife met Abu Harran at Harran's 60th birthday party and decided to host a Mansaf in his honor.  Jiries Zureikat also attended the lunch.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Reema said, "They were talking about old days, and Jiddo had a lot of information to contribute.  He was very happy; it was a very nice reunion."

   

  • Hala is currently preparing for the "Arab Song Concert" which will be held in Amman starting August 15, 2002.  Hala is the coordinator of the concert, which is organized by Jordan Television and the Arab TV Stations Union, based in Tunisia.  Fourteen Arab countries are expected to participate in the concert.

  • Abla N.'s mobile phone was stolen last week right after the graduation ceremony of the Jubilee School.

  • Reem sent an e-mail to the Editor in Chief a few days after his graduation.  In her e-mail, Reem said, "Congratulations!!  You deserve all the best,  I just wish I was there to celebrate it with you.  It was nice seeing you at the engagement...I really liked your and your father's speeches, especially that part about Jiddo,  It was very touching."

  • Abla Z.'s new Indonesian helper, Astuti, arrived at the Shomalis residence last week.

 

  • Dalia, Deena, and Salem had lunch at Abla Z.'s on Saturday, July 20, 2002.  After the lunch, Salem said that the hamburgers he had at Abla's were "better than the hamburgers at McDonald's"!
