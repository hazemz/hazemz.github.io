165th Issue - July 14, 2002

Hazem Graduates from Amman National School

Marwan delivered the parents' speech in this year's ceremony

Hazem graduated from Amman National School on Thursday, July 11, 2002.  The graduation ceremony, which was held at the school, was attended by many Zureikats.  During the ceremony, Marwan delivered the parents' speech.  Hazem is the fourth Zureikat to graduate from Amman National School, after Bashar, Tarek, and Omar.  This year, Hazem was the only one among the 38 graduates to graduate with distinction.  What should also be mentioned is that Abla Z. was this year's host at the graduation.  Abla last hosted ANS's graduation ceremony in 1996, the year Tarek graduated.  After the graduation ceremony, Marwan, Abla N., Hazem, and Abla Z. attended the graduation dinner which was held at the Bristol Hotel in Amman.  (Please note that digital pictures were not taken at the ceremony.  We will probably be scanning the normal pictures as soon as they are available.)

Leanne Sends her First E-mail to The Zureikat Weekly

Leanne sent her first e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly after officially becoming a member last week.  In her e-mail, Leanne said, "Dear Zureikats...First and foremost, I would like to thank the Editor in Chief, the talented Hazem Zureikat for giving me such a warm welcome into The Zureikat Weekly and congratulate him on the beautiful website he has created.  The 4th of July was a wonderful day, and I would like to thank each and every one of you for making it the most memorable night of my life, and for joining in celebrating Amer's 'dependence' day!!  You have truly made me feel part of the family (especially Ammo Marwan, with the Mansaf and Ma'loubeh...), and I am thrilled to be the new addition to The Zureikat Weekly and the Zureikat family.  You have touched my heart with your love and generosity..."

Mona and Sylvia sent two e-mails to the Editor in Chief last week.  In her e-mail, Mona said, "First of all, we would like to congratulate the Editor in Chief on this marvelous job, and we congratulate him on his student exam.  Second, we welcome Leanne to the Zureikat.  The whole Henning family join me in wishing all the best for this very beautiful couple.  We really missed this nice occasion.  Thanks to The Zureikat Weekly which helped us imagining how great the time you had all of you.  Again, our very warm congratulations to Leanne and Amer and to their parents and the whole family."  Regarding her family's latest news, Mona said in her e-mail, "The Henning family is very active this summer.  Sylvia is working during this month in Bonn University clinic.  Helene, who came back from London, is enjoying her vacation at some friends summer house.  Dag is happy and is changing job leaving behind him the information technology for the time being.  I myself am very busy printing lots of books both old and new.  Next week, Gero and I will be touring in north Sweden where the midnight sun is.  We will be in the polar circle watching the everlasting sun at midnight.  Nick is very happy and still going strong."  Sylvia, in her e-mail, also congratulated Amer and Leanne on their engagement and Harran on his 60th birthday.  "I would like to send my very best wishes to Amer's engagement and many congratulations to Harran's birthday," she said.  Sylvia also thanked the Editor in Chief for the pictures in the previous issue.  "I want to tell you my dear Editor in Chief that you are doing a great job.  I loved the photos from Amer's engagement with Leanne and the photos from Harran's birthday.  With your internet presentation, it's possible for me to participate the family meetings even if I am not with you in Jordan.  Thank you."  Regarding her latest activities, Sylvia said, "At the moment I pass an internship at the University Hospital in Bonn, here in 'good old Germany'.  I enjoy ship tours on the Rhine, many castles from the middle age, fine wine...Many kisses to the whole Zureikat, the Shomali, the Karadsheh, and the Sharaiha family."
