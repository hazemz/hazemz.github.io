Dana moved in to her new apartment at 4411 Connecticut Avenue in Washington DC on Thursday, October 31, and started her new job at the Voice of America on Friday, November 1.  After helping her settle down in her new apartment, Abla N. is expected to leave Washington on Monday, November 4.  Abla and Dana had earlier left Amman to Washington DC on Monday, October 28.  Hazem, on the other hand, who is currently studying at Macalester College in St. Paul, Minnesota, returned to St. Paul on Monday, November 4, after visiting Washington.  Hazem had left Minnesota to DC on Friday, November 1, 2002.  Dana's apartment building, the Park Connecticut, is considered a very safe building in the DC area.  It includes many different services, such as a 24-hour front desk, a business center, and a gym.

 

Zureikats Congratulate Dana on her New Job

The family gathered at Marwan's before Dana's departure

   

Zureikats gathered at Marwan's house the night before Dana's departure to congratulate her on her new job and wish her all the best in Washington.  On the other hand, the Editor in Chief and Dana received a number of e-mails from Zureikats around the world congratulating Dana on her new job.  In her e-mail to Dana, Mona said, "Happy to hear your news through the ZW.  I am very proud of you."  Alia and Reem also sent e-mails to the Editor in Chief congratulating Dana on her new job.  In her e-mail, Alia said, "What she has achieved so far is truly amazing.  We're all very proud of her!"  Reem, in her e-mail, said, "I just wanted to congratulate Dana on her job and say that she 'raf3at ras el-ayleh'."

Reema Hosts Annual Charity Breakfast for FFSPD

The event was patronized by HRH Princess Mona and attended by about 210 people

Reema hosted the annual charity breakfast for the Family and Friends Society of Persons with Disabilities (FFSPD).  The event was attended by around 210 people, most of whom were representatives of organizations Reema works with, as well as friends of hers.  From the family, Amal, Dian, and some Ayyoubs attended.  The event was held under the patronage of HRH Princess Mona Al-Hussein on Wednesday, October 30, 2002.  Reema gave a welcoming speech during the event, thanking Princess Mona and everyone for their continuous support and encouragement.

Reem Sends her Regards to the Family

"It was nice seeing Tarek here in London on his way back to Amman"

Reem sent an e-mail to the Editor in Chief last week.  In her e-mail, Reem said, "It was nice seeing Tarek here in London on his way back to Amman.  I'm glad he's decided that he's done the right thing by returning.  Also just to say goodbye to Vijita, who spent ages with both us and the Shomalis, and that she's an integral part of the family and we'll miss her dearly.  I also want to wish Reema alf salameh, and I hope she's feeling better and back to her old self again.  Lastly, I just want to give my regards to Jiddo and say that one of the forgotten hayaya el-mogadeh says hello to him especially from London."

Controversial Issue of Appointing Abla as Photographer Continues to Evolve as Marwan Replies to Ziad and Leanne's E-mails

Marwan sent the following e-mail to the Editor in Chief in response to what Ziad and Leanne lately wrote regarding the issue of appointing Abla N. as a ZW Photographer:

The Editor in Chief,
The Zureikat Weekly,
Minnesota,
USA

Dear Sir,

I have read the recent comments made by Ziad and Leanne on my letter to the Editor concerning the appointment of Abla N. as a Weekly Photographer.  I do not doubt their good intentions, but one must not exclude their “ulterior motives”.  Abla, as you know, is Ziad's patient.  He also made no secret of the fact that he enjoys her cooking.  Leanne's position, on the other hand, is completely understood being a new member of The Zureikat Weekly.

I believe that the recent technological advancement in telecommunication will contribute to ending the argument over this issue.  I am referring, of course, to the combined cellular-camera system referred to as VP (Videophone).  I will shortly arrange for Abla to have one of those as soon as we sort out some minor outstanding issues.  One of such issues was indirectly alluded to in Ziad's letter when he used the word "passion" to describe Abla N.'s pictures.  Both Ziad and Leanne, as well as many others, do not know that Abla exhibit the same passion when using her mobile phone.  This is most seen when Abla N. discovers, when trying to use her phone, that there are “missed call(s)”.  For reasons that are not apparent to us, Abla N.'s reaction would be to shout out loudly "EH EH MISSSSSSED CAOOOAL."  The surprise, agony, or "passion" (to use Ziad's words) continue while she is searching for her eyeglasses to proceed then into a process of finding the right button to solve the mystery (using trial and error techniques, of course).  The whole ordeal ends by the last sigh of relief together with the name of the missed caller.  I referred to this as one of the issues to be resolved as I do not know how the new VP show the missed calls and whether or not it shows pictures rather than words, etc, and how will Abla N.'s reaction be like.

I am grateful to The Zureikat Weekly for giving me the chance to share my ideas on this issue with the family members.

Please accept my kind regards,
Marwan Zureikat
