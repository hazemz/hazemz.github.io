177th Issue - October 13, 2002

Dana Offered Job in Washington, Leaves Soon

Dana has been offered a job at the Voice of America (VOA) in Washington DC.  She is expected to leave Amman soon.  More details in our coming issues.

Reema and Alia Leave to the UK

Alia: I'll be doing intensive physiotherapy in Hemel Hempstead

 

Reema and Alia left to the UK on Friday, October 11.  In an e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Alia said, "I'll be leaving to London tomorrow (Friday, October 11) and returning on Saturday, October 19, 2002, (night time).  I'll be doing intensive physiotherapy (2.5 hours everyday!!) in Hemel Hempstead, in the north of London."

Tarek Joins JCE as the Family Continues to Celebrate his Return

 

Nine days after his return home, Tarek joined the Jordanian Consulting Engineer Company (JCE).  Marwan, Chairman of the Board of JCE, and Tarek both gave speeches in a special gathering for JCE staff which was held on Saturday, October 12, to welcome Tarek to the company.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Tarek on joining the JCE family and wish him all the best in the future.  On the other hand, the family continued to celebrate Tarek's return home throughout the past week.  Abu Harran and some of Tarek's friends had Mansaf for lunch at Marwan's on Monday, October 7, 2002.

Amal Returns Home

After spending more than two weeks in the US where she visited New Orleans and Houston, Amal returned home on Wednesday, October 9, 2002.

Hassan Returns from Beirut

Hassan returned from Beirut last week.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Hala said, "He has just come from Beirut and Dian is always nagging because he is traveling a lot."

Zureikats Celebrate Dag's Birthday

Zureikats celebrated Dag's birthday on Tuesday, October 8, 2002.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Dag a very happy birthday and all the best.

A few days after Marwan sent his e-mail protesting against appointing Abla N. as photographer

Ziad Says Abla's Pictures Contain "Passion and Emotions"

"I cannot but disagree with [Marwan's] objection about our new photographer"

A few days after Marwan sent his e-mail to the Editor in Chief protesting against appointing Abla N. as the new ZW photographer, Ziad sent an e-mail to the Editor in Chief.  In his e-mail, Ziad, who called himself "Zoo Zoo", said, "I was astonished by last week's letter to the Editor from Mr. Marwan.  I cannot but disagree with his objection about our new photographer.  It is for the first time we don't see only pictures but we see some passion and emotions involved in photography, so we cannot but congratulate our new photographer and her new technique."

  • Marwan and Abla's friends from Egypt, Mr. Sameh and Mrs. Amira Sabbour, returned to Cairo after visiting Jordan for almost one week.  During their visit, Mr. and Mrs. Sabbour visited Umm Al-Amad, Madaba, and Mount Nebo.

 

  • Hala updates us on the latest in her family: "Our kids will finish today (Sunday, October 13) the first round of exams at ANS.  Dalia was tired but did well, also for Deena and Salem.  As for our ARAMEX soccer team, we are not winning this year.  Salem is playing always (defense)."
