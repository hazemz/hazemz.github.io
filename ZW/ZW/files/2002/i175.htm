175th Issue - September 29, 2002

 

 

Mona, Dag, and Helene in Amman

Family lunches and dinners were held at Harran's, Abla's, and at Marwan's farm in Umm Al-Amad

The Hennings visit Anjara and the Dead Sea

 

 

Mona, Dag, and Helene are currently on a visit to Jordan.  Mona and Dag arrived in Amman on Thursday, September 26, while Helene arrived on Friday, September 27, 2002.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Alia, our Chief Correspondent in Amman, updated us with the Hennings' activities in Jordan in the last weekend.  On Thursday, September 26, Dag picked up Alia from work then had lunch at Harran's.  After lunch, Abu Harran, Harran, Mona, and Dag all went to Anjara.  Later that night, the family had dinner at Harran's.  The dinner included Mashawi, Koosa and Waraq, and Burak bi Jibneh.  After dinner, Mona, Bana, and Omar went to the airport to get Helene.  On Friday, September 27, there was, what Alia called, a "big, big lunch" at Abla Z.'s which included Kharoof Mahshi and Koosa and Waraq.  The whole family, including Leanne, attended both events.  What should also be noted is that Abla Z. woke up at 3 AM on Friday to prepare for the family lunch.  On Friday night, Dag, Helene, Alia, and Bana had dinner at the Vinaigrette Restaurant and later went to the Al-Nai Night Club to show Dag and Helene, as Alia put it, "a model of a night club in Amman".  As for Saturday, September 28, Bashar and Bana took Dag and Helene to the Dead Sea after which the whole family had dinner at Marwan's farm in Umm Al-Amad.  Dag is expected to leave Amman on Monday, September 30; Helene is expected to leave on Tuesday, October 1, while Mona is expected to leave on Saturday, October 5, 2002.

Editor in Chief Appoints Abla N. as ZW Photographer

The Editor in Chief appointed Abla N. as a new Zureikat Weekly Photographer.  The Zureikat Weekly now has three photographers, Abla N., Bana, and Kareem.

Alia Injures her Back at Work

Alia fell down at work and injured her backside on Tuesday, September 24, 2002.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Alia, our Chief Correspondent in Amman, a very speedy recovery and all the best.

Omar Starts Third Year at Sumayya

Omar started his third year at the Princess Sumayya University for Technology last week.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Omar all the best in his new academic year.

Tropical storm Isidore strikes in Louisiana...Tulane evacuated

Samer Enjoys a 6-Day Weekend

Amal leaves to Houston Tuesday

 

Residence halls at Samer's university in New Orleans, Louisiana, Tulane University, were evacuated on Tuesday, September 24, due to a tropical storm, called 'Isidore', that was moving towards that area on that day.  Classes at Tulane will resume on Monday, September 30, 2002.  Samer spent his 'six-day weekend' in a hotel with his mother, who was also in New Orleans at the time.  On the other hand, the conference Amal had attended in New Orleans ended on Saturday, September 28.  Amal is expected to spend some time with Samer and then leave to Houston on Tuesday, October 1.  She will be returning to Amman about a week later.

Hassan Leaves to Lebanon

Hassan left to Lebanon on Sunday, September 29, 2002.  More details on Hassan's trip in the next issue.

Ziad Returns from Rhodes

Ziad returned from Rhodes last week after attending a gastroenterology conference there.  Ziad had left Amman to Rhodes on Thursday, September 19, 2002.

The ZW Gets the First Glimpse of Tipsy

Reema: It seems Tipsy is going to be the future family dog!

This picture was taken in the family dinner held at Harran's on Thursday, September 26, 2002.

We have finally received the first picture of Tipsy, the new dog at the Harran household.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Reema said, referring to the Thursday, September 26, 2002, dinner at Harran's, "The family children played beautifully with Tipsy.  It seems Tipsy is going to be the future family dog!"

In her phone call with the Editor in Chief, Reema also said, "Neither The Zureikat Weekly nor the Zureikat family is the same without Hazem."  (Reema asked the Editor in Chief to include this quote as it is in this issue.)

  • Reem finished the first part of her final exams for this year on Friday, September 27, 2002.  She will have more exams in May after which she will graduate from St. George's.

  • Kareem was the main presenter at the fashion show held in his school, the Amman Baccalaureate School, on Friday, September 27, 2002, as part of the school's annual fair.

  • Reema attended the First Conference on Rehabilitation which was held in Amman last week in cooperation with the World Health Organization and the Ministry of Health.  The conference lasted for two days.

  • Hazem attended a talk on the Middle east which was held at Macalester College on Thursday, September 26, 2002.  The talk, entitled "Finding Common Ground: Steps Towards a Mideast Peace", featured Sentor George Mitchell, Sari Nusseibeh, the President of Al-Quds University and Commissioner for Jerusalem at the PLO, and Ami Ayalon, former commander of the Israeli Navy and the former director of Israeli Internal Security.

  • During her visit to Jordan, Helene got a haircut!  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Alia said, "Helene got a haircut after lunch at Harran's.  She looks beautiful."
