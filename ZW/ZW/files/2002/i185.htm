185th Issue - December 8, 2002

Amer Returns to Dublin after Two Interviews in the US

Amer: On the whole, the trip was very successful

Amer will be returning to Dublin on Sunday, December 8, after making a weeklong visit to the US during which he had two interviews, one in Cleveland, Ohio, and the other in Baltimore, Maryland.  Amer, who arrived to New York City on Saturday, November 30, 2002, said, in a phone call with the Editor in Chief, "On the whole, the trip was very successful, however, the winter cold is discouraging me about coming to the US."  What should be mentioned is that, during his stay in New York City, Amer stayed with his cousin Hussein Ayyoub, who he hasn't seen in a long time "although he only saw me twice in the whole week", as Amer put it.  He continued, "This shows the difference between people from the north and people from the south!"

Tarek returned home on Sunday, December 8, after spending the Eid El-Fitr holiday in Beirut, Lebanon.  Tarek had earlier left to Beirut on Wednesday, December 4, 2002.

Marwan and Abla Host the Shomalis

 

Abu Harran and the Shomalis were invited for lunch at Marwan's on Friday, December 6, 2002.  The lunch was "exclusively for the Shomalis" quote Marwan.  The lunch was mainly fish, including fried fish, fillet, and sayyadieh, not to mention the usual tabbouleh, mazzeh and 3araq.  The lunch came upon the request of Abla Z., as she hasn't eaten fish in a while, and, as expected, the fish served was exquisite!!  The Shomalis would like to take this opportunity to compliment Abla for her excellent choice of fresh fish, and thank Marwan for being such a gracious host.

Harran and Reema Host 2 Picnics in Anjara

 

Harran and Reema took Leanne, George, and Sue (Leanne's parents) to Anjara on Thursday, December 5, where they had a KFC picnic, followed by Nabil's famous tea with na3na3 -- Nabil is Harran's first cousin (ibin khalto) who lives in  Anjara and looks after the land.  This 'picnic' was repeated on Saturday, December 7, 2002, with Abla Z., Omar, and Alia on board!  They also had KFC and enjoyed the ride to and from Anjara on the pickup, dubbed by Abla Z. as  "Harran's Tri-Star", since both take-off and landing went pretty smoothly, in spite of  the occasional turbulences along the way!

Reema Praises Abla N.'s Work Calling Marwan's Comments "Out of Context"

The Editor in Chief received another e-mail regarding the issue of appointing Abla N. as a ZW Photographer, this time from Reema.  In her e-mail, Reema said, "With regards to Abla N.'s photographic techniques, I must say that the last pictures I have seen are of excellent quality.  In fact, I think they seem so real.  Marwan, I believe your comments are out of context!  Abla, keep up the good work!"
