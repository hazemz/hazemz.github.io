148th Issue - March 17, 2002

Alia Appointed at the National Council for Family Affairs

Alia's post will be determined later

Alia, who graduated from Royal Holloway last July, started her first job on Monday, March 18, 2002.  Alia was appointed at the National Council for Family Affairs.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Alia said that there will first be a month-long training period after which her post will be determined.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Alia on her new job and wish her all the best.

Tarek Returns Home on Brief Visit

Tarek, who is currently working in the UK, returned home on Friday, March 5, on a brief visit.  He is expected to return back to Edinburgh on Wednesday, March 20, 2002.

Kareem Grabs 1st and 2nd Place in Talent Show

Kareem is currently preparing for "West Side Story" performance

Kareem won the first and second prizes at the Amman Baccalaureate School's Talent Show held on Thursday, March 14, 2002.  The first prize was given to Kareem and his friend on their "Stomp" performance in which they used wooden sticks and drums to create music.  The second prize was given to a band in which Kareem also played the drums.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Kareem on this accomplishment and wish him all the best.  What should be mentioned is that Kareem is currently preparing for his performance in the musical "West Side Story". The performance will take place at the Amman Baccalaureate School theater next week.

Editor in Chief Returns from Beirut after School Visit

Hazem returned from Beirut on Sunday, March 17, after making a four-day visit to Lebanon with his school.  Hazem, his classmates, and a number of teachers from the school had left Amman on Thursday, March 14, 2002.

Two More Stories for Dana Air on JTV

Dana's 23rd story aired on JTV on Friday, March 15.  Her 24th story also aired on Saturday, March 16, 2002.  The first story was about a demonstration held in Amman in support of the Palestinian intifada; the second was about an event held under the patronage of HRH Princess Basma bint Talal on the occasion of the International Women's Day.
