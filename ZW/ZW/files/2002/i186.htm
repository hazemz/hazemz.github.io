Several family members attended the performance

Bana participated in the YWCA Christmas Choir: "The Spirit of Christmas".  Several family members attended the choir's performance, including Abla Z., Basil, Yacoub S., Abla N., Tarek, Hala, Dalia, Deena, and Leanne.  The YWCA Christmas Choir performed at the Royal Cultural Center in Amman on three nights: Thursday, Friday, and Saturday, December 12, 13, and 14, 2002.

Editor in Chief receives medical report from Hala:

Hassan has the Flu, Dian has an Infection, Yacoub to Undergo Blood Tests, Karadsheh Children Suffer from Hommos Poisoning

In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Hala updated us on the latest health situation for several family members.  In her e-mail, Hala said, "In a telephone call this morning, Dian explained to me that she has an
infection in the nerves in her hand and shoulder.  She was very busy having carpenters at home doing maintenance, so she is very tired.  Hassan is sick.  He has grippe but fine.  Dian will do blood tests for Youbi to check whether he has to remove his tonsils or not."  As for the health situation in her family, Hala said, "We had a nice Christmas party with the Zarzar family, our friends, Thursday afternoon.  It was cold, and the party was by the pool.  Nice food, nice carols, but it was freezing.  Going home that night, all my kids were sick, vomiting and high fever 39.5.  It started with Salem at 10:00 pm on Thursday; Friday, early morning, Deena, and then, in the afternoon, Dalia.  Right away, Naief started investigations...Could it be the Zarzar?  But finally, he came to the conclusion that it may be the sandwiches he did for the kids on Thursday morning.  It was Hommos.  Only they and our maid ate from them, and she also is sick, so, the problem is Naief, hahahaha.  He was feeling very guilty, but still we don't know the reason exactly."

Samer: I cannot but agree with Khalo Marwan

"In the past few weeks, the pictures were vague; I couldn't tell Harran from Hassan and Abla from Hala"

A few weeks after Reema praised Abla N.'s work saying that the pictures she takes "seem so real", Samer, in a phone call with the Editor in Chief, said, "In reply to Reema's e-mail, the fact that the pictures seem real is due to the camera and not the photographer.  Although it's the photographer who makes the pictures, it doesn't seem to be so in this case.  I cannot but agree with Khalo Marwan.  In the past few weeks, the pictures were vague; I couldn't tell Harran from Hassan and Abla from Hala."

The Story of Amer, Tarek, and the Grendizer Videos

   

Tarek sent the following e-mail to the Editor in Chief last week:

Amer and I have made a joint venture of purchasing all the Grendizer series.  In fact, we are the only ones in Jordan who have the full 19 original video set.  It was our agreement that the videos, although owned by both of us, must be safely kept in Amer's room in the event of both of us leaving the country.  Now that I am back and Amer appears to be moving to the US, this issue is not so simple, in fact, could cause serious problems since neither of us are prepared to let go of this phenomenal series, and splitting them up is not an option.  I have not discussed this with Amer yet, however, this subject has caused me a considerable amount of stress lately.  I have trouble sleeping at night, especially after I found out that Amer has changed his phone ring to the Grendizer tune.  I wonder if he was signaling something to me with that.  Today, however, my case received a boost from Leanne, who, when I brought up the subject, said, and I quote, "You keep them for God's sake.  Khallisni minhom (the videos)".  I have made this story public not to cause problems, but, hopefully, to avoid them.  When Amer comes back, hopefully, the Zureikats will persuade him that the videos should be left with me when he leaves to the US."
