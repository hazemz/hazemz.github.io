Tarek Returns Home...for Good

Tarek was supposed to return on Monday but made a surprising return on Thursday

"I would like to thank the Zureikats for the warm welcome I received"

Tarek, who was supposed to return home on Monday, October 7, made a surprising return on Thursday, October 3, 2002.  After studying and working in the UK and the US for the past six years, Tarek will now be working in Jordan.  In a chat session with the Editor in Chief, Tarek said, "I would like to thank the Zureikats for the warm welcome I received and promise the Karadshehs and Hassan that I will be visiting them soon.  It's good to be back home after a rocky year."  When asked about his future plans, Tarek said, "For the next four months, I will be undergoing an orientation period where I will be learning about my father's business of which I know nothing about, and then I will see where I can add value.  In the long run, I can see myself being involved with JCE, and, maybe, if the opportunity allows me, running my own business, in a sales-oriented industry."

Hennings Return to Sweden

 

Mona, Dag, and Helene returned to Sweden last week after visiting the family in Amman.  Dag left Amman on Monday, September 30, Helene left on Tuesday, October 1, and Mona left on Saturday, October 5, 2002.

Amal in Houston, Returns Wednesday

"I received most of my dental supplies, and I'll be leaving Houston on Tuesday"

After attending a conference and spending some time with Samer in New Orleans, Louisiana, Amal left to Houston, Texas on Tuesday, October 1.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Amal said that during her stay in Houston, she bought a new laptop for Talal and shipped it to him.  In the phone call, Amal also said that she has renewed her driver's license.  "I renewed my driver's license," she said, "so now I will have a definite ID when I move around the US, since I'm traveling a lot these days!"  Amal will be leaving Houston on Tuesday, October 8.  She is expected to arrive in Amman on Wednesday, October 9, 2002.  "I received most of my dental supplies," she said, "and I'll be leaving Houston on Tuesday."

Harran Chairs Meeting in Petra

Harran and Reema spent one night in Petra last week

The family had 'Msakhan' for lunch at Harran and Reema's upon their return

 

Harran and Reema returned from Petra on Friday, October 4, 2002, after spending one night there.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Alia said, "Dad and Mom spent one night in Petra (the Movenpick) because Dad had a 'Khulwa', meaning a retreat.  Harran was the chair person.  They left on Thursday afternoon and returned on Friday lunchtime.  Jiddo, Mona, Hala, Dian and the kids, Abla Z., Bana, and Yacoub all had Msakhan for lunch on that day at our house.  They came right after Mom and Dad returned!!  Obviously, my beloved parents were feeling exceptionally fit and energetic on that day!!!"  What should be mentioned is that before having lunch at Harran's, Abu Harran and Mona enjoyed some appetizers at Abla Z.'s.

Marwan Slams Editor's Decision to Appoint Abla as Photographer

Marwan sent the following e-mail to the Editor in Chief on Tuesday, October 1, 2002:

The Editor in Chief
The Zureikat Weekly
Minnesota, USA

Dear Sir,

I noted with interest your decision to appoint Abla N. as a photographer for the Weekly.  I know that you were impressed with the contribution that she had made to the Weekly's pictures library, particularly in the last two issues, but ignored to consider the number of pictures that were wasted in the process.  Nearly 90% of the pictures that she has taken were either (not there) or were so bad that one would wish they were (not there).  Furthermore, your decision will jeopardize our continued effort to train her on how to use the mobile phone that she had been carrying for over two years since she will now be devoting more time to (PHOTOGRAPHY).

I trust you will give serious consideration to this issue.

Kind Regards
Marwan Zureikat
