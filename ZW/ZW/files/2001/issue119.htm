119th Issue

Sunday, August 26, 2001

Hala, Dalia, Deena, and Salem Return from Germany

After joining Naief in Germany for one month, Hala, Dalia, Deena, and Salem returned home on Saturday, August 25, 2001.  Naief is currently doing researches at Gottingen University in Germany as part of a three-month Dad Scholarship he earned from the German Government last month.

Amal, Reem, Samer, and Ayman leave to the US

Ayman Starts 1st Year at Tufts

Amal, Reem, Samer, and Ayman left to the US last week.  In just a few days, Ayman will be starting his first year at Tufts University in Boston.  Samer, on the other hand, will start his second year at Tulane in New Orleans.  Talal, who will be studying at St. George's in London, will be leaving Amman by the end of next month.

As part of an official Jordanian delegation

Harran Visits Iraq

As part of an official Jordanian delegation, Harran left to Iraq on Friday, August 24, and is expected to return home on Monday, August 27, 2001.

Marwan Returns from Dubai

Marwan returned from Dubai on Thursday, August 23, after a two-day business trip.  Marwan had left to the United Arab Emirates on Tuesday, August 21, 2001.

Kareem's IGCSE Results...1 A and Straight B's

In an e-mail she sent to The Zureikat Weekly, Alia said, "We are happy and proud to announce Kareem's IGCSE results...1 A and straight B's.  The A - and this is for you Auntie Mona! - is in Arabic!  Believe it or not, Kareem was the only student who got an A in Arabic!"  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Kareem on his results and wish him all the best in the years to come.

Zureikats Celebrate Ayman and Talal's Birthday

  

Zureikats celebrated Ayman and Talal's birthday on Thursday, August 23, 2001.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish both Ayman and Talal a happy birthday and all the best.

Better late than never...

We mentioned in the 88th issue which was released on Sunday, January 7, 2001, that Zureikats celebrated Abu Harran's 90th birthday.  The number isn't 90, it's 88.  Abu Harran was born in 1913 not 1911.  We apologize for this mistake.
