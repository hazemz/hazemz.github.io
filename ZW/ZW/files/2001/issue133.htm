Editor in Chief: Hazem Zureikat

133rd Issue

Sunday, December 2, 2001

Khaleel Zureikat Passes Away

Khaleel Subuh Zureikat died in Rabba, Karak on Monday, November 26.  His funeral was held there the next day.  Khaleel Zureikat is the father of Khaled and Nahla Zureikat and the brother of Yacoub (Abu Harran), Jameel (Abu Hisham), Mariam (Umm Jaleel), and Aziza (Umm Ihsan) Zureikat.

Condolences

The Editor in Chief of The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to offer his condolences to Abu Harran and the Zureikat family on the passing away of Khaleel Subuh Zureikat.

Marwan, Abla, Dana, and Hazem leave to Edinburgh on Tuesday

Tarek Graduates Next Wednesday

After completing all the requirements for his BA degree in Business & Accounting last June, Tarek will be graduating from the University of Edinburgh on Wednesday, December 5.  Marwan, Abla, Dana, and Hazem are expected to leave to Edinburgh, Scotland on Tuesday, December 4, 2001, to attend Tarek's graduation.

Tarek in Beirut...Leaves to Edinburgh Sunday

Tarek, who spent about ten days in Amman, left to Beirut on Friday, November 30, on a two-day vacation.  He is expected to leave to Edinburgh from there on Sunday, December 2, 2001.

Amal Leaves to Houston

Amal left to Houston, Texas on Monday, November 26, to attend a conference which was postponed earlier due to the September 11 attacks on New York and Washington.  In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Amal said that she may be visiting Samer for one day during her trip, and that she will be returning home on Monday or Tuesday, December 3 or 4, 2001.

Hala and Family Move Back

"Wa Akheeran Dhawa"

"Salem is doing fine in school...[Dalia and Deena] will have a show next Saturday in school"

Hala, Naief, Dalia, Deena, and Salem moved back to their house in Umm Al-Summaq after weeks of renovation.  In an e-mail she sent to The Zureikat Weekly before moving back, Hala said, "After a long stay away from home, we are now 98% ready to go back to Umm Al-Summaq.  Everything is almost done.  I have spent all day Friday and all day Saturday working, working, and working at home.  I was so tired that I could not sleep yesterday (Saturday, November 24).  This morning (Sunday), they came to put the curtains.  The kitchen is beautiful and the bathrooms also.  Everybody is invited to take a shower and have a snack after with cheese
and wine.  Don't bring towels; we have plenty.  I think we will sleep Monday night at home.  Wa akheeran dhawa, as Umm Harran used to say...I am sure she is glad now that I have clean home.  We will do a small party for Deena's friends on Thursday for her birthday.  We will be putting Christmas decorations starting next Thursday.  Salem is doing fine in school after his slight fever last week.  Dalia and Deena are doing great in their creative dance classes.  They will have a show next Saturday in school."

Tarek Holds Awards Banquet for Jordanian Students

 

Tarek, who is currently working with the Southwestern Company, held an awards banquet for the Jordanian students who participated in his summer work program last summer.  The banquet was held at the Amman Marriott Hotel on Thursday, November 29.  It was attended by the students and their parents.  What should be mentioned is that this is Tarek's second awards banquet.  The first was held on January 9, 2001 for the students who worked with him in the summer of 2000.

Zureikats Celebrate Marwan's Birthday

Zureikats celebrated Marwan's birthday on Sunday, December 2, 2001.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Marwan a very happy birthday and all the best.

Samer Visits Ayman in Massachusetts

 

Samer, who is studying at Tulane University in New Orleans, visited Ayman, who is studying at Tufts University near Boston, Massachusetts, during the Thanksgiving holiday.  In an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly after Samer's departure, Ayman said, "My brother Samer came over for Thanksgiving holiday on Tuesday (November 20) night, and he just left today (Saturday, November 24) early morning.  We spent a lot of quality time together.  He stayed in my dorm room.  We spent a lot of time going around Boston, so and so, and now he is back in New Orleans."

  • Mona (in an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly congratulating Marwan on classifying his company, JCE, as Class 1A): We would like to send our sincere congratulations to Marwan and his company.  We always did believe that Mawan has a great vision and strong loyalty to what he believes in, and that is serving his country in the most honest and sincere way.  Our warmest wishes to Marwan, his company, and his family who supported him.

  • Comari returned to Abu Harran's house to work there.  She arrived in Amman on Saturday, December 1, 2001.

  • Houston's reopened last week after two weeks of renovation.
