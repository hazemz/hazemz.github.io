Harran "Up and Around" after Undergoing Partial Thyroidectomy

Reema: Everything is ok.  His recovery was very speedy

Harran is up and around and almost ready to go back to work after undergoing a partial thyroidectomy in the Jordan Hospital on Thursday, February 15.  In a statement made to The Zureikat Weekly, Harran said that due to the presence of a lump in the thyroid gland, doctors had to extract the right lope of it (the thyroid gland).  In a similar statement, Reema said, "The biopsy showed negative; everything is ok.  His recovery was very speedy, and, after two hours of his operation, he was eating solid food, and he was attending to his patients as they called him in the room!"  Harran returned home on Friday, February 16, 2001, one day after his operation.  Sources say he may return to work on Sunday.

Everyone is Waiting to See What Happens to Spotty

Spotty has been refusing food for the last 4 days and has become very slim

From The Zureikat Weekly's archive - Spotty (April 30, 2000)

Everyone is waiting to see what happens to Spotty who has been refusing food and vomiting continuously for the last 4 days.  Veterinarians don't know the reason, and Spotty, who has grown slimmer and slimmer through the last few days, is now under medication.  Details will be provided as they emerge.
