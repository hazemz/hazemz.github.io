Editor in Chief: Hazem Zureikat

5th Issue

Friday, May 21, 1999

Tarek Wins Elections

Tarek won the elections for president of the Arab Society in Edinburgh.  Tarek had decided to run for president a few weeks ago.   We will have more details in the 6th issue.

 

The Zureikat Weekly receives two e-mails from Sweden

Mona Sells Her Summer House

Gero to go to Frankfort with Helene and Dag to visit Japan soon

Mona sent two e-mails to The Zureikat Weekly last week.   In her e-mails, Mona said that she will be selling her summer house to Dag's friend.  Mona owned this summer house since 1982.  "It is a pity to sell, but we go very seldom there," she said.  Mona also said that Helene will be working in a Swedish bank in Frankfort this summer.  Gero will be going there with her to help furnish her small room.  Helene will be also studying next winter in Milan, Italy.  On the other hand, Dag will be going to Japan on a business trip for the second time soon.

 

In her first e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly

Reem Sends Her Love to the Zureikat Family

"I had a great time at Tarek's"

After returning to London from a visit to Edinburgh, Reem wrote her first e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly.  Regarding her trip to Scotland, Reem said, "I had a great time at
Tarek's.  He was  the perfect host.  Edinburgh is really beautiful, and the weather was actually ok!!  Tarek took me all around the main sites and gave me the history of them.  I had lots of fun."  Reem also said that she might be going to Singapore, Australia and Thailand this summer with some friends from her university.

 

Harran and Reema are Now in the UK

On May 15, 1999, Alia left for London to sit for her final exams at the Royal Holloway and return to Amman at the end of the month.   Harran and Reema also left for Paris on May 17.  On May 19, Harran and Reema left Paris for London to visit Alia.  Harran is expected to return to Amman on Saturday, May 22, while Reema will stay in London and return to Amman with Alia.

 

Hala, Naief and the Kids Attend a Pool Party

Hala, Naief, Dalia, Deena and Salem, who are currently staying in Birmingham, Alabama, attended a pool party on Saturday, May 15, organized by a doctor in the Fulbright Board.  In an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly, Hala said, "it was a beautiful house with a nice swimming pool.  The children played and played and we had lots of presents and delicious barbecue."

 

After undergoing hysterectomy a few weeks ago

Amal To Continue Normal Work in 2 Weeks

"I will start my light work on the 22nd"

After undergoing hysterectomy a few weeks ago, Amal told The Zureikat Weekly that after weeks of staying at home to recover, she will continue her "light work" (a few patients per day) starting Saturday, May 22, 1999.   In our previous issue, we mentioned that Amal will continue her light work on the 17th, but later Amal told The Zureikat Weekly that she decided to start on the 22nd.   Amal also said that she may resume her normal work after two weeks.

 

Hassan Spends a Day in Syria

Hassan left for Syria on Monday (May 17) morning.  Hassan had some business meetings there and returned to Amman later that day.

 

Bana Gets Her Driving License

On Wednesday, May 19, 1999 Bana passed her theoretical and practical driving tests and therefore got her driving license.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to congratulate Bana on earning her license and wish her all the best.

 

Zureikats Prepare For Their Finals

Ayman and Talal already started their IGCSE's

Ayman and Talal, who study at the Amman Baccalaureate School, started their IGCSE exams May 17.   Samer, who also studies in the same school, will start his exams on the first week of June.  Ayman, Talal and Samer will finish their exams around June 15.  Omar and Hazem, who study at Amman National School, will start their exams on June 3 and finish around June 13, while Kareem will start his exams on June 9 and finish around June 16.

 

Ziad to Return Home Next Monday

Ziad, who is currently on a business trip in Orlando, Florida, will return home on Monday, May 24, 1999.

 

The Amir

~ A new book to be published by Dar Al-Muna ~

BY MONA HENNING - The Amir is a little camel who discovers what freedom is really like.  He leaves his home in the desert and goes to town with the help of a star twinkling to him from far away.  He meets a donkey on the way and they make few adventures together.

 

Amal and Abla Eat Lunch at Abu Harran's

Amal, Abla Z., Basil, Bashar, Bana and Omar ate lunch at Abu Harran's house on Thursday May 20, 1999.

 

Hazem to Attend His Cousin's Graduation in Beirut

Hazem will attend his cousin's graduation in Beirut.   Khaled Bashour (Abla N.'s sister's son) will be graduating from school on July 12.   Hazem may leave for Lebanon at the beginning of July.

 

Yacoub Camps Near Salt

Accompanied by students from the 3rd and 4th grades in Amman National School, Yacoub S. went to camp near Salt, Jordan on Thursday, May 20, 1999.   The students will be returning to Amman on Friday the 21st.

 

Sabine and Nadine Participate in Activities with The Modern School

In cooperation with Arab Radio & Television (ART), The Modern School made a competition which included different games.  Last week, Sabine made some gymnastic moves and was a cheerleader.  Nadine also was a cheerleader in another competition later last week.  Sabine will be also singing a part from Al-Hilm Al-Arabi (solo) in the Modern School next Monday (May 24).

End of 5th Issue
