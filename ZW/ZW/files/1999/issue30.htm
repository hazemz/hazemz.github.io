Accompanied by Mahmoud and Nabila Lababidi

Harran, Reema, Marwan, Abla Return

     

Harran, Reema, Marwan, Abla N., Mr. Mahmoud and Mrs. Nabila Lababidi returned to Amman on Tuesday, November 23, after visiting Cairo.  Marwan, Abla N and the Labaabidi's left Amman on Wednesday, November 17, while Harran and Reema followed them later next week.  Harran attended a conference in Cairo and is expected to attend another one in Beirut.  He, accompanied by Reema, will be leaving for Beirut on Thursday, November 25. 

In a nine-day visit to Italy

Amal and Ziad Visit Talal

Ziad returned to Amman a few days before Amal

Amal and Ziad left Amman for Rome on Friday, November 12, 1999.  Amal stayed their for nine days, while Ziad returned to Amman before her.  During their stay, Amal and Ziad visited Trieste and met with Talal.  Talal also visited Amal and Ziad in Rome.

Alia Returns from Hemel Hempstead

Alia reveals her plans as the new Disabled Representative

Alia, who became the new Disabled Representative at Royal Holloway a few weeks ago, sent an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly.  In her e-mail, Alia said, "Yesterday, (Friday, November 5) was the beginning of Reading Week; a week where we have no lectures in order to be able to catch up on all the work, etc.  I'm going to Hemel Hempstead, an area in the north of London, for five days to do intensive physiotherapy (2 hours everyday), and I'm taking a friend of mine along.  We will be staying at a hotel (Yahoo!!).  Hopefully, I'll do some exercises, work and manage to have a good time as well!"  The Zureikat Weekly has learned that Alia and her friend have returned to London after spending a few days in Hemel Hempstead.  Regarding her new post as the Disabled Representative, Alia said, "As the new Disabled Representative at university, I will soon have a lot to do.  As it is a job I'll be doing in collaboration with another friend of mine, it won't be very demanding, I hope!!  Basically, Tanya and I are responsible for making sure that every student with special needs is granted the extra assistance he/she needs and deserves to make life easier for them, as the campus is huge and not 100% accessible, unfortunately.  We will also be responsible for several functions that are to be carried out at the Student Union of the university.  Such activities include The Disability Awareness Day, where we, along with all the other DR's of other universities in the country, organize activities that are based on the everyday life of disabled people (e.g. strapping students to wheelchairs and making race one another around  the campus!!) so that they understand and appreciate how
difficult it is for us to move about campus."

Omar, Hazem Return Home after Visiting Lebanon

Omar, as a member of sports teams in Amman National School and as a Tawjihi student, and Hazem, as the Vice President of the Student Council at ANS, left for Lebanon on Wednesday, November 10, accompanying ANS sports teams, Tawjihi students and members of the Student Council there.  The students stayed in the city of Junieh and visited some touristic sites in Lebanon.  The sports teams also played games against Sayyidat Al-Jamhour School, The American University in Beirut and a Lebanese club.  Omar and Hazem returned to Amman on Sunday, November 14, 1999.

In an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly

Talal Updates the Family on the Latest

"I really want to thank you for The Zureikat Weekly, because when you are outside the country you really enjoy it a lot."

Talal sent an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly last week.  In his e-mail, Talal said, "I am having lots of fun here.  I recently signed up for skiing for the winter.  I hope I get accepted.  This week has been very busy for me in terms of exams; I have to study a lot, but I hope I'll do well."  Regarding the Editor in Chief's latest objection, Talal said, "I really want to thank you for The Zureikat weekly, because when you are outside the country you really enjoy it a lot.  And yes, you need some support from the family or else it wouldn't be published."

Hassan and Dian Return from London

Hassan and Dian returned to Amman after a business trip for Hassan in London.  They both returned on Saturday, November 6 after spending about five days there.  During their trip, the children stayed at Marwan's house.

Nine Zureikats Connected to ICQ (I Seek you)

ICQ is a program used to send messages, chat and do other things with different people around the world.  When you install this program, you will see your empty CONTACT LIST.  To fill this contact list, you must search for people.  You can search by entering the person's first or last name, ICQ number (UIN), nickname or e-mail.  When you add people to your contact list and you connect to ICQ, you will see who's online and who's not (from your list of course).  Then, you can send messages, chat, send greeting cards, play games and do many other things with those who are online.  You can also send messages to those who are offline.

For more details on ICQ, send an e-mail to: marwanz@go.com.jo

To download ICQ, go to http://www.icq.com/
