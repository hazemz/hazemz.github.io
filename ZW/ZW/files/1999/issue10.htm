In an exclusive interview with The Zureikat Weekly after returning home

Hala: We're Very Happy to See the Family

"We missed all those soirées the family had"

"...but life was easier there and the children were happier"

The Editor in Chief of The Zureikat Weekly made an exclusive interview with Hala almost two weeks after her return from Birmingham, Alabama.   The interview took place at Amal's house on Tuesday, June 22, 1999.  In the interview, Hala said that she has missed the family here in Amman after spending all this time in the States and is very happy to see them.  Hala also said that she missed all the soirees the family had.  "We are happy to see the family," she said, "but life was much better in Birmingham, for us and for the kids.  The apartment was better, we had a nice view and there were trees, pools and playgrounds everywhere.  I managed living with the kids there without any help.  Life was easier and the children were much more happier in their social life (they had many friends even in the neighborhood) and their school life.  Cleaning the apartment was much easier, even shopping."   "We used to sit as a family, together, there without any (nakad)," she continued, "Dalia and Deena miss the teachers and their classmates."  In the interview, Hala told The Zureikat Weekly about what she and her family did every day from morning to night.  She said, "Everyone wakes up at 6:30 AM.  The girls dress by themselves and we have our Turkish Coffee then we all eat Corn Flakes for our breakfast.  At 7:30, I take Dalia and Deena to the bus stop near our complex then I return home and watch Good Morning America on ABC.  If Salem had to go to daycare, Naief and I take him at 9:30 AM.  We later go to UAB (The University of Alabama at Birmingham), I drop Naief, who works there, and I go to a sports center, unless Salem didn't go to daycare.  I walk one hour on the treadmill, excerise for half an hour and swim for another hour or so.  After I shower and dress, I get Salem from daycare at about 1:30 PM.  We return home and I go get Naief, along with Salem, at 3:00 or 6:00 PM (it depends on when he finishes).  Dalia and Deena return home at 4:00 and have some snacks.  Then, I prepare dinner and we eat at about 6:00 PM."  Hala also told us about their program in the weekends.   "On Saturdays," she said, "we used to take it easy.  We wake up a little late.  Our main activity for that day is either shopping or going on a picnic.  We return at about 6:00 PM and the children sleep at 10:00."   "As for Sunday," she continued, "we go to church in the morning.   In the afternoon, we either go to a restaurant or visit some friends."   Hala concluded the interview by congratulating the Editor in Chief on the release of the 10th issue of The Zureikat Weekly.

Zureikats Continue Celebrations for Hala's Return

Many family members had dinner at Marwan's farm in Umm Al-Amad

    

    

Hala, Naief, Dalia, Deena, Salem, Basil, Abla, Omar, Yacoub, Dr. Yousef Nusseir (Abla N.'s brother), his wife and his children all had dinner at Marwan's house in Umm Al-Amad celebrating Hala's return from Birmingham, Alabama.   The dinner included hamburgers, a barbecue and a selection of salads.  Marwan, Abla N. and Hazem slept in Umm Al-Amad that night and returned to Amman the next day.

Amal Leaves for Morocco and London

Amal will be attending a wedding in Casablanca

Amal left for Casablanca on Thursday, June 24, to attend the wedding of the Morocco's ex-prime minister's daughter.  Amal will be later going to visit Reem in London.  Amal and Reem will be attending a memorial for the late King Hussein in London.  Amal told The Zureikat Weekly that she is expected to return to Amman on July 6.  Before leaving Amman, Amal congratulated the Editor in Chief on releasing the 10th issue.

Ziad Gets 2nd Prize in a Large Medical Competition

Ziad, who is currently in Brussels, Belgium, came 2nd in a large medical competition there.  The competition, which was about patient management, was made for over one thousand doctors from around the world.  Ziad is expected to return to Amman on Friday, June 25, 1999.

Alia Gets Excellent Results

Reema told The Zureikat Weekly in a phone call last week that Alia did very well in her exams and got excellent results.  Alia will be starting her 2nd year in the university in a few months.

Helene Starts Working for SEB

In her first e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly from Frankfort, Helene said, "you are doing a fantastic job with your cyber newspaper.   It is great being able to update myself with my family, even though I am far away from you guys!"  Helene also said that she is now in Frankfort working for a merchant back called SEB.  "The bank has arranged an apartment for me in the center of the city," she said.

Bana to Sing at Marriott Hotel Next Wednesday

Bana will be singing a duet with Ala' Zureikat, the son of Sami Zureikat, at the Marriott Hotel in Amman next Wednesday, June 30, 1999.  Bana is also expected to be a part of a choir at the Royal Cultural Center in July.

Amer Returns Home

Amer, who studies in Dublin, Ireland, returned home on Saturday, June 19.  Family members welcomed Amer at Harran's house.  Earlier this month, Amer earned his first diploma in obstetrics and gynecology with honors.   Amer is expected to stay in Amman till September.
