Friday, May 28, 1999

In an exclusive interview with The Zureikat Weekly

Tarek Reveals his Plans for the Society

"I welcome any suggestion from the Zureikats"

Zureikats congratulate Tarek on his new job

Mona: We wish the very best for Tarek in his new endeavor

Reem: He deserves all the best

Last Thursday, May 20, 1999, Tarek won the elections for president of the Arab Society in Edinburgh which was started by a Lebanese girl one year ago.  Tarek's opponent in the elections was Mohammad Al-Qasem, a Jordanian PHD student.  Tarek will start his new job as president next year (October 1999).  In an exclusive interview with The Zureikat Weekly, Tarek said, "the theme for the society was, (no politics, just socializing).  Some of my friends and I found that to be a bit disturbing since there are things going on in the Arab World which  you  can't  pretend they don't exist."  Tarek also said that he had decided to run for president after Professor Edward Sa'id from Yale University came to the Arab Society in Edinburgh and decided not to advertise it because of its theme.  In the interview, Tarek included some of his plans for the society.   "Here are some of the new things I am planning to do," he said, "1. Get some Arabic newspapers to be sold at a discount in the university.  (The ones we have are very expensive for some students). - 2. Invite guest speakers to talk about certain issues facing the Arab world. - 3. Make a web site for the society which includes a discussion section similar to the one in Jordan, where a student would write an article about a particular subject, and then other students would comment on the article.  Then you would have a debate on the web.  We can advertise this if it works, to all the Arab Societies in Britain. - 4. A bulletin board for the Arab Society which will include articles that some Arabic student thought would be of interest to other Arab students. - 5. Continue with social events to promote the Arabic culture  to Arabs and non-Arabs - 6. Have a close coordination with GAPS.  I know  their president and some of their members."  At the end of the interview, Tarek said, "I welcome any suggestion from the Zureikats."  On the other hand, Zureikats all around the world congratulated Tarek on winning the elections.  Family members in Jordan expressed their congratulations to Tarek in phone calls to Marwan's house.  In an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly, Mona said, "we wish the very best for Tarek in his new endeavor."  Hala also told The Zureikat Weekly that she will be writing Tarek an e-mail to congratulate him.  In another e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly, Reem said, "he deserves all the best."

 

Hala sends her news update to The Zureikat Weekly

Dalia Receives the "Good Citizen" Award

Hala sells most of her furniture and prepares for her return

Hala sent an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly last week.  In her e-mail, Hala said that Dalia received the Good Citizen Award from the school she's attending, The Green Valley Elementary School, which will be celebrating the last school day on May 27.  Hala and Naief attended a party held by the school.  Regarding the party, Hala said, "we went this afternoon with Dalia's class to their end of school party at the Bowling Club.  It was very nice.   Salem and Deena have scored many points.  At the end of the party, the teacher gave the students their awards.  Dalia has been chosen by the members of her fourth grade class as Nicest Smile for 1998-1999.  Then, the teacher, in her farewell speech to the class, said that as she taught them many things, she learned from them many things.   She said that from Dalia she learned that it is possible to overcome challenges while keeping a gentle spirit."  In her e-mail, Hala also said, "I am so busy right now.   We sold almost all our furniture."  At the end of the e-mail, Hala said, "with our best regards and love to all the Zureikats and a special one from Salem to Jiddo."
