Editor in Chief: Hazem Zureikat

4th Issue

Friday, May 14, 1999

After Spending Almost One Year in the U.S

Hala to Return June 10

"Tell Jiddo That Salem is Coming Soon"

"Please prepare the red carpet for the Karadshehs!"

After spending almost one year in Birmingham, Alabama in the United States of America, Hala, Naief, Dalia, Deena and Salem will return to Jordan on June 10 on Air France.  This is what Hala told The Zureikat Weekly in an e-mail she sent last week.  In her e-mail, Hala said, "Air France did a good reservation for us, we will leave on June 08 and arrive Amman on June 10 with Air France.   Please prepare the red carpet for the Karadshehs!"  Hala also said, "tell Jiddo that Salem is coming soon.  Give him our best regards."

 

Amal to Resume Her "Light Work" Next Week

After undergoing hysterectomy a few weeks ago, Amal told The Zureikat Weekly that she had some minor complications but will resume her light work (two-three patients per day) next week, probably on Monday, May 17.

 

Abu Harran: Mona Sends Her Greetings to Everyone

In a phone call with The Zureikat Weekly, Abu Harran said that he is fine and he didn't go out a lot this week.  Abu Harran also said that Mona called him on Wednesday, May 12, 1999.  Mona said that she was fine and sent her greetings to the whole family.

 

After Returning From a Business Trip to Beirut With Dian

Hassan: The Only Activity We Did Was Shopping

"I advise every friend not to travel with his wife"

On Friday, May 7, 1999, Hassan returned from a business trip to Beirut, Lebanon accompanied by Dian.  In a phone call to The Zureikat Weekly, Hassan said, "I had meetings with some clients to see if they're satisfied with our work.  We just wanted to stay in touch."  Hassan also said, "when you travel with your wife, the only activity you do is shopping.  You go by 1st class and come back in a bus because you're broke!  I advise every friend not to travel with his wife."

 

Hala Sends Her Latest News to The Zureikat Weekly

Dalia Meets the Governor of Alabama

"We are invited to a pool party on Saturday"

In her weekly e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly, Hala said that Dalia went to Montgomery, the capital of Alabama, with her class and met Alabama's governor there.  "They visited the governor in his office.  He shook hands with them and presented each a badge," she said.  Dalia also had lunch on a boat with her classmates and visited different museums.  In her e-mail, Hala also said that The Alabama Fulbright Organization is doing a farewell pool party for Naief on Saturday.  In another e-mail, Hala said, "as for Salem, he always tells me, "I wanna stay," then he continues in Arabic, "ALALAMERICA," hahaha.  Another thing about him is that he refuses to put on anything new we buy for him.  It seems that he refuses things that are made in America.  He is always wearing his clothes from Amman and I am always fighting with him.  He is so nationalist.  Tell Reema thanks for what she did for our home.  I hope Amal is getting better and both Ablas are fine.  We wish a happy return for Hassan and Dian."

 

Tarek E-mails The Zureikat Weekly

His message included news about Reem's visit to Edinburgh

Reem visited Tarek in Edinburgh on May 8, 1999.   In an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly, Tarek said, "Reem and I had a very good time, I introduced her to all my friends and they all liked her."  Tarek also sent us his news.  He said that he will be leaving to the United States on June 16, 1999 and will come to Jordan on October 2, 1999.  In his e-mail, Tarek also included his best regards to the Zureikat family.

 

Ziad Goes to Florida

Ziad left for Florida on Wednesday (May 12) morning to attend a conference there.  We will include more details on Ziad's trip in our coming issues.

 

Bashour Returns to Beirut

Dr. Maan eats dinner with some Zureikat members at Marwan's House

After coming to Amman to deliver a lecture on the history of political parties in the Arab World, Dr. Maan Bashour (Abla N.'s sister's husband from Lebanon) left for Beirut Thursday (May 13) morning.  On Monday, May 10, 1999, Marwan, Abla N., Abla Z., Reema, Bashar and Hazem attended Bashour's lecture.  Everyone liked the lecture and said it should be circulated everywhere.  On his last night in Amman (Wednesday, May 12), Bashour had dinner at Marwan's house with Marwan, Abla, Harran, Reema, Basil, Abla, Amal, Hazem, Dr. Yousef Nusseir (Abla N.'s brother), his wife, his children and Mr. Mu'nis Al-Razzaz, a writer and a friend of the Zureikats.

 

Ayman and Talal to Start Their Exams on May 17

Ayman and Talal, who study at the Amman Baccalaureate School, will start their IGCSE exams on Monday, May 17, 1999.  Their summer vacation will start right after the exams (around June 16).  Amal told The Zureikat Weekly that Ayman will be going to the "Seats of Peace" conference in Washington and Talal will go to a camp in Ontario, Canada in the summer.

 

Sabine, Nadine and Yacoub Spend the Weekend at Marwan's House

After Hassan and Dian traveled to Beirut on Wednesday, May 5, 1999, Sabine, Nadine and Yacoub came to Marwan's house on Thursday, May 6.   Marwan, Abla N., Hazem and Hassan's children went to Umm Al-Amad Thursday evening and slept there.  They all returned to Amman Friday (the 7th) morning and had lunch with Abu Harran in Marwan's House.  Later Friday, Abla N. and Abla Z., along with some friends, went to Umm Al-Amad and spent the evening there while Hazem stayed with Omar in Basil's house.

 

Kareem's Talent Show Canceled

The Amman Baccalaureate School canceled their annual talent show this year because it was "not organized".  This is what Kareem told The Zureikat Weekly in a phone call on Tuesday, May 11.  Kareem was supposed to play drums with a band on that talent show.  On the other hand, a talent show was held at Amman National School on Thursday, May 13, 1999, but none of the Zureikats performed there.

End of 4th Issue
