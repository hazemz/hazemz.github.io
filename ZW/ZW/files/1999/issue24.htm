Hazem Wins Elections

After a four-day campaign, the elections for Amman National School's Student Council were held on Wednesday, September 29, 1999.  Hazem, who ran for Vice President, won, becoming the third Zureikat to be a member of Amman National School's Student Council.

The election results

Yanal Touk, president of the 1998-1999 Student Council, announced the results at 12:00 PM Wednesday.  The following are the full results: Committee Coordinator: Saed Abu Innab, Secretary: Suad Al-Taher, Treasurer: Zeina Al-Sallakh, Vice President: Hazem Zureikat and Masa Hijazi, President: Taroub Malhas.

Highest number of votes

A source in Amman National School told The Zureikat Weekly that Hazem got 234 votes, which is the highest number of votes for this year's elections.  The source said that the other Vice President, Masa Hijazi, got 167 votes.  The source also added that the new Secretary, Suad Al-Taher, got the second highest number of votes after Hazem.

Zureikats and the Student Council of ANS

Hazem is not the first Zureikat to be a member in Amman National School's Student Council.  Tarek won the elections twice, first for Committee Coordinator then for President.  Bana was also a Vice President at Amman National School's Student Council in her last year there before attending UWC in Wales.

Harran Leaves for Ramallah to Spend Three Days

Harran left Amman on Thursday, September 30, 1999, for Ramallah to spend three days there.  Harran will be delivering some lectures in Ramallah and is expected to return home on Sunday, October 3.

After spending two weeks with Alia in London

Reema Returns Home

After spending two weeks with Alia in London, Reema returned to Amman on Thursday, September 30.

Mona Arrives Saturday...Tarek on Sunday

Sources told The Zureikat Weekly that Mona will be arriving in Amman on Saturday, October 2, 1999.  On the other hand, Tarek will also be returning to Amman on Sunday, October 3, after spending three months in the US.  More details in the next issue...
