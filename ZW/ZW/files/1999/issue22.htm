22nd Issue

Friday, September 17, 1999

In a phone call with The Zureikat Weekly, Amal:

Talal Still has a Chance to Attend UWC

"The Italian Embassy in Amman is trying its best"

On April 15, 1999, Talal was interviewed to attend one of the seven United World Colleges this year to complete his IB diploma. The interview came after a series of exams that Talal had to sit for and he was chosen with 19 other students of his age from a huge group of 500 students to be eligible for the scholarship.  The results of the interview stated that Talal was a first runner-up for the scholarship, i.e. if any of the chosen students refuse to accept the scholarship, he will be granted it instead.  Last week, one of the students who were chosen for the scholarship returned to Amman after "misbehaving", therefore, Talal was supposed to take her place in Italy.  In a phone call with The Zureikat Weekly, Amal said that the president of the UWC said that there are "many boys in the college" and that they wanted girls.  Amal also said that despite what the president said, the Italian Embassy in Amman is trying its best so Talal still has a chance to attend UWC.  What should be mentioned here is that Bana was previously granted the same scholarship three years ago to attend the UWC of the Atlantic in Wales.  She graduated in May 1998.

Dana Back in Egypt to Start Second Year at AUC

After staying in Amman for more than one month, Dana returned to Cairo, Egypt to start her second year at AUC.  During her stay, Dana's friends, Sara Sullivan and Hiba Andeel visited Amman.

Accompanied by Reema

Alia Returns to London

    

Alia returned to London on September 15, 1999 after spending her summer vacation in Amman.  Reema, who accompanied Alia to London, is expected to return to Amman within the next two weeks.

Ziad Returns Friday...Amal Leaves for Texas Monday

Amal will be attending a conference in the States

Ziad, who left for Cairo to attend a conference last week, is expected to return to Amman on Friday, September 17.  On the other hand, Amal is expected to leave for Texas to attend a conference on Monday, September 20.  Amal will be spending two weeks there.

Hazem to Run for Vice at ANS Student Council

Elections to be held September 29

Hazem, who is currently in the 10th grade at Amman National School, is expected to run for Vice President of the Student Council.  A source told The Zureikat Weekly that the elections are expected to be held on Wednesday, September 29.
