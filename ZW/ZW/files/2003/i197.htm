Snow Storm Hits Jordan

The snow storm started on Monday and ended on Thursday

A severe snow storm, possibly the strongest in about ten years, hit Jordan between Monday and Thursday, February 24 and 27, 2003.  The storm resulted in the closure of all schools, universities, and offices for three days, Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday.  Most roads in Amman were also closed from Monday evening and all through Tuesday and Wednesday.  Following are the 'damage reports' from our Correspondents and Photographer:

Alia:

"The Harran household survived the snow pretty well, as the damages were 'materialistic', using Reema's words.  A number of trees died, the pool solar system broke down, and the garage door collapsed! (thankfully, the cars were far apart from one another!), and, Friday evening, we had water flooding from the storeroom."

Abla N.:

In a phone call with the Editor in Chief, Abla said that the melting snow resulted in a leakage in the living room and in other parts of the house.  The main door was also damaged.

Amal:

"We had leaking in the reception area in two places, so we, Ziad and I, started to clean the terraces in the master bed room and the family room.  Two hours of hard work since we don't have a maid or a gardener.

Bana:

"At first, when it started snowing, all the family was excited, as it meant that there would be no school/work for the next few days.  However, by the time the third day of being stuck at home had passed, everyone was in a cranky mood, and everyone was arguing with everyone.  So much for spending quality time with the family!!

As for the damage report, we woke up to the sound of a crash on Tuesday morning.  Apparently,  the snow that had fallen upon our Jasmine tree was so heavy, it caused the tree to break and fall, pulling down with it the iron bars that protect our window (that was the sound of the crash).  Other damages included (as expected) broken trees and plants in the orchard."

Harran and Reema Adventure to Anjara!

After mentioning her damage report in her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Alia said, "We also had a bit of adventure!  Knowing Harran, he would not allow something like snow to get in the way of going to Anjara.  Poor Reema had to obediently follow.  Apparently, once they got to the Ajloun area, 'gharrazoo'!  Reema even went as far as to describe the event as "shoft il mout bi 3einey!"  To conclude, I can definitely say that the one person in the family who was celebrating was Harran.  To use Abla Z.'s words, "Al-Tifel Al-Mundahesh" - the elated child - was definitely having a blast!!

Zureikats Resume Work...Students Return to School on Sunday

We mentioned above that offices were also closed on Thursday, February 27, 2003.  However, a number of Zureikats made it to work on that day.  Others resumed their jobs on Saturday and Sunday, March 1 and 2.  Zureikat students also returned to their schools and universities on Sunday, March 2, 2003.

JCE Awarded for Distinguished Project

The 'Ministry of Public Works' Award will be given in a formal reception soon

The Jordanian Consulting Engineer Company, with Marwan as the chairman of its board, was awarded the 2002 'Ministry of Public Works' Award for a distinguished project.  In an e-mail he sent to the Editor in Chief, Marwan said, "The Award will be given shortly in a formal reception organized by the Ministry and the Association of Engineers.  The project selected was the Lajjoun Water Supply Project.  This is a major water supply project valued at nearly US$40 million.  JCE managed to complete the designs and carry out the supervision of the project which was completed in a record 9 months."  What should be mentioned is that the 'Ministry of Public Works' Award had started in 2001 and is given to one consulting company every year.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Marwan and the JCE family on this distinguished award and wish them all the best.

Hala Returns after Successful Trip

It took Hala four hours to get home from the airport due to the snow storm

Hala returned home on Tuesday, February 25, after making a successful working visit to Geneva, Switzerland.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Dalia said that it took her mother four hours to get home from the airport due to the snow storm that hit Jordan last week.  "Hala was transported home via a hammer (normally used by the army!)," she said.  Hala had earlier left Amman to Geneva on Wednesday, February 19, 2003.

Amer Returns from the US

Amer visited Dana and Ammar last Sunday

Amer returned from the US on Wednesday, February 26.  He had left Amman on Sunday, February 16.  During his ten-day stay, Amer sat for the nineteen-hour final part of the USMLE.  He also visited Dana and Ammar in Washington, DC on Sunday, February 23, 2003, where he met Ammar for the first time.

Hassan in Beirut on a Brief Business Trip

Hassan left to Beirut on Friday, February 28, on a brief business trip.  He is expected to return home on Sunday, March 2, or Monday, March 3, 2003.

Ammar Returns from North Carolina

Ammar and his colleagues were ranked third place at the 'Venture Capital Investment Competition'

Ammar left to North Carolina on Thursday, February 27, and returned to Washington, DC on Sunday, March 2.  During his three-day visit, Ammar, who is currently doing his MBA at Georgetown University, represented his university, along with three of his colleagues, at the VCIC (Venture Capital Investment Competition) which took place at the University of North Carolina in Raleigh-Durham.  Ammar and his colleagues were ranked third place and received the Entrepreneur Choice Award.  What should be mentioned is that Ammar will be starting his one-week residency on-campus on Sunday, March 2, 2003.
