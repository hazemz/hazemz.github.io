A royal decree was issued on Monday, February 17, 2003, appointing Amal, along with twenty others, in the Board of Trustees for the National Center for Human Rights.  The board was appointed to a four-year term.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to congratulate Amal on her new post and wish her all the best.

Following is an editorial which was published in Thursday's edition of the Jordan Times:

Advancing the human rights cause

THE FORMATION of the Board of Trustees of the National Centre for Human Rights (NCHR) was the fruit of many years of painstaking work and preparation. The support of Their Majesties King Abdullah and Queen Rania for the creation of a national human rights body gave a fresh and determined impetus to the over a decade-long call for such an institution and the creation of the post of a human rights ombudsman. That the 21-member board of trustees is headed by former Prime Minister Ahmed Obeidat is encouraging. Obeidat comes with credentials as a strong and determined supporter of the cause of human rights in Jordan recognised both locally and internationally.

Now it is up to the newly formed board to make something meaningful of the NCHR by the articulation and adoption of viable and operational policies to advance the cause of human rights in the country. The first order of business, it would seem, is to make sure that Jordanian practices and policies are in conformity with the country's legislation. Secondly, to embark on an investigative course to determine where national laws are or are not in conformity with international norms and standards that are binding on the country. This course would require giving our human rights treaty obligations the force of law, something that has yet to be done.

The promotion and protection of economic, social and cultural rights must be pursued in parallel with the promotion and protection of civil and political rights. The King has repeatedly alluded to this issue by calling on the government to give equal attention to both sets of rights. It has been proven worldwide that the pursuit of one set of rights cannot be done in isolation of the other. The creation of the post of ombudsman would, likewise, be a most welcome development as such an office would serve as the pivotal point for streamlining the national efforts to promote and protect human rights across the board in the Kingdom.

The fact that the NCHR will enjoy a great degree of independence and has a broad-based mandate should give it ample opportunity to carry out its functions under the most favourable conditions. In the final analysis it is up to the newly formed board of trustees to make a success of NCHR. If the board takes seriously enough its mandate and goes about its work in earnest, there is no reason for it to fail. The country needs a NCHR that is operational and serious. It is up to the centre to prove its worth to the people of Jordan.

'Media Monitors Network' Publishes Article for Tarek

Media Monitors Network (MMN), a non-profit news website based in California, published an article for Tarek last week.  Following is the article (click HERE for source):

The American Public is being lied to everyday

by Tarek Zuriekat

The most recent polls according to Fox news suggest that 69% of the American population are supporting a military attack on Iraq, while 23% oppose it, making the US the only country in the world (along with Israel I suppose) whose population support the war. After watching CNN and other American news channels, I was surprised by the 23% who are opposed to war. I suppose those are the ones that read European newspapers. Americans are told a different truth about what is happening by their politicians and their media.

Yesterday witnessed the biggest peace demonstration in the world. Polls in Britain and Spain (the only supporters of war in the UN assembly) suggest that over 70% of their population is against the war. With a UN backing, the support in Spain for war would rise to only 28%. In Turkey (a likely US base and a traditional ally), the polls show that 90% are against the war. Those are the people the US hopes are most sympathetic to its plans. It is therefore clear that the majority of the world is against war at this time. Nevertheless, yesterday, Fox News broadcasted an interview with Senator Bob McCain talking about the "increased isolation" of France and Germany in their stance against the war. This "isolation" has been reiterated endlessly on CNN. That is what the Americans are being told.

Another issue regularly raised is the prospect of Iraq being the "first democracy" in the region. No explanation is required. It is a given fact. I will not go into the historic argument of how America has, on many occasions, done the complete opposite by placing a military dictator in place of a democratically elected leader as in the case of Chili and Indonesia. I will only point out the following: Lebanon is a full-fledged democracy, with a democratically elected leader, democratically elected PM, and a freedom of speech that is envied by the majority of the Arab world. It is no coincidence that all the "experts" on CNN, and indeed the people interviewing them, failed to spot this. Iran also has a democratically elected leader, President Khatami. The Republics of Iran and Lebanon have something else in common. They are both likely targets for US assault should the strike on Iraq be successful. The media campaign there against both these countries in the US has already begun.

Another point worth mentioning is that none of the "experts" who were discussing the likelihood of an "Iraqi democracy" after the war talked about the prospect of having a Shi’ite leader elected to rule Iraq. This is an outcome the US would never allow because a Shi’ite leader would likely be an ally to Iran. Why should there be one? Maybe because the majority of the Iraqi population are Shi’ites, a fact that has surprisingly eluded the "experts" on CNN.

To be fair though, the worldwide protests received a lot of coverage in the US media. The problem was the incorporation of the word "anti-Americanism" throughout the coverage. It is anti-American for European politicians to be against the war, but its not anti-European for Rumsfeld to publicly insult France and Germany, in such a blunt manner that would have made Reagan blush. It is anti-Americanism for Belgium, Germany, and France to block NATO from arming Turkey against the prospect of an Iraqi retaliation (a much weaker country militarily), but it is not anti-Arabism for the US to veto a UN resolution to send peacekeeping forces to the occupied territory of the West Bank to defend an unarmed occupied population against the fourth most powerful army in the world, the Israeli army. But then again Israel is in "self-defence" and if you question that you’re "anti-Semitic".
