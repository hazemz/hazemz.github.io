Diana Carazon...the Superstar

TV Dinner at Marwan and Abla's on the Final Episode of Superstar

Diana Carazon, a Jordanian, was announced Superstar on Monday, August 18

 

 

Zureikats gathered at Marwan and Abla's on Sunday, August 17, to watch the final episode of the first season of Superstar on Future Television.  Results of the voting, which were announced the next day, showed that Diana Carazon, a Jordanian, had received 52 percent of the 4.8 million votes which were cast from all around the Arab World using the internet and mobile phones, making Carazon the first Superstar of the Arab World.  Following is the front-page article which was published in the Jordan Times on Tuesday, August 19, 2003, a day after the results were announced:

Diana Carazon is a Superstar!
By Natasha Twal

AMMAN — Jordanian singer Diana Carazon was made victorious Monday night, beating out thousands of competitors in a pan-Arab vocal competition to be crowned “Superstar” on Future TV's programme of the same name.

The victory, announced last night after two days of intensive polling, brought forth nationwide jubilation, with many citizens taking to the streets hoisting Jordanian flags and carrying pictures of the Jordanian singer. Gunshots and car horns honking filled the hot summer night in the Jordanian capital.

Carazon's acquisition of 52 percent of 4.8 million audience votes won her a one-year record contract and extensive publicity, leaving rival Syrian singer Rowaida Attieh first runner-up.

Carazon told The Jordan Times in an exclusive interview Sunday that “regardless of the final results I am ecstatic.” The singer has already secured a contract with Warner Arabia's Music Master.

She also said she was overwhelmed by the support from the Arab world — principally Jordan, as the Kingdom has shown remarkable support through newspaper ads and banners, as well as television and radio announcements and programmes. Carazon was also given an Opel automobile, as a gift from a car dealership due to her “most beautiful voice.”

A number of mammoth screens were erected in various places throughout the capital showing the final performances for the show, as well as her final victory.

For computer engineering student Wajeeh Saeed, voting for a fellow Jordanian was about national pride. “It will be great if Jordan can present a superstar to the world since Jordanian stars have been away from Arabic pop singing scene for a long time,” Saeed told The Jordan Times.

“I voted for her 146 times via the Internet,” said Saeed, as he sat in an ice cream shop, which promised free product to those that voted for Carazon.

“She has a very nice voice and she's Jordanian too,” said student Hakam Abdul Karim, 25. Karim said he also backed Carazon out of sympathy because of contestant Melhem Zein's comment to vote for Attieh after he was ejected.

“All the people, they care about this show more than anything else,” he added.

“I think she deserves it,” said 20-year-old student Sara Baradakji, while eating lunch with friends Monday. Baradakji says even though her family comes from Syria, she is Jordanian and supports Carazon. “She's beautiful [and] has nice charisma.”

It was also discovered that there was another Jordanian winner, as the $25,000 prize drawn from all the mobile numbers that voted throughout the programme was revealed to be a Jordanian telephone number.

In a related development, the Islamic Action Front (IAF) called on official authorities Monday to end the “outrageous and exaggerated publicity that has surrounded Superstar.”

In a statement, the IAF warned that the programme “distracted citizens from national issues” and was a corruption of the public taste, harming social values.

The programme “toys with people's feelings, consciousness and awareness,” read the statement, facilitating the process of cultural imperialism led by the US in an attempt to change people's cultural identity.

Airing on Lebanon's Future TV satellite channel, the 21-episode “Superstar” was licensed from Fremantle Media and 19TV ad is the Arabic version of the UK programme “Pop Idol,” which sprang to life in 2001.
