Editor in Chief: Hazem Zureikat

75th Issue (Platinum Issue)

October 8 , 2000

Abu Harran Returns Home Monday

Sources confirmed on Sunday that Abu Harran will be returning home on Monday, October 9, 2000, from the Jordan Hospital after undergoing an operation there last week.  Details in the next issue.

Mona in Amman..Leaves Monday

After making a business trip to Cairo last week, Mona arrived in Amman on Thursday, October 5.  Mona is expected to return to Sweden on Monday, October 9, 2000.

After spending a few days in Amman upon his return from the US

Tarek Returns to Edinburgh on Monday

After spending a few days in Amman upon his return from the US where has worked from three months, Tarek is expected to return to Edinburgh on Monday, October 9, 2000, to start his last year at the University of Edinburgh.

Omar Starts 1st Academic Year at Sumayya

Last week, Omar started his first academic year at the Princess Sumayya College.  This college is considered to be one of the top in Computer Engineering in Jordan.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to wish Omar the best of luck in the future.

Editor in Chief Receives

E-mails from Sylvia, Alia, and Talal

  

Sylvia sent an e-mail to the Editor in Chief last week.  In her e-mail, Sylvia said, "I know I'm a bad writer, but I want you to know that you are doing a wonderful job with these news from the Zureikat family (somehow you keep the bounds of our family together like Gidde always did).  I heard the News about Giddo and his operation...I know I haven't been in Amman for such a long time, but I do think of you all a lot and I want to see my Giddo this year so he and I can talk about the old Arabic poetry, eat Warag Dawaly and drink Araq
together.  Give all my love, and when you see Giddo, tell him I think of him, and he better get well again."

The Editor in Chief also received the following from Alia: "For the past two weeks or so, since I have arrived in the UK, I have been up north in Hemel Hempstead doing intensive physiotherapy.  It was ok, but tough!  After the sessions were over, we, my mother and I, came here to Surrey to unload my things at university and then go to my uncle, Samir's, house.  Dana even came over for a traditional Sunday lunch!!  It was sort of like a family reunion, as Amer, who was on his way back to Dublin, uncle Jack, who lives in Cardiff, Wales, and his son Faris were also there.  Today (Oct 2), I started classes.  Everything seems to be going ok.  My subjects are the same as the last two years but a lot harder!  So this definitely means more work and no play, at least for the next six months or so.  God help me!!  I hope you are all doing fine.  Send my love to all for now."

Talal also sent the following e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly: "Everything here is fine, although I am pressured a lot, but I am enjoying every moment of it.  This weekend, we are going to the Dolomites in the Alps Mountains.  It's gonna be amazing.  I am going there with the climbing group.  It will be one hell of a challenge and a wonderful experience."

Hassan Makes a Brief Visit to Syria

Hassan made a brief visit to Syria on Monday, October 2.  He returned to Amman on Tuesday, October 3, 2000.
