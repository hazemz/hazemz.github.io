Helene Arrives in Milan, Starts February

Helene arrived ten days ago and is currently taking Italian classes

Helene arrived in Milan, Italy, where she will be studying, ten days ago.  In an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly, Helene said, "I live in an apartment only five minutes walk to my university and ten minutes walk to Il Duomo, which is the center of the city.  I am, at the moment, taking an Italian course for three weeks.  My "real" classes will start in mid-February, and most of them will be in Italian, so it is essential that I learn some Italian before I attend them!"  "People here are really friendly," Helene continued, "I found a lot of great new friends, and we do fun things together.  I am considering visiting Talal if he starts mailing me!"  Talal is currently in Trieste, Italy studying in the United World Colleges (UWC).

Tarek Visits the Highlands of Scotland

"I will be playing my first game with the Edinburgh University basketball team...Wednesday"

Tarek, who is currently studying in Edinburgh, Scotland, visited the Highlands last weekend with the Hillwalking Society.  "We climbed three monros (mountains over 3000 feet)," he said in an e-mail to The Zureikat Weekly a few days after returning to Edinburgh from the Highlands, "it was amazing; I had the time of my life there.  At night, we celebrated "Burn's Supper", which is a Scottish occasion, the birthday of a Scottish poet called Robert Burns.  At this occasion, it is a tradition that you drink whiskey and eat haggus, the inside of a lamb I think, which is what we did.  Some people even recited some of his poems while we were eating, which was nice."  In his e-mail, Tarek also said, "I just found out yesterday (Monday, January 24) that I will be playing my first game with the Edinburgh University basketball team.  The game will be played on Wednsday against Strouthclyde.  The top two Scottish teams, Strouthclyde (the Scottish champions) and us, will be playing in a tournament which includes the top English and Welsh universities to determine the British champions.  Wednsday's game is a friendly, but we are all looking forward for it, because Strouthclyde is the only team that has beaten us so far."
