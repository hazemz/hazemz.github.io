Hazem Visits UC Berkeley

 
Left: Buildings at the UC Berkeley campus / Right: Hazem talking to one of the professors in the Transportation Engineering program

Hazem visited the University of California, Berkeley (near San Francisco) between Thursday, March 16, and Sunday, March 19, to attend a graduate student open house at the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering.

During the visit, Hazem met with Ayman, who is currently working in Stockton, about an hour and a half away from San Francisco.

Graduate schools generally hold an 'open house' for admitted students in order for them to get acquainted with the university and the program to which they have been admitted. These events are held before students make their final decisions on April 15 on which university they will be attending. Hazem is choosing between UC Berkeley and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), which he will be visiting on April 6. He has been admitted to the Master of Science in Transportation Engineering program at both institutions.

Posted Thursday, March 9, 2006 (News item #0043)

Jordan Times Interviews Amal on Women's Day

The following appeared in the Jordan Times' 'Weekender' supplement on Thursday, March 9, 2006:

INTERVIEWS | women's day

Mastering a profession to help others

"Every child has the right to a beautiful smile"
By Francesca Sawalha

Amal Yacoub Zureikat Sharayha is radiant. She smiles with her eyes, in addition to her mouth. "Everyone should have a beautiful smile," she says. "Every child has the right to a beautiful smile."

Giving people a beautiful smile is Dr Sharayha's job.

The first Jordanian woman periodontist - actually the first woman east of New York to ever attain a diploma by the American Board of Periodontists - has not limited herself to becoming a successful dentist, with a well-established, state-of-the art, spotless and chic clinic in Shmeisani. As a board member of the Higher Dental Education Council, she's actively involved in efforts to raise professional standards. She also dedicates a great deal of time and energy to helping others.

Her work as chairperson of Operation Smile-Jordan is what makes her most proud. "We carried out our first mission last year, we helped more than 700 Jordanian children, especially in the south. It was a dream come true." With Operation Smile, Sharayha is now trying to work on a study on the incidence of cleft lip and cleft palate in the Kingdom, and establish a specialised centre that would serve the entire region.

But her involvement for the betterment of her community does not end there. She is also a member of the board of trustees of the National Centre for Human Rights and a member of the Jordan Chapter of the International Women's Forum.

But it is still dentistry That has always been Sharayha's passion. "I love dentistry, I love my job."

Nowadays, the Jordan Dental Association counts some 3,600 members, 1,600 of whom are women. But things were different back in 1972, when, as a young graduate, Dr Sharayha started practising in the army.

"It was tough. This is a male-dominated society, and dentistry in general was still considered a man's profession."

But she carried on tenaciously and left for the US to obtain her specialisation from the University of Texas in 1982. By that time, she already had two toddlers, and was expecting twins.

If it is true that, as the old saying goes, behind every great man, there is a great woman, then it is also true that behind a great woman there is often a greatly supportive man.

"I wouldn't have reached where I am today without my husband. In order to complete my specialisation, I had to leave our two children behind with him and take off to Texas, and study hard, while pregnant with twins."

After her specialisation, Dr Sharayha took a vow she'd keep until her children grew up: Never to work in the afternoons. Her eldest daughter is now a doctor, studying for her own specialisation.

"My job is about making people beautiful and making them feel good. If you are not going to read and subscribe to the best journals, if you are not going to update yourself and undergo regular training, if you are not going to keep up with the latest in the field, then you should not even think about going into dentistry."
