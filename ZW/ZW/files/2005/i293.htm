Vijita and Chandra's Families Survive Tsunami

Vijita and Chandra's families both survived the earthquake and tsunami waves that swept through vast regions of Asia last week.  Vijita's house was not affected; Chandra's, however, was flooded.  Our best wishes for both families as they recover from this disaster.

Reem Sends E-mail on her First Christmas Abroad

Reem is currently on vacation in France

Reem sent an e-mail to the Editor in Chief on her first Christmas away from the family in Jordan.  In her e-mail, Reem said, "Just wanted to wish everyone a very happy Christmas and a lovely new year.  It's my first Christmas abroad, and I've missed many of our little traditions that makes Christmas what it is for us: Christmas eve with Reema's chicken pie specialty, Tarek's yeeha game (although I'm definitely better than him) and Abla's ability to make everyone sing.  I've been working nights during Christmas, but some of my friends included me in their Christmas tradition, which was lovely.  I even got to go carol singing in a village in Hampshire which was a beautiful experience.  I have a few days off for new year's, and I have spent those in Megeve in France, skiing...my regards to everybody.  Love, Reem."

Local

  • Two aircraft laden with humanitarian and medical aid and a team consisting of 27 doctors and medical technicians left the Kingdom on Thursday for the Maldives to assist in relief efforts in the tsunami-affected island. The shipment is the first of a series of relief measures taken by Jordan to aide victims affected by last week's earthquake and tsunami.

  • Jordanian officials on Sunday said it was important that all six countries neighboring Iraq attend Thursday's meeting here for their own national interests as well as Iraq's political future.

  • The winter session of the Tawjihi examination started on Sunday.

International

  • An unprecedented global aid operation gathered steam Sunday to save survivors of Asia's tsunamis as signs of disease appeared in the overflowing morgues following the disaster that claimed almost 150,000 lives.

  • The Israeli military has made a new incursion into the northern Gaza Strip, saying the operation was aimed at halting Palestinian rocket attacks. About 50 tanks and other armored vehicles poured into the Beit Hanoun area, near the Israeli border, pulling back again after nightfall. They acted after missiles fired from Gaza struck a nearby Israeli town. The new raid came a day after an operation in southern Gaza during which 11 Palestinians were killed. Meanwhile, Mahmoud Abbas, tipped to become the new Palestinian president, says he will not fight against militant groups. After receiving a hero's welcome by militants in the Gaza Strip, Mr. Abbas said he would use persuasion to bring about a ceasefire with Israel.

  • Iraq's election body rejected a suggestion in Washington it adjust the results of next month's vote to benefit the Sunni minority if low turnout in Sunni areas means Shiites win an exaggerated majority in the new assembly.

  • Kuwait's information minister has offered his resignation, the day before he was due to face questioning by parliament over morality issues. Mohammed Abul-Hassan is accused of failing to keep Western ways out of the conservative Gulf state by not doing enough to enforce censorship.
