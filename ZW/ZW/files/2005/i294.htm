Sharaiha elected chairman of Operation Smile Jordan

AMMAN (JT) — Operation Smile Jordan (OSJ) was officially registered this month by the Ministry of Social Development.

At the OSJ general assembly meeting last week, 10 board members for 2005-2007 were elected.

Dr. Amal Yacoub Zureikat Sharaiha, a periodontist who also serves on the board of trustees of National Centre for Human Rights, was elected as chairman of the board; Amin Kawar, director of Jordan Post, as vice chairman; and Mazen Darwazeh, chairman of Hikma Pharmaceuticals, as treasurer.

Manal Wazani was appointed as OSJ executive director at the meeting, which was also attended by representatives from the ministries of social development and interior.

Based in Norfolk, Virginia, Operation Smile is a not-for-profit volunteer medical services organization that provides reconstructive surgery to children and young adults who suffer from facial deformities internationally and in the United States. Operation Smile has provided treatment to hundreds of children and young adults in the Kingdom since 2000.

Last month a 40-member international team of Operation Smile, including volunteers from the United States, United Kingdom, Ireland, Honduras, Italy and Morocco, traveled to Irbid and worked with local volunteers at Al Yarmouk Hospital.

Volunteer medical professionals including plastic surgeons, anesthesiologists, nurses, pediatricians, dentists and speech pathologist spent two weeks providing free medical evaluations to 248 and surgery to 100 children and young adults with cleft lips, cleft palates, burn scars, tumors and other facial deformities.

Since 1982, Operation Smile volunteers have cared for tens of thousands of patients in Bolivia, Brazil, China, Colombia, Ecuador, Gaza Strip/West Bank, Honduras, India, Jordan, Kenya, Morocco, Nicaragua, Panama, Peru, the Philippines, Romania, Russia, Thailand, Venezuela, Vietnam, and the United States.

Local

  • Less than a month to go before the Iraqi elections, His Majesty King Abdullah on Wednesday said “any Iraqi government should be representative of all Iraqis regardless of religious sects or ethnic backgrounds.” Stressing the importance of establishing a government that builds a better future for Iraq, the King said such a government must be able to preserve the “unity and character of Iraq.” In an interview with the Kuwait-based Al Rai Al Aam, focusing on the upcoming elections in both Iraq and the Palestinian territories and other significant regional developments, King Abdullah spelled out Jordan's position, emphasizing the Kingdom's respect for the sovereignty of other countries, the Hashemite history of pan-Arab stands and unity, and the country's support for its Arab brethren.

  • Jordan Telecom offered calls free of charge on Friday to eight Asian nations which were ravaged by devastating tidal waves more than 10 days ago.

  • King Abdullah on Saturday returned after a brief visit to Doha, where he met Qatari Emir Sheikh Hamad Ben Khalifa Al Thani and attended the wedding of Crown Prince Sheikh Tamim Ben Hamad Al Thani.

International

  • Pragmatist Mahmoud Abbas was elected Palestinian Authority president by a wide margin Sunday, exit polls showed, winning a decisive mandate to renew peace talks with Israel, rein in fighters and try to end more than four years of Mideast bloodshed.

  • Bare-chested warriors danced and turbaned heads bowed in prayer when Sudan's Islamist government and southern rebels sealed a comprehensive peace agreement on Sunday ending Africa's longest-running civil war.

  • Israeli planes and artillery have fired on suspected Hezbollah positions in southern Lebanon after an Israeli soldier was killed by militants. The UN said a French officer on patrol was killed by the shelling near the disputed Shebaa Farms area. A Swedish UN officer and a Lebanese man were reported wounded in the incident. The attack came after an Israeli officer died and three soldiers were hurt when Hezbollah militants hit an army vehicle. One militant also died.

  • Rebels took their battle to the heart of Iraq's political and security apparatus Tuesday, assassinating Baghdad's governor, bombing an elite commando base, and bombing Iyad Allawi's party headquarters in another week of bloodshed that left many dead.

  • Iraq's neighboring states on Thursday pledged not to interfere in the violence-torn country's internal affairs and urged all Iraqis to vote on January 30. Concluding a meeting in Amman, foreign ministers of Jordan, Iraq, Syria, Saudi Arabia, Turkey and Kuwait, as well as officials from Iran “reconfirm respect for the sovereignty, independence, territorial integrity and national unity of Iraq, and the principles of non-interference in any country's internal affairs as stated in United Nations Charter.”
