Ayman in London for Few Days

Ayman joined Reem and Talal to celebrate Reem's birthday on Friday

Ayman, who will be graduating from Tufts University in Boston this May, arrived in London on Wednesday, February 23, 2005, to spend a few days with Reem and Talal.  He is expected to return to Boston on Monday, February 28.

Zureikats Celebrate Reem's Birthday

During his visit, Ayman joined Reem and Talal in celebrating Reem's birthday on Friday, February 25.  The Zureikat Weekly would like to take this opportunity to wish Reem a very happy birthday and all the best.

Hazem Joins Computer Science Honor Society

Hazem was invited last week to become a member of Upsilon Pi Epsilon, a computer science honor society.  Hazem is currently in his third year at Macalester College in St. Paul, Minnesota majoring in Economics with minors in both Mathematics and Computer Science.

International

  • A suicide bombing has killed four and injured about 30 outside a popular seafront night club in the Israeli city of Tel Aviv, police report.  The attack is the first since Israeli and Palestinian leaders agreed earlier this month to end violence in pursuit of peace efforts.  Meanwhile, a new Palestinian Cabinet dominated by technocrats and cleared of ministers from the Arafat era took office Thursday after Prime Minister Ahmed Qureia finally won approval from MPs for his radically overhauled lineup.

  • A half brother of Saddam Hussein who has played a leading role in the insurgency has been captured with help from Syria, which faces US allegations of abetting terrorism, Iraqi government officials said on Sunday.

  • In a surprise move, President Hosni Mubarak on Saturday took the first significant step towards democratic reform in Egypt in decades, ordering a constitutional amendment to allow multi-candidate presidential elections for the first time.

  • Rescuers gave up their search Thursday for survivors and victims of a devastating earthquake in southeastern Iran that turned remote mountain villages to rubble and left about 500 people dead.
