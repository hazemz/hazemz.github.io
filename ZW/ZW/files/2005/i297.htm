Amal and Ziad Return Home after Official Visit to Russia

Amal and Ziad were part of a delegation receiving an award from the Unity of Orthodox Nations International Foundation

 
Left: (Right to left): Ziad, Estonian President Arnold Ruutel, Patriarch Alexy II of Russia, President of the Orthodox community in Russia Vladimir Tikhomirov, Patriarch Maxim of Bulgaria, Mr. Akel Biltaji (advisor of HM King Abdullah II -- representing the king), and former Russian prime minister Yevgeny Primakov. \ Right: Amal walking next to former Russian prime minister Yevgeny Primakov.

Amal and Ziad returned to Amman on Monday, January 24, 2005, after an official visit to Moscow, Russia, where they were part of a Jordanian delegation representing HM King Abdullah II.  The delegation, lead by the king's advisor, Mr. Akel Biltaji, attended a ceremony in which Jordan (as well as Bulgaria, Estonia and Russia) received an award from the Unity of Orthodox Nations International Foundation.  In her weekly e-mail to the Editor in Chief, Amal said, "The ceremony was on Friday; a mass was on Saturday; and tours on Sunday.  [On Sunday] evening, we went to the Bolshoi theater to attend a ballet...We [also] had dinner at the [Jordanian] ambassador's house."  The Unity of Orthodox Nations International Foundation presents award every year to those with "outstanding activity in strengthening the unity of Orthodox nations".  Previous recipients include the Presidents of Greece, Belarus and Bulgaria.

Local

  • HM King Abdullah on Wednesday announced plans to decentralize local government by redrawing provincial administrations that are to be run by publicly elected local councils. Emphasizing that political, social, economic and administrative development is an integrated process, King Abdullah said it is “essential to expand the base of public participation in that process.” In a televised address to the nation, the King said a review of the current administrative divisions would yield “development areas, or regions,” each consisting of various governorates.

  • Iraqi expatriates in Jordan cast their ballots in the Iraqi national election. Ballot boxes in Jordan were open on Friday, Saturday and Sunday.

  • Jordanians celebrated HM King Abdullah II's 30th birthday on Sunday.

  • Palestinian Authority President Mahmoud Abbas visited Amman and met with the king.

  • Foreign Minister Hani Mulki and his Israeli counterpart Silvan Shalom met in Davos, Switzerland on Friday, where they discussed ways to put the peace process back on track and the issue of Jordanian prisoners in Israel.

International

  • The first multi-party election in Iraq for 50 years was held on Sunday and has been declared a success at the end of polling. The electoral commission claimed a high turnout and US President George W Bush congratulated the Iraqi people on a "great and historical achievement". A series of election-day attacks across Iraq killed at least 36 people, mainly in Baghdad, health officials said.

  • Dozens of Iraqis were killed in attacks around Iraq in the past week. Meanwhile, 31 US troops were reported killed in a helicopter crash and five more died in insurgent attacks on Wednesday in the deadliest day for American forces since they invaded Iraq 22 months ago. Also, Iraq claimed Monday it had netted an aide to Abu Mussab Zarqawi who was plotting election day attacks.

  • Israeli Defense Minister Shaul Mofaz has said Israel is ready to transfer control of several West Bank towns to Palestinian forces in the coming days.
    Mr. Mofaz and Palestinian security chief Mohammed Dahlan worked out details of the handover in talks on Saturday, which were described as 'constructive'. The talks were part of preparations towards staging a summit between Israeli Prime Minister Ariel Sharon and newly elected Palestinian President Mahmoud Abbas.

  • A gun battle has erupted in Kuwait Sunday after police raided a building where militants were believed to be hiding. Three suspected militants and one officer were killed in the shoot-out in the Salmiyah neighborhood, state television reported.

  • Renewed fighting in Sudan's Darfur region may have killed up to 105 civilians and displaced more than 9,000 last week, the United Nations said on Wednesday.

  • Syrian President Bashar Assad concluded a historic four-day trip to Russia.
