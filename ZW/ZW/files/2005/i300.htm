Just over one month after his birth, Jad arrived in Amman on Wednesday, February 16, 2005, accompanied by Abla N. and Dana.  Upon Jad's arrival, Zureikats in Jordan gathered at Marwan and Abla's to celebrate.  The house was filled with flowers and balloons.  Hala's children also made a card (shown above), on which several members of the family wrote messages welcoming Jad to Amman.  Meanwhile, Ammar also arrived from Dubai on Wednesday night and joined the family at Marwan and Abla's.  He returned to Dubai on Saturday, February 19.  Dana and Jad are expected to stay in Amman for a month or two before joining Ammar in Dubai.

Samer Returns Home

Samer spent his last semester at Tulane completing a second major, in Economics

After completing his undergraduate studies at Tulane University, Samer returned to Amman on Saturday, February 12, 2005.  Samer had spent his last semester at Tulane completing a major in Economics, after he had graduated last May with another major in Electrical Engineering.

Hazem Becomes Member of Math Honor Society

One week after becoming a member of an Economics honor society, Hazem received an invitation to join Pi Mu Epsilon, a Mathematics honor society.  The membership will officially be conveyed in a ceremony on April 14.  "Pi Mu Epsilon, Inc (PME), is the Honorary National Mathematics Society.  The purpose of PME is the promotion of scholarly activity in mathematics among the students in academic institutions." (Source: PME website)  Hazem is currently in his third year at Macalester College in St. Paul, Minnesota, where he is working towards a Bachelor of Arts, with a major in Economics and minors in both Mathematics and Computer Science.

Local

  • Marouf Bakhit, the new Jordanian ambassador to Israel, arrived in Tel Aviv on Sunday, marking the resumption of top-level diplomatic ties after a four-year hiatus due to the Palestinian uprising.  Bakhit was previously Jordan's ambassador to Turkey.

  • Tayseer Smadi was sworn in Sunday as the new Minister of Planning and International Cooperation, one day after the resignation of former minister Bassem Awadallah.  Before assuming the new post, Smadi was the Secretary General at the ministry.

  • President of the Czech Republic, the King of Bahrain and the Prime Minister of Croatia visited Amman last week.

International

  • A massive bomb tore through former Prime Minister Rafik Hariri's motorcade Monday, assassinating the billionaire businessman who helped rebuild his country after decades of war but resigned last fall amid a sharp dispute with Syria.  At least 12 other people were killed and 100 wounded, including a former economy minister. The blast raised immediate fears that Lebanon — largely peaceful since the 1990 end of its civil war — was headed towards a new and bloody twist in its divisive dispute over the role of Syria, which maintains about 15,000 troops in the country.  A statement issued by the American University Hospital said Hariri was pronounced dead on arrival, his body mutilated.  Some 350 kilograms of TNT explosives were used in the bombing, security officials said on condition of anonymity. They did not say whether the explosives were placed in a vehicle or on the side of the street.  A few days after the assassination, Lebanon's opposition called for a peaceful "independence uprising" and said the government should step down.

  • Prime Minister Ariel Sharon's Cabinet approved his Gaza withdrawal plan on Sunday, the first time an Israeli government has decided to dismantle settlements on land Palestinians want for a state.  In another development, Israel ordered a halt to the army's demolition of the homes of Palestinian suicide bombers Thursday after an internal review found that the controversial policy had little deterrent effect.

  • The Shiite list which won Iraq's elections agreed Tuesday on Vice President Ibrahim Jaafari as its candidate for the premiership, as parties continued to haggle over the makeup of the next executive.
