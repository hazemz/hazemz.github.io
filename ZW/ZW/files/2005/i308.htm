ZW Ends Next Week

The weekly family newsletter has been released every week for the past six years

ZW moved overseas in '02, underwent major changes

Family criticizes Editor's decision to discontinue, calls for reconsidering

New e-mail newsletter expected to start May 1

Next week, Zureikats around the world will be reading the final issue of a weekly family newsletter that has managed to continue for six years, bringing family news and pictures to its readers wherever they were.  The Zureikat Weekly started with a short, picture-less issue on Friday, April 23, 1999, and underwent major changes and overhauls throughout the past years, the most important of which was in September 2002, when the Editor in Chief moved to the US.  The move allowed for increased contributions from other family members, as correspondents and photographers were appointed to join the effort of maintaining the newsletter.

Changing times and priorities, however, have caused the Editor in Chief to announce the discontinuation of this newsletter after the 309th issue is released on April 24, 2005.  The move drew wide criticism from several members of the family, including correspondents.  The move also initiated an effort by other members of the family to start another weekly newsletter, which would be sent by e-mail every week starting May 1.

 

As we prepare for the final issue of The Zureikat Weekly, the Editor in Chief would like to ask all members of the family to send any comments they would like to have published in that issue by clicking HERE or by sending an e-mail as soon as possible.  The Editor in Chief also welcomes any suggestions for the layout and logo of the final issue.

The final issue of The Zureikat Weekly will be released next Sunday, April 24, 2005.

Local

  • Around 40 deputies on Wednesday said they were committed to their stand against the formation and composition of the new government.  During a meeting, they also entrusted Lower House Speaker Abdul Hadi Majali to be a liaison between them and government or any other party.  Deputy Abdel Jalil Maatyeh (Karak) told reporters after the meeting that the lawmakers called for their demands to be met. Maatyeh, however, said the MPs also realise that the government should take sufficient time to do so.  Prime Minister Adnan Badran recently said that when the government was formed, the focus was on the qualifications of the team entrusted with reform.  Thirty-four deputies issued a statement on Sunday criticising the formation of the new Cabinet and Badran for not consulting the Lower House on its formation.  The statement also expressed the signatories' dissatisfaction with the composition, saying it did not “fairly represent all regions,” which contradicts Royal directives to relegate more authority to the regions of the Kingdom. The number of signatories increased to 41 on Wednesday.

  • King Abdullah II and Prime Minister Adnan Badran met with Palestinian President Mahmoud Abbas in Amman.

  • The government on Sunday welcomed Israel's decision to release nine Jordanian prisoners from its jails, but said it was committed to bringing all detainees home.

  • The special hotline proposed last week by education officials to monitor and curb school violence will be installed at the Ministry of Education (MoE) on Monday.

International

  • Hopes rose Sunday that Lebanon's newly named Prime Minister Nagib Miqati could break six weeks of political deadlock by forming a government after he obtained support from opposition and pro-Syrian figures.  Miqati was appointed Prime Minister by President Emile Lahoud last week after former Prime Minister-designate Omar Karami failed to form a government.

  • Outgoing World Bank chief James Wolfensohn on Thursday was named special representative of the Middle East "Quartet" with the job of smoothing Israel's withdrawal from the Gaza Strip and parts of the West Bank.
